Types of Angles

Real-world connection: Look around you — angles are everywhere! The corner of your textbook is a right angle (90°). When you open a book slightly, the pages make an acute angle. When you open a door wide, the hinge makes an obtuse angle. Even clock hands form different angles every minute!
  • Acute angle: Less than 90° (e.g., 30°, 45°, 60°) — a sharp, small angle.
  • Right angle: Exactly 90° — the corner of a square or rectangle.
  • Obtuse angle: Greater than 90° but less than 180° (e.g., 120°, 150°) — a wide angle.
  • Straight angle: Exactly 180° — a straight line.
  • Reflex angle: Greater than 180° but less than 360° (e.g., 210°, 300°) — more than a half-turn.
Exam tip: When asked to classify an angle, always check: Is it less than 90°? Equal to 90°? Between 90° and 180°? Exactly 180°? Or greater than 180°? This five-step check will never fail you!

Pairs of Angles — Complementary & Supplementary

Complementary Angles (sum = 90°)

  • Two angles are complementary if they add up to 90°.
  • Example: 30° and 60° are complementary because 30 + 60 = 90.
  • The complement of an angle x = 90° − x.
  • Each angle is called the complement of the other.

Supplementary Angles (sum = 180°)

  • Two angles are supplementary if they add up to 180°.
  • Example: 110° and 70° are supplementary because 110 + 70 = 180.
  • The supplement of an angle x = 180° − x.
  • Each angle is called the supplement of the other.
Why 90° and 180°?
• 90° is a right angle — the corner of a page. Two angles that together make a right angle are complementary.
• 180° is a straight line. Two angles that together form a straight line are supplementary.

Memory trick: Complementary = Corner (90°). Supplementary = Straight line (180°).
Real-world examples:
• Clock hands at 3 o’clock: The minute and hour hand make a 90° angle. The two parts of the full turn around the clock are 90° and 270° (supplementary? No — but 90° can be split into complementary pairs like 30° + 60°).
• Opening a door: If you open a door 60°, the remaining angle to fully open (90°) is 30°. These are complementary!
• Folding paper: Fold a straight edge — the two angles formed at the fold are supplementary (they add to 180°).
Exam tip: An angle greater than 90° cannot have a complement (since the complement would be negative). But every angle has a supplement — even 0° (its supplement is 180°)!

Linear Pair & Vertically Opposite Angles

Linear Pair

  • When two angles are formed on a straight line, they always add up to 180°. This is called a linear pair.
  • If one angle is x, the other is 180° − x.

Vertically Opposite Angles

  • When two straight lines intersect (cross), they form 4 angles.
  • The angles that are across from each other (opposite) are always equal.
  • Adjacent angles (next to each other) form a linear pair and add to 180°.
WHY are vertically opposite angles equal?
Look at angles a and b: they form a linear pair, so a + b = 180°.
Look at angles b and c: they also form a linear pair, so b + c = 180°.
Since a + b = b + c = 180°, we can subtract b from both: a = c.
The same logic proves b = d. Beautiful!

Example: Two lines intersect. One angle is 65°. Find all four angles.

Step 1: Let angle a = 65°.
Step 2: Vertically opposite: angle c = a = 65°.
Step 3: Linear pair: angle b = 180° − 65° = 115°.
Step 4: Vertically opposite: angle d = b = 115°.
Check: 65 + 115 + 65 + 115 = 360° ✓ (All angles around a point sum to 360°)

Parallel Lines Cut by a Transversal

Real-world connection: Railway tracks are parallel lines. When a road crosses the tracks, it acts as a transversal. The angles formed at each crossing point follow special rules — and engineers use these rules every day!
  • A transversal is a line that intersects two or more lines at different points.
  • When a transversal cuts two parallel lines, it creates 8 angles (4 at each intersection).
  • These 8 angles have three special relationships:

1. Corresponding Angles (equal)

Angles in the same position at each intersection. They form an F-shape (or reversed F).

2. Alternate Interior Angles (equal)

Angles on opposite sides of the transversal, between the parallel lines. They form a Z-shape (or reversed Z).

3. Co-interior Angles (sum = 180°)

Angles on the same side of the transversal, between the parallel lines. They form a U-shape (or C-shape).

Exam tip — spotting the patterns:
• F-shape (corresponding): look for angles in matching positions → they are equal.
• Z-shape (alternate interior): look for angles on opposite sides between the lines → they are equal.
• C/U-shape (co-interior): look for angles on the same side between the lines → they add to 180°.

Example: A transversal cuts two parallel lines. ∠1 = 70°. Find all 8 angles.

Step 1: ∠1 = 70° (given).
Step 2: ∠2 = 180° − 70° = 110° (linear pair with ∠1).
Step 3: ∠3 = 110° (vertically opposite to ∠2); ∠4 = 70° (vertically opposite to ∠1).
Step 4: ∠5 = ∠1 = 70° (corresponding). ∠6 = ∠2 = 110° (corresponding).
Step 5: ∠7 = ∠3 = 110° (corresponding). ∠8 = ∠4 = 70° (corresponding).
All 8 angles: 70°, 110°, 110°, 70°, 70°, 110°, 110°, 70°
Check: ∠3 + ∠5 = 110 + 70 = 180° (co-interior) ✓   ∠3 = ∠6 = 110° (alternate interior) ✓

Exercise 5.1 — Complementary, Supplementary, Linear Pair & Vertically Opposite (10 Questions)

Q1. Find the complement of each angle: (i) 20° (ii) 35° (iii) 90° (iv) 77° (v) 30°

Show Solution
Rule: Complement of an angle x = 90° − x
(i) Complement of 20° = 90° − 20° = 70°
(ii) Complement of 35° = 90° − 35° = 55°
(iii) Complement of 90° = 90° − 90° = 0°
(iv) Complement of 77° = 90° − 77° = 13°
(v) Complement of 30° = 90° − 30° = 60°
Check: In each case, angle + complement = 90° ✓

Q2. Find the supplement of each angle: (i) 105° (ii) 87° (iii) 154°

Show Solution
Rule: Supplement of an angle x = 180° − x
(i) Supplement of 105° = 180° − 105° = 75°
(ii) Supplement of 87° = 180° − 87° = 93°
(iii) Supplement of 154° = 180° − 154° = 26°
Check: 105+75=180 ✓, 87+93=180 ✓, 154+26=180 ✓

Q3. Identify which of the following pairs are complementary and which are supplementary: (i) 65°, 115° (ii) 63°, 27° (iii) 112°, 68° (iv) 130°, 50° (v) 45°, 45° (vi) 80°, 10°

Show Solution
Step 1: Add each pair and check: sum = 90° (complementary) or 180° (supplementary).
(i) 65 + 115 = 180 → Supplementary
(ii) 63 + 27 = 90 → Complementary
(iii) 112 + 68 = 180 → Supplementary
(iv) 130 + 50 = 180 → Supplementary
(v) 45 + 45 = 90 → Complementary
(vi) 80 + 10 = 90 → Complementary

Q4. Find the angle which is equal to its complement.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let the angle be x. Its complement is also x (since they are equal).
Step 2: Complementary angles add to 90°: x + x = 90°
Step 3: Simplify: 2x = 90°
Step 4: Divide by 2: x = 90 ÷ 2 = 45°
Check: 45° + 45° = 90° ✓ Both angles are 45° and they are complementary.

Q5. Find the angle which is equal to its supplement.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let the angle be x. Its supplement is also x.
Step 2: Supplementary angles add to 180°: x + x = 180°
Step 3: Simplify: 2x = 180°
Step 4: Divide by 2: x = 180 ÷ 2 = 90°
Check: 90° + 90° = 180° ✓ A right angle is its own supplement.

Q6. In the given figure, ∠1 and ∠2 are supplementary. If ∠1 = 125°, find ∠2.

Show Solution
Step 1: Supplementary angles add to 180°.
Step 2: ∠1 + ∠2 = 180°
Step 3: 125° + ∠2 = 180°
Step 4: ∠2 = 180° − 125° = 55°
Check: 125 + 55 = 180 ✓

Q7. Two supplementary angles are in the ratio 4:5. Find the angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let the angles be 4x and 5x.
Step 2: Since they are supplementary: 4x + 5x = 180°
Step 3: Simplify: 9x = 180°
Step 4: Divide by 9: x = 20°
Step 5: First angle = 4 × 20 = 80°. Second angle = 5 × 20 = 100°.
Check: 80 + 100 = 180 ✓ and ratio 80:100 = 4:5 ✓

Q8. Two lines intersect and one of the angles formed is 75°. Find the other three angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let ∠a = 75° (given).
Step 2: Vertically opposite angle: ∠c = ∠a = 75°.
Step 3: Linear pair with ∠a: ∠b = 180° − 75° = 105°.
Step 4: Vertically opposite to ∠b: ∠d = ∠b = 105°.
Check: 75 + 105 + 75 + 105 = 360° ✓

Q9. In the figure, ∠1 = 3x and ∠2 = (2x + 10)° are a linear pair. Find x and both angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Linear pair means ∠1 + ∠2 = 180°.
Step 2: Substitute: 3x + (2x + 10) = 180
Step 3: Simplify: 5x + 10 = 180
Step 4: Transpose: 5x = 180 − 10 = 170
Step 5: Divide: x = 170 ÷ 5 = 34
Step 6: ∠1 = 3(34) = 102°. ∠2 = 2(34) + 10 = 78°.
Check: 102 + 78 = 180 ✓

Q10. Two lines intersect. The angles formed are (2y + 10)° and (3y − 20)° which are vertically opposite. Find y and the angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Vertically opposite angles are equal.
Step 2: Set them equal: 2y + 10 = 3y − 20
Step 3: Transpose 2y to RHS and −20 to LHS: 10 + 20 = 3y − 2y
Step 4: Simplify: 30 = y, so y = 30
Step 5: First angle = 2(30) + 10 = 70°. Second angle = 3(30) − 20 = 70°. (Equal ✓)
Step 6: Other two angles = 180 − 70 = 110° each (linear pair).
Check: 70 + 110 + 70 + 110 = 360° ✓
Exercise 5.2 — Parallel Lines and Transversal (8 Questions)

Q1. State the property used: If a || b and a transversal cuts them, ∠1 = ∠5.

Show Solution
Step 1: Identify the positions of ∠1 and ∠5 — they are on the same side of the transversal, in matching positions at each intersection.
Step 2: Angles in matching positions are called corresponding angles.
Step 3: When lines are parallel, corresponding angles are equal.
Answer: The property used is corresponding angles (when parallel lines are cut by a transversal, corresponding angles are equal).

Q2. State the property used: If a || b and a transversal cuts them, ∠4 = ∠6.

Show Solution
Step 1: ∠4 and ∠6 are on opposite sides of the transversal.
Step 2: They are between the parallel lines (in the interior region).
Step 3: Angles on opposite sides of the transversal, in the interior, are alternate interior angles.
Answer: The property used is alternate interior angles (when parallel lines are cut by a transversal, alternate interior angles are equal).

Q3. In the figure, l || m, transversal t cuts them. If ∠3 = 110°, find all other angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: ∠3 = 110° (given).
Step 2: ∠1 = 180° − 110° = 70° (linear pair with ∠3 on line l).
Step 3: ∠2 = 180° − 110° = 70° (linear pair with ∠3). ∠4 = 110° (vertically opposite to ∠3).
Step 4: Using corresponding angles (l || m): ∠7 = ∠3 = 110°, ∠5 = ∠1 = 70°, ∠6 = ∠2 = 70°, ∠8 = ∠4 = 110°.
All angles: ∠1=70°, ∠2=70°, ∠3=110°, ∠4=110°, ∠5=70°, ∠6=70°, ∠7=110°, ∠8=110°
Check: Alternate interior: ∠3 = ∠6? 110 ≠ 70 — wait, let us recheck. ∠3 and ∠6 are alternate interior so ∠6 = ∠3 = 110°. Correcting: ∠6 = 110°, ∠5 = 70° (linear pair with ∠6). So: ∠5=70°, ∠6=110°, ∠7=70° (vert. opp. to ∠6?). Let me label carefully.
Careful labelling: At first intersection: ∠1(top-right)=70°, ∠2(top-left)=110°... The answer depends on exact positions. Using standard labelling: ∠3=110° → ∠1=70°, ∠2=110°, ∠4=70° at first point. At second point: ∠5=110°, ∠6=70°, ∠7=110°, ∠8=70° (corresponding to ∠1,∠2,∠3,∠4).
Final answer: ∠1=70°, ∠2=110°, ∠3=110°, ∠4=70°, ∠5=110°, ∠6=70°, ∠7=110°, ∠8=70° ✓

Q4. If l || m, find the value of x when a transversal makes angles (2x + 20)° and (3x − 10)° as corresponding angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Corresponding angles are equal when lines are parallel.
Step 2: Set them equal: 2x + 20 = 3x − 10
Step 3: Transpose: 20 + 10 = 3x − 2x
Step 4: Simplify: x = 30
Step 5: Angles = 2(30)+20 = 80° and 3(30)−10 = 80°. Equal ✓

Q5. If l || m, find x when co-interior angles are (2x)° and (3x + 30)°.

Show Solution
Step 1: Co-interior angles add up to 180° (when lines are parallel).
Step 2: 2x + (3x + 30) = 180
Step 3: Simplify: 5x + 30 = 180
Step 4: Transpose: 5x = 180 − 30 = 150
Step 5: Divide: x = 150 ÷ 5 = 30
Step 6: Angles = 2(30) = 60° and 3(30)+30 = 120°.
Check: 60 + 120 = 180 ✓

Q6. In the figure l || m. The transversal makes ∠1 = 55°. Find ∠2 (alternate interior to ∠1) and ∠3 (co-interior with ∠1).

Show Solution
Step 1: ∠1 = 55° (given), l || m.
Step 2: ∠2 is alternate interior to ∠1. Alternate interior angles are equal.
Step 3: ∠2 = ∠1 = 55°
Step 4: ∠3 is co-interior with ∠1. Co-interior angles add to 180°.
Step 5: ∠3 = 180° − 55° = 125°
Check: ∠1 + ∠3 = 55 + 125 = 180 ✓

Q7. AB || CD. A transversal EF intersects AB at G and CD at H. If ∠AGE = 130°, find ∠GHD.

Show Solution
Step 1: Draw the situation: AB and CD are parallel. EF crosses them at G and H.
Step 2: ∠AGE = 130° is above line AB, on the left of the transversal.
Step 3: ∠GHD is below line CD, on the right of the transversal.
Step 4: ∠AGE and ∠GHD are alternate interior angles (on opposite sides of the transversal, between the parallel lines).
Step 5: Alternate interior angles are equal: ∠GHD = ∠AGE = 130°
Check: Both angles are between the parallel lines and on alternate sides ✓

Q8. l || m. A transversal cuts them making angles (5x − 20)° and (3x + 40)° as alternate interior angles. Find x and both angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Alternate interior angles are equal (since l || m).
Step 2: Set equal: 5x − 20 = 3x + 40
Step 3: Transpose: 5x − 3x = 40 + 20
Step 4: Simplify: 2x = 60 → x = 30
Step 5: First angle = 5(30) − 20 = 150 − 20 = 130°
Step 6: Second angle = 3(30) + 40 = 90 + 40 = 130°
Check: Both angles = 130°. Equal ✓ (alternate interior angles verified)

9. The supplement of an angle is four times its complement. Find the angle.

Show Answer
Step 1: Let the angle = x.
Step 2: Supplement = 180 − x. Complement = 90 − x.
Step 3: Given: 180 − x = 4(90 − x).
Step 4: Expand: 180 − x = 360 − 4x.
Step 5: Transpose: 4x − x = 360 − 180 → 3x = 180 → x = 60°
Check: Supplement = 120, Complement = 30. Is 120 = 4 × 30? Yes ✓

Show Answer

Step 2: ∠AEP and ∠EFD are alternate interior angles (on opposite sides, between the parallel lines).
Step 3: ∠EFD = ∠AEP = 110° (alternate interior angles, AB || CD).
Step 4: ∠EFC + ∠EFD = 180° (linear pair on line CD).
Step 5: ∠EFC = 180 − 110 = 70°
Check: 110 + 70 = 180 ✓

1. Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. One of the angles is (7x − 15)° and its co-interior angle is (5x + 3)°. Find x, both angles, and all remaining 6 angles.

Show Answer
Step 1: Co-interior angles sum to 180°: (7x − 15) + (5x + 3) = 180.
Step 2: 12x − 12 = 180 → 12x = 192 → x = 16.
Step 3: First angle = 7(16)−15 = 112−15 = 97°.
Step 4: Second angle = 5(16)+3 = 80+3 = 83°.
Step 5: At first intersection: 97°, 83°, 97°, 83° (linear pairs + vertically opposite).
Step 6: At second intersection: 83°, 97°, 83°, 97° (corresponding angles).
Check: 97 + 83 = 180 ✓
HardHOTS

2. An angle is twice its supplement. Find the angle.

Show Answer
Step 1: Let the angle = x. Its supplement = 180 − x.
Step 2: Given: x = 2(180 − x).
Step 3: Expand: x = 360 − 2x.
Step 4: Transpose: x + 2x = 360 → 3x = 360.
Step 5: x = 360 ÷ 3 = 120°
Check: Supplement = 180 − 120 = 60°. Is 120 = 2 × 60? Yes ✓
HardHOTS

3. Three lines meet at a point. The six angles formed are in the ratio 1:2:3:1:2:3. Find all six angles.

Show Answer
Step 1: Angles around a point sum to 360°.
Step 2: Let angles be x, 2x, 3x, x, 2x, 3x.
Step 3: x + 2x + 3x + x + 2x + 3x = 360 → 12x = 360.
Step 4: x = 30°.
Step 5: The six angles are 30°, 60°, 90°, 30°, 60°, 90°.
Check: 30+60+90+30+60+90 = 360 ✓. Vertically opposite pairs: 30=30, 60=60, 90=90 ✓

Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

  • 1. Mixing up complementary and supplementary
    Wrong: "Supplementary angles add to 90°"
    Right: Complementary = Corner = 90°. Supplementary = Straight = 180°.
  • 2. Thinking adjacent angles are vertically opposite
    Wrong: Angles next to each other at an intersection are equal.
    Right: Adjacent angles form a linear pair (sum = 180°). Only opposite angles are equal.
  • 3. Using parallel-line rules when lines are NOT parallel
    Wrong: Any two lines cut by a transversal have equal corresponding angles.
    Right: Corresponding angles are equal only if the lines are parallel. Always check for the parallel condition first!
  • 4. Confusing alternate interior with co-interior angles
    Wrong: "Co-interior angles are equal."
    Right: Alternate interior angles are equal. Co-interior (same-side interior) angles are supplementary (add to 180°). Use the Z-shape vs C-shape test!
  • 5. Forgetting to verify your answer
    Wrong: Writing the answer without any check.
    Right: Always substitute back. Check: do linear pairs add to 180°? Do vertically opposite angles match? Do all angles at a point sum to 360°?
