Chapter 5: Lines and Angles

Summary

  • A line extends infinitely in both directions (e.g., a railway track that goes on forever in both directions), while a ray starts at one point and goes on forever in one direction (e.g., a torch beam starts at the torch and goes in one direction).
  • An angle is formed when two rays meet at a common endpoint (called the vertex) — e.g., the corner of a book is a right angle.
  • Types of angles: acute (<90°), right (=90°), obtuse (>90° and <180°), straight (=180°), and reflex (>180°) — e.g., 45° is acute, 90° is right, 120° is obtuse, 180° is straight.
  • Complementary angles are two angles that add up to 90° (e.g., 30° and 60° are complementary).
  • Supplementary angles are two angles that add up to 180° (e.g., 110° and 70° are supplementary).
  • Vertically opposite angles (formed when two lines cross) are always equal (e.g., when two lines cross, opposite angles are both 50°).
  • When a transversal crosses two parallel lines (e.g., if one angle is 60°, the corresponding angle on the other line is also 60°):
    • Alternate interior angles are equal.
    • Co-interior (same-side interior) angles add up to 180°.
    • Corresponding angles are equal.

Key Formulas

Complementary angles: a + b = 90°

Supplementary angles: a + b = 180°

Linear pair: a + b = 180°

Vertically opposite angles: They are always equal.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Find the supplement of 65°

Step 1: Supplementary angles add up to 180°.
Step 2: Supplement = 180° − 65° = 115°

Example 2: Two angles are complementary. One is 35°. Find the other.

Step 1: Complementary angles add up to 90°.
Step 2: Other angle = 90° − 35° = 55°

Example 3: A transversal cuts two parallel lines making an angle of 70°. Find all the angles.

Step 1: At the first intersection, we get 70° and its linear pair: 180° − 70° = 110°. Vertically opposite angles give us another pair of 70° and 110°. So the four angles at the first point are: 70°, 110°, 70°, 110°.
Step 2: Since the lines are parallel, corresponding angles are equal. The four angles at the second intersection are also: 70°, 110°, 70°, 110°.

Show Answer

No. Acute angles are less than 90°. The maximum sum of two acute angles is just under 90° + 90° = 180°, but since each must be strictly less than 90°, their sum is always less than 180°. So they cannot be supplementary.

Show Answer
Linear pair angles add up to 180°: (3x + 10) + (2x + 20) = 180 → 5x + 30 = 180 → 5x = 150 → x = 30. The angles are 3(30) + 10 = 100° and 2(30) + 20 = 80°.

Common Mistakes

  • Mixing up complementary and supplementary: Remember — Complementary adds to 90° (think "C" for "Corner", a right angle), Supplementary adds to 180° (think "S" for "Straight line").
  • Forgetting vertically opposite angles are equal: When two lines cross, the angles across from each other are always equal. Don’t add them to 180°!
  • Not identifying parallel-line angle relationships: Before using rules like alternate angles or corresponding angles, make sure the lines are actually parallel and you’ve correctly spotted the transversal.

NCERT Exercise Solutions — All Questions Solved

Exercise 5.1 — Pairs of Angles (10 Questions)

Q1. Find the complement of: (a) 45° (b) 65° (c) 28° (d) 0°

Show Solution
Rule: Complementary angles add to 90°. Complement = 90° − given angle.
(a) 90° − 45° = 45°
(b) 90° − 65° = 25°
(c) 90° − 28° = 62°
(d) 90° − 0° = 90°

Q2. Find the supplement of: (a) 105° (b) 70° (c) 90° (d) 0°

Show Solution
Rule: Supplementary angles add to 180°. Supplement = 180° − given angle.
(a) 180° − 105° = 75°
(b) 180° − 70° = 110°
(c) 180° − 90° = 90° (a right angle is its own supplement!)
(d) 180° − 0° = 180°

Q3. Two supplementary angles are in the ratio 2:3. Find them.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let the angles be 2x and 3x.
Step 2: Supplementary means they add to 180°: 2x + 3x = 180°
Step 3: 5x = 180°
Step 4: x = 180° ÷ 5 = 36°
Step 5: Angles: 2(36°) = 72° and 3(36°) = 108°
Check: 72° + 108° = 180° ✓

Q4. Two complementary angles are in the ratio 1:4. Find them.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let angles = x and 4x
Step 2: x + 4x = 90° → 5x = 90°
Step 3: x = 18°
Step 4: Angles: 18° and 4(18°) = 72°
Check: 18° + 72° = 90° ✓

Q5. Two lines intersect. One angle is 65°. Find the other three angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Vertically opposite angles are equal. So the angle opposite 65° = 65°
Step 2: Adjacent angles form a linear pair (add to 180°): 180° − 65° = 115°
Step 3: The fourth angle (opposite to 115°) = 115°
Answer: The four angles are 65°, 115°, 65°, 115°

Q6. The angles of a linear pair are (3x + 10)° and (2x + 20)°. Find x and the angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Linear pair adds to 180°: (3x + 10) + (2x + 20) = 180
Step 2: Simplify: 5x + 30 = 180
Step 3: 5x = 150 → x = 30
Step 4: First angle = 3(30) + 10 = 100°
Step 5: Second angle = 2(30) + 20 = 80°
Check: 100° + 80° = 180° ✓

Q7. Can two angles be supplementary if both are: (a) acute? (b) obtuse? (c) right angles?

Show Solution
(a) Both acute? Acute means < 90°. Max sum = 89° + 89° = 178° < 180°. No!
(b) Both obtuse? Obtuse means > 90°. Min sum = 91° + 91° = 182° > 180°. No!
(c) Both right? 90° + 90° = 180°. Yes!

Q8. An angle is 30° more than its complement. Find it.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let the angle = x. Its complement = 90° − x.
Step 2: “30° more than complement”: x = (90 − x) + 30
Step 3: x = 120 − x
Step 4: 2x = 120 → x = 60°
Check: Complement = 30°. Is 60 = 30 + 30? Yes ✓

Q9. An angle is 4 times its supplement. Find both angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let supplement = x. Then the angle = 4x.
Step 2: Sum = 180°: x + 4x = 180
Step 3: 5x = 180 → x = 36°
Answer: Supplement = 36°, Angle = 4(36) = 144°
Check: 36 + 144 = 180 ✓

Q10. Two vertically opposite angles are (3x + 5)° and (5x − 25)°. Find them.

Show Solution
Step 1: Vertically opposite angles are equal: 3x + 5 = 5x − 25
Step 2: Transpose: 5 + 25 = 5x − 3x → 30 = 2x
Step 3: x = 15
Step 4: Angle = 3(15) + 5 = 50°
Check: 5(15) − 25 = 75 − 25 = 50° ✓ (Equal!)
Exercise 5.2 — Parallel Lines & Transversal (8 Questions)

Q1. A transversal cuts two parallel lines. One angle is 70°. Find all 8 angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: At the first intersection: 70° and its linear pair 180°−70° = 110°. Vertically opposite: 70° and 110°. So: 70°, 110°, 70°, 110°
Step 2: At the second intersection: corresponding angles are equal. So same set: 70°, 110°, 70°, 110°
All 8 angles: 70°, 110°, 70°, 110°, 70°, 110°, 70°, 110°

Q2. A transversal makes 55° with one parallel line. Find the co-interior angle.

Show Solution
Step 1: Co-interior angles (same side of transversal, between parallel lines) add to 180°.
Step 2: Co-interior angle = 180° − 55° = 125°

Q3. Find x if two parallel lines are cut by a transversal making angles (2x + 15)° and (3x − 20)° which are alternate interior angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Alternate interior angles are equal (when lines are parallel).
Step 2: 2x + 15 = 3x − 20
Step 3: 15 + 20 = 3x − 2x → 35 = x
Step 4: x = 35
Step 5: Angles: 2(35)+15 = 85° and 3(35)−20 = 85° ✓

Q4. Find x if co-interior angles are (4x)° and (x + 40)°.

Show Solution
Step 1: Co-interior angles add to 180°: 4x + (x + 40) = 180
Step 2: 5x + 40 = 180
Step 3: 5x = 140 → x = 28
Step 4: Angles: 4(28) = 112° and 28+40 = 68°
Check: 112 + 68 = 180 ✓

Q5. Two parallel lines are cut by a transversal. If corresponding angles are (7x − 10)° and (5x + 14)°, find x.

Show Solution
Step 1: Corresponding angles are equal: 7x − 10 = 5x + 14
Step 2: 7x − 5x = 14 + 10 → 2x = 24
Step 3: x = 12
Step 4: Angle = 7(12) − 10 = 74°
Check: 5(12) + 14 = 74° ✓

Q6. Are lines parallel if alternate interior angles are 48° and 48°?

Show Solution
Step 1: If alternate interior angles are equal, the lines are parallel.
Step 2: 48° = 48° ✓
Yes, the lines are parallel.

Q7. Are lines parallel if co-interior angles are 120° and 50°?

Show Solution
Step 1: For parallel lines, co-interior angles must add to 180°.
Step 2: 120° + 50° = 170° ≠ 180°
No, the lines are NOT parallel.

Q8. In the figure, AB ∥ CD. A transversal makes 62° at AB. Find all angles at CD.

Show Solution
Step 1: Corresponding angle at CD = 62° (corresponding angles are equal)
Step 2: Linear pair at CD: 180° − 62° = 118°
Step 3: Vertically opposite angles give us: 62° and 118°
Angles at CD: 62°, 118°, 62°, 118°

1. Can two supplementary angles both be acute? Both obtuse? Explain.

Show Answer
Both acute? Max sum of two acute angles = 89+89 = 178 < 180. No!
Both obtuse? Min sum = 91+91 = 182 > 180. No!

2. If two parallel lines are cut by a transversal and one angle is x°, express ALL 8 angles in terms of x.

Show Answer
The 8 angles are only two values: x° and (180−x)°, each appearing 4 times. Corresponding angles = x, alternate = x, co-interior = 180−x, vertically opposite = x.
HardHOTS

3. Three lines intersect at a single point. One angle is 40° and another is 90°. Find all 6 angles.

Show Answer
Step 1: Three lines make 6 angles at a point. Total = 360°.
Step 2: Vertically opposite to 40° = 40°. Opposite to 90° = 90°.
Step 3: Remaining: 360 − 40 − 40 − 90 − 90 = 100. Two equal angles: 50° each.
All 6: 40°, 90°, 50°, 40°, 90°, 50°

Common Mistakes

  • Mixing up complementary and supplementary
    Complement adds to 180°
    Complement = 90° (think: C for Corner, 90°)
    Supplement = 180° (think: S for Straight, 180°)
  • Forgetting vertically opposite angles are equal
    All 4 angles at an intersection are different
    Vertically opposite angles are ALWAYS equal. Only 2 distinct values!
  • Using alternate angles when lines aren’t parallel
    Alternate angles are always equal
    Alternate angles are equal ONLY when lines are parallel!
  • Confusing corresponding and alternate angles
    Can’t tell which is which
    Corresponding = same position (both top-left). Alternate = opposite sides, between lines (Z-shape).
