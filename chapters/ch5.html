Chapter 5: Lines and Angles

Summary

  • A line extends infinitely in both directions, while a ray starts at one point and goes on forever in one direction.
  • An angle is formed when two rays meet at a common endpoint (called the vertex).
  • Types of angles: acute (<90°), right (=90°), obtuse (>90° and <180°), straight (=180°), and reflex (>180°).
  • Complementary angles are two angles that add up to 90°.
  • Supplementary angles are two angles that add up to 180°.
  • Vertically opposite angles (formed when two lines cross) are always equal.
  • When a transversal crosses two parallel lines:
    • Alternate interior angles are equal.
    • Co-interior (same-side interior) angles add up to 180°.
    • Corresponding angles are equal.

Key Formulas

Complementary angles: a + b = 90°

Supplementary angles: a + b = 180°

Linear pair: a + b = 180°

Vertically opposite angles: They are always equal.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Find the supplement of 65°

Step 1: Supplementary angles add up to 180°.
Step 2: Supplement = 180° − 65° = 115°

Example 2: Two angles are complementary. One is 35°. Find the other.

Step 1: Complementary angles add up to 90°.
Step 2: Other angle = 90° − 35° = 55°

Example 3: A transversal cuts two parallel lines making an angle of 70°. Find all the angles.

Step 1: At the first intersection, we get 70° and its linear pair: 180° − 70° = 110°. Vertically opposite angles give us another pair of 70° and 110°. So the four angles at the first point are: 70°, 110°, 70°, 110°.
Step 2: Since the lines are parallel, corresponding angles are equal. The four angles at the second intersection are also: 70°, 110°, 70°, 110°.

Practice Problems

Easy

1. Find the complement of 48°.

Show Answer

Complement = 90° − 48° = 42°

Easy

2. Find the supplement of 123°.

Show Answer

Supplement = 180° − 123° = 57°

Medium

3. Two supplementary angles are in the ratio 2:3. Find both angles.

Show Answer

Let the angles be 2x and 3x. Then 2x + 3x = 180°, so 5x = 180°, x = 36°. The angles are 72° and 108°.

Medium

4. Two lines intersect. One of the angles formed is 42°. What are the other three angles?

Show Answer

Vertically opposite angle = 42°. The other two angles (linear pairs) = 180° − 42° = 138° each. So the four angles are 42°, 138°, 42°, 138°.

Hard

5. A transversal cuts two parallel lines. One of the co-interior angles is 65°. Find the other co-interior angle and all eight angles at both intersections.

Show Answer

Co-interior angles add up to 180°, so the other co-interior angle = 180° − 65° = 115°. At the first intersection: 65°, 115°, 65°, 115°. At the second intersection: 115°, 65°, 115°, 65°. All eight angles are 65°, 115°, 65°, 115°, 115°, 65°, 115°, 65°.

Hard

6. Can two angles be supplementary if both of them are acute? Explain your reasoning.

Show Answer

No. Acute angles are less than 90°. The maximum sum of two acute angles is just under 90° + 90° = 180°, but since each must be strictly less than 90°, their sum is always less than 180°. So they cannot be supplementary.

Common Mistakes

  • Mixing up complementary and supplementary: Remember — Complementary adds to 90° (think "C" for "Corner", a right angle), Supplementary adds to 180° (think "S" for "Straight line").
  • Forgetting vertically opposite angles are equal: When two lines cross, the angles across from each other are always equal. Don’t add them to 180°!
  • Not identifying parallel-line angle relationships: Before using rules like alternate angles or corresponding angles, make sure the lines are actually parallel and you’ve correctly spotted the transversal.
