Types of Angles

Real-world connection: Open a book slightly — that’s an acute angle. Open it to an L-shape — that’s a right angle. Open it wide — obtuse angle. Lay it flat — straight angle!

Pairs of Angles

Complementary Angles (add to 90°)

  • Two angles are complementary if their sum = 90°.
  • Example: 30° and 60° are complementary (30 + 60 = 90).
  • To find the complement: 90° − given angle.
Memory trick: Complementary = Corner (a corner/right angle is 90°). Supplementary = Straight (a straight line is 180°).

Supplementary Angles (add to 180°)

  • Two angles are supplementary if their sum = 180°.
  • Example: 110° and 70° are supplementary (110 + 70 = 180).
  • To find the supplement: 180° − given angle.
Real-world: When you open a door partway, the angle between the door and the wall, plus the angle between the door and the doorframe, is always 180° — they’re supplementary!

Intersecting Lines

Linear Pair

  • When two adjacent angles sit on a straight line, they form a linear pair.
  • A linear pair always adds to 180°.

Vertically Opposite Angles

  • When two lines cross, they form 4 angles.
  • The angles opposite each other are called vertically opposite and are always equal.
Quick trick: When two lines cross, you only need to find ONE angle. The other three are automatically determined: opposite = same, adjacent = 180° minus.

Parallel Lines and Transversal

  • A transversal is a line that cuts two or more lines.
  • When a transversal cuts two parallel lines, special angle relationships form.
How to remember which is which:
• Corresponding = same position (both top-left, or both bottom-right)
• Alternate interior = between the lines, opposite sides (makes a Z-shape)
• Co-interior = between the lines, same side (makes a U-shape)
The big shortcut: When a transversal cuts parallel lines, ALL 8 angles are just two values: x° and (180−x)°. Find one angle and you know them all!

NCERT Exercise Solutions — All Questions Solved

Exercise 5.1 — Pairs of Angles (10 Questions)

Q1. Find the complement of: (a) 45° (b) 65° (c) 28° (d) 0°

Show Solution
Rule: Complement = 90° − angle.
(a) 90 − 45 = 45°   (b) 90 − 65 = 25°
(c) 90 − 28 = 62°   (d) 90 − 0 = 90°

Q2. Find the supplement of: (a) 105° (b) 70° (c) 90°

Show Solution
Rule: Supplement = 180° − angle.
(a) 180 − 105 = 75°   (b) 180 − 70 = 110°
(c) 180 − 90 = 90° (a right angle is its own supplement!)

Q3. Two supplementary angles are in ratio 2:3. Find them.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let angles = 2x and 3x
Step 2: Supplementary: 2x + 3x = 180 → 5x = 180
Step 3: x = 36
Step 4: Angles: 2(36) = 72° and 3(36) = 108°
Check: 72 + 108 = 180 ✓

Q4. Two complementary angles are in ratio 1:4. Find them.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let angles = x and 4x
Step 2: x + 4x = 90 → 5x = 90 → x = 18
Step 3: Angles: 18° and 72°
Check: 18 + 72 = 90 ✓

Q5. Two lines intersect. One angle is 65°. Find the other three.

Show Solution
Step 1: Vertically opposite to 65° = 65°
Step 2: Linear pair: 180 − 65 = 115°
Step 3: Vertically opposite to 115° = 115°
All four: 65°, 115°, 65°, 115°

Q6. Linear pair angles are (3x+10)° and (2x+20)°. Find x and both angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Linear pair = 180°: (3x+10) + (2x+20) = 180
Step 2: 5x + 30 = 180 → 5x = 150 → x = 30
Step 3: Angles: 3(30)+10 = 100° and 2(30)+20 = 80°
Check: 100 + 80 = 180 ✓

Q7. Can two supplementary angles both be: (a) acute? (b) obtuse? (c) right?

Show Solution
(a) Both acute? Max sum: 89+89 = 178 < 180. No!
(b) Both obtuse? Min sum: 91+91 = 182 > 180. No!
(c) Both right? 90+90 = 180. Yes!

Q8. An angle is 30° more than its complement. Find it.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let angle = x. Complement = 90 − x.
Step 2: x = (90 − x) + 30
Step 3: x = 120 − x → 2x = 120 → x = 60°
Check: Complement = 30°. Is 60 = 30 + 30? Yes ✓

Q9. An angle is 4 times its supplement. Find both.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let supplement = x. Angle = 4x.
Step 2: x + 4x = 180 → 5x = 180 → x = 36
Step 3: Supplement = 36°, Angle = 144°

Q10. Vertically opposite angles are (3x+5)° and (5x−25)°. Find them.

Show Solution
Step 1: Vertically opposite = equal: 3x + 5 = 5x − 25
Step 2: 30 = 2x → x = 15
Step 3: Angle = 3(15)+5 = 50°
Check: 5(15)−25 = 50 ✓
Exercise 5.2 — Parallel Lines & Transversal (8 Questions)

Q1. Transversal cuts parallel lines. One angle is 70°. Find all 8 angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: At first intersection: 70°, 110°, 70°, 110° (linear pairs + vert. opp.)
Step 2: Corresponding angles are equal, so second intersection: same.
All 8: 70°, 110°, 70°, 110°, 70°, 110°, 70°, 110°

Q2. Transversal makes 55° with a parallel line. Find the co-interior angle.

Show Solution
Step 1: Co-interior angles add to 180°
Step 2: 180 − 55 = 125°

Q3. Alternate interior angles are (2x+15)° and (3x−20)°. Find x.

Show Solution
Step 1: Alternate interior angles are equal: 2x+15 = 3x−20
Step 2: 15+20 = 3x−2x → x = 35
Step 3: Angles: 2(35)+15 = 85°
Check: 3(35)−20 = 85 ✓

Q4. Co-interior angles are (4x)° and (x+40)°. Find x.

Show Solution
Step 1: Co-interior = 180°: 4x + (x+40) = 180
Step 2: 5x + 40 = 180 → 5x = 140 → x = 28
Step 3: Angles: 4(28)=112° and 28+40=68°
Check: 112+68 = 180 ✓

Q5. Corresponding angles are (7x−10)° and (5x+14)°. Find x.

Show Solution
Step 1: Corresponding = equal: 7x−10 = 5x+14
Step 2: 2x = 24 → x = 12
Step 3: Angle = 7(12)−10 = 74°

Q6. Are lines parallel if alternate interior angles are 48° and 48°?

Show Solution
Step 1: Alternate interior angles are equal (48=48).
Step 2: Yes, lines are parallel.

Q7. Are lines parallel if co-interior angles are 120° and 50°?

Show Solution
Step 1: Co-interior must add to 180°: 120+50 = 170 ≠ 180
No, lines are NOT parallel.

Q8. AB ∥ CD. Transversal makes 62° at AB. Find all angles at CD.

Show Solution
Step 1: Corresponding angle at CD = 62°
Step 2: Linear pair: 180−62 = 118°
Step 3: Vertically opposite: 62° and 118°
Angles at CD: 62°, 118°, 62°, 118°

Common Mistakes

  • Mixing up complementary and supplementary
    Complementary = 180°
    Complementary = Corner (90°). Supplementary = Straight (180°).
  • Forgetting vertically opposite angles are equal
    All 4 angles at intersection are different
    Only 2 distinct values. Opposite angles are ALWAYS equal!
  • Using parallel line rules when lines aren’t parallel
    Alternate angles are always equal
    Only when lines are parallel!
  • Confusing corresponding and alternate angles
    Can’t tell which is which
    Corresponding = same position. Alternate = Z-shape. Co-interior = U-shape.
