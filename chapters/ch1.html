What are Integers?

Real-world connection: Have you ever seen a weather report showing −5°C? Or pressed “B1” in a lift to go to the basement? Or heard someone say “I owe Rs 200”? All of these use negative numbers — and that’s exactly what integers are about!
  • You already know Natural Numbers: 1, 2, 3, 4, … (the counting numbers).
  • Add zero and you get Whole Numbers: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, …
  • Now extend in the other direction and you get Integers: … −3, −2, −1, 0, 1, 2, 3, …
  • Integers include all positive numbers, zero, and negative numbers. They go on forever in both directions!
  • Zero is special — it is neither positive nor negative. It sits right in the middle.
Where do we see negative numbers in real life?
• Temperature: −10°C means 10 degrees below zero (very cold!)
• Buildings: Floor B2 = 2 floors below ground = −2
• Money: If you owe Rs 500, your balance is −500
• Sea level: The Dead Sea is 430 m below sea level = −430 m
• Timeline: 200 BC means 200 years before year 0
• Golf: A score of −3 means 3 under par (that’s good!)

Comparing Integers

Tricky part — comparing two negative numbers:
Many students think −8 is bigger than −3 because 8 > 3. But that’s wrong!

Think of temperature: −8°C is colder than −3°C. Colder = smaller. On the number line, −8 is further LEFT, so −8 < −3.

Rule: For negative numbers, the one with the bigger digit is actually smaller!
Frequently asked in exams: “Arrange the following integers in ascending/descending order.” Always draw a quick number line in your rough work to avoid mistakes with negatives!

Addition of Integers

Case 1: Same Signs — Add and Keep the Sign

  • Positive + Positive: (+5) + (+3) = +8. Normal addition!
  • Negative + Negative: (−5) + (−3) = −8. Going further into negatives.
Money: You owe Rs 5 (−5). Borrow Rs 3 more (−3). Now you owe Rs 8 total (−8). Debts add up!

Case 2: Different Signs — Subtract, Keep Sign of Bigger

  • (−7) + (+4): Subtract 7 − 4 = 3. Since 7 is bigger and negative → −3
  • (+9) + (−4): Subtract 9 − 4 = 5. Since 9 is bigger and positive → +5
Money: You owe Rs 7 (−7). Earn Rs 4 (+4). Still owe Rs 3 (−3). Debt was bigger, so result stays negative.

Subtraction of Integers

The Golden Rule: a − b = a + (−b) — Subtracting any number = Adding its opposite.
WHY does subtracting a negative = adding?

A “negative” is like a debt. If someone removes your debt, that’s the same as giving you money!

• You owe Rs 300 (−300). Friend says “I’m removing that debt.”
• That’s subtracting a negative: 0 − (−300) = 0 + 300 = +300.
• Removing a debt = gaining money!

More Solved Examples

(−12) − (−5) = ?

Step 1: Convert: (−12) + (+5)
Step 2: Different signs. 12 − 5 = 7. Bigger number (12) is negative.
Answer: −7 (You owed 12, someone forgave 5, you still owe 7)

(−4) − 7 = ?

Step 1: Convert: (−4) + (−7)
Step 2: Same signs (both negative). 4 + 7 = 11. Keep negative sign.
Answer: −11 (You owed 4, borrowed 7 more)

0 − (−6) = ?

Step 1: Convert: 0 + (+6)
Answer: 6 (Removing a debt from zero = a gain)

Sign Rules Summary

Multiple negatives? Count the negative signs:
• Even count of negatives = positive (they cancel in pairs)
• Odd count of negatives = negative
Example: (−2) × (−3) × (−4) = 3 negatives (odd) = −24

Division of Integers

  • Division follows the same sign rules as multiplication.
  • Same signs → positive: (−12) ÷ (−4) = +3
  • Different signs → negative: (−15) ÷ 3 = −5
  • Division is the reverse of multiplication: if (−3) × 4 = −12, then (−12) ÷ 4 = −3.
  • You can NEVER divide by zero! (−7) ÷ 0 is undefined. No number multiplied by 0 gives −7.

Properties of Integers

Distributive Property: a × (b + c) = a × b + a × c
Example: (−2) × (5 + 3) = (−2)×5 + (−2)×3 = −10 + (−6) = −16 ✓
Additive Inverse: a + (−a) = 0
Every integer has an opposite. The opposite of 5 is −5. The opposite of −3 is 3.

Exercise 1.1 — Recall of Integer Operations (10 Questions)

Q1. Write the following using integers with appropriate signs: (a) 500 m above sea level (b) 35°C below zero (c) A deposit of Rs 2000 (d) A withdrawal of Rs 700

Show Solution
(a) 500 m above sea level = +500 m (above = positive)
(b) 35°C below zero = −35°C (below zero = negative)
(c) A deposit of Rs 2000 = +2000 (deposit = money coming in = positive)
(d) A withdrawal of Rs 700 = −700 (withdrawal = money going out = negative)

Q2. Find: (a) (−11) + (−12)   (b) (+10) + (−15)   (c) (−250) + (+180)   (d) (−380) + (−270)

Show Solution
(a) Same signs (both −): 11 + 12 = 23, keep negative. Answer: −23
(b) Different signs: 15 − 10 = 5, bigger (15) is negative. Answer: −5
(c) Different signs: 250 − 180 = 70, bigger (250) is negative. Answer: −70
(d) Same signs (both −): 380 + 270 = 650, keep negative. Answer: −650

Q3. Find: (a) (−8) − (−3)   (b) (+15) − (−6)   (c) (−20) − (+13)   (d) 0 − (−9)

Show Solution
(a) (−8) − (−3) = (−8) + 3 = −5
(b) 15 − (−6) = 15 + 6 = 21
(c) (−20) − 13 = (−20) + (−13) = −33
(d) 0 − (−9) = 0 + 9 = 9

Q4. Fill in the blanks: (a) (−5) + (_) = −2   (b) 8 + (_) = 0   (c) (−3) + (_) = −10   (d) (_) + (−7) = −1

Show Solution
(a) (−5) + x = −2 → x = −2 − (−5) = −2 + 5 = 3
(b) 8 + x = 0 → x = −8 (additive inverse of 8)
(c) (−3) + x = −10 → x = −10 + 3 = −7
(d) x + (−7) = −1 → x = −1 + 7 = 6

Q5. Verify: (−6) + (−4) = (−4) + (−6). Which property does this show?

Show Solution
LHS: (−6) + (−4) = −10
RHS: (−4) + (−6) = −10
LHS = RHS = −10 ✓ This shows the commutative property of addition (order doesn’t matter).

Q6. The sum of two integers is −13. If one of them is 7, find the other.

Show Solution
Let the other integer = x. Then 7 + x = −13.
x = −13 − 7 = −20
Check: 7 + (−20) = −13 ✓

Q7. On a winter night, the temperature in Delhi was 3°C. It fell by 6°C by midnight. What was the temperature at midnight? It further dropped 2°C. What was the final temperature?

Show Solution
At midnight: 3 − 6 = 3 + (−6) = −3°C
After further drop: −3 − 2 = −3 + (−2) = −5°C

Q8. Find the successor and predecessor of: (a) −15   (b) 0   (c) −1   (d) −200

Show Solution
Successor = number + 1. Predecessor = number − 1.
(a) −15: Successor = −14, Predecessor = −16
(b) 0: Successor = 1, Predecessor = −1
(c) −1: Successor = 0, Predecessor = −2
(d) −200: Successor = −199, Predecessor = −201

Q9. Arrange in ascending order: −15, 0, −35, 7, −1, 23, −8

Show Solution
On a number line, further left = smaller.
Ascending order: −35, −15, −8, −1, 0, 7, 23

Q10. Evaluate using suitable properties: (−7) + 3 + (−5) + 7 + (−3) + 5

Show Solution
Rearrange (commutative): [(−7) + 7] + [3 + (−3)] + [(−5) + 5]
Each pair sums to 0: 0 + 0 + 0 = 0
Trick: look for additive inverses that cancel out!
Exercise 1.2 — Multiplication of Integers (8 Questions)

Q1. Find each product: (a) 3 × (−4)   (b) (−5) × 6   (c) (−7) × (−8)   (d) 9 × (−11)

Show Solution
(a) 3 × (−4) = −12 (different signs = negative)
(b) (−5) × 6 = −30 (different signs = negative)
(c) (−7) × (−8) = +56 (same signs = positive)
(d) 9 × (−11) = −99 (different signs = negative)

Q2. Find: (a) (−18) × (−10)   (b) (−1) × 325   (c) (−1) × (−1) × (−5)   (d) (−12) × (−11) × 0

Show Solution
(a) (−18) × (−10) = +180 (same signs)
(b) (−1) × 325 = −325 (multiplying by −1 flips the sign)
(c) (−1) × (−1) = +1, then +1 × (−5) = −5 (3 negatives = odd = negative)
(d) Any number × 0 = 0. No need to calculate the rest!

Q3. Find: (a) (−8) × (−2) × (−3)   (b) (−4) × (−5) × (−2) × (−1)

Show Solution
(a) 3 negatives (odd) → negative. 8 × 2 × 3 = 48. Answer: −48
(b) 4 negatives (even) → positive. 4 × 5 × 2 × 1 = 40. Answer: +40

Q4. In a class test, (+5) marks are given for every correct answer and (−2) for every wrong answer. (a) Rani answered 20 correct and 5 wrong. Find her score. (b) Jay answered 15 correct and 10 wrong. Find his score.

Show Solution
(a) Rani: Correct = 20 × 5 = 100. Wrong = 5 × (−2) = −10. Total = 100 + (−10) = 90
(b) Jay: Correct = 15 × 5 = 75. Wrong = 10 × (−2) = −20. Total = 75 + (−20) = 55

Q5. Replace the blank with an integer: (a) (−3) × (_) = 27   (b) 5 × (_) = −35   (c) (_) × (−8) = −56   (d) (_) × (−12) = 132

Show Solution
(a) 27 ÷ (−3) = −9. Check: (−3) × (−9) = 27 ✓
(b) (−35) ÷ 5 = −7. Check: 5 × (−7) = −35 ✓
(c) (−56) ÷ (−8) = 7. Check: 7 × (−8) = −56 ✓
(d) 132 ÷ (−12) = −11. Check: (−11) × (−12) = 132 ✓

Q6. A shop earns a profit of Rs 5 per pen and a loss of Rs 3 per pencil. Find net profit or loss if: (a) 25 pens and 15 pencils sold (b) 10 pens and 30 pencils sold

Show Solution
(a) Pens: 25 × 5 = Rs 125. Pencils: 15 × (−3) = Rs −45. Net = 125 + (−45) = Rs 80 profit
(b) Pens: 10 × 5 = Rs 50. Pencils: 30 × (−3) = Rs −90. Net = 50 + (−90) = Rs −40 (loss of Rs 40)

Q7. Verify: (−6) × (−3) = (−3) × (−6). What property is this?

Show Solution
LHS: (−6) × (−3) = 18
RHS: (−3) × (−6) = 18
LHS = RHS = 18 ✓ This is the commutative property of multiplication.

Q8. The temperature drops 2°C every hour. If it is currently 8°C: (a) What will it be after 5 hours? (b) What was it 3 hours ago?

Show Solution
(a) After 5 hours: Drop = 5 × (−2) = −10. Temp = 8 + (−10) = −2°C
(b) 3 hours ago: It was warmer. Rise = 3 × 2 = +6. Temp = 8 − (−6) = 8 + 6... Wait, think carefully: 3 hours ago the temp was 6° higher (since it drops 2/hour). So: 8 + 6 = 14°C
Exercise 1.3 — Properties of Multiplication (7 Questions)

Q1. Verify: [(−3) × (−4)] × 5 = (−3) × [(−4) × 5]. What property is this?

Show Solution
LHS: [(−3) × (−4)] × 5 = 12 × 5 = 60
RHS: (−3) × [(−4) × 5] = (−3) × (−20) = 60
LHS = RHS ✓ This is the associative property of multiplication (grouping doesn’t matter).

Q2. Evaluate using distributive property: (a) (−30) × 10 + (−30) × (−2)   (b) 7 × (−50) + 7 × (−30)

Show Solution
(a) = (−30) × [10 + (−2)] = (−30) × 8 = −240
(b) = 7 × [(−50) + (−30)] = 7 × (−80) = −560

Q3. Verify that a × (b + c) = a × b + a × c for: (a) a = 3, b = −4, c = −5   (b) a = −2, b = 6, c = −3

Show Solution
(a) LHS: 3 × (−4 + (−5)) = 3 × (−9) = −27
(a) RHS: 3 × (−4) + 3 × (−5) = −12 + (−15) = −27 ✓
(b) LHS: (−2) × (6 + (−3)) = (−2) × 3 = −6
(b) RHS: (−2) × 6 + (−2) × (−3) = −12 + 6 = −6 ✓

Q4. Evaluate: (−2) × (−4) × (−6) × (−8) × 0

Show Solution
Any product that includes 0 = 0. No need to calculate anything else!
This is the zero property of multiplication: a × 0 = 0 for any integer a.

Q5. Find the product using suitable rearrangement: (a) (−25) × (−4) × (−8) × (−5)   (b) 125 × (−8) × (−2)

Show Solution
(a) Rearrange: [(−25) × (−4)] × [(−8) × (−5)] = 100 × 40 = 4000
Check: 4 negatives (even) → positive ✓
(b) Rearrange: 125 × [(−8) × (−2)] = 125 × 16 = 2000

Q6. What will be the sign of the product if we multiply: (a) 8 negative integers (b) 21 negative integers (c) 1 negative and 5 positive integers?

Show Solution
(a) 8 negatives: even count → positive
(b) 21 negatives: odd count → negative
(c) 1 negative: odd count of negatives → negative (positive integers don’t affect the sign)

Q7. State whether true or false: (a) (−1) × (−1) × (−1) × ... (500 times) = −1   (b) The product of a negative integer with itself is always positive

Show Solution
(a) False. 500 negatives = even count = positive. So (−1)500 = +1, not −1.
(b) True. (−a) × (−a) = +a². The number is multiplied with itself = 2 negatives (even) = always positive.
Exercise 1.4 — Division of Integers (7 Questions)

Q1. Evaluate: (a) (−36) ÷ (−4)   (b) (−201) ÷ 3   (c) 0 ÷ (−5)   (d) (−98) ÷ (−7)   (e) 52 ÷ (−4)

Show Solution
(a) Same signs → positive. 36 ÷ 4 = 9
(b) Different signs → negative. 201 ÷ 3 = −67
(c) 0 divided by anything = 0
(d) Same signs → positive. 98 ÷ 7 = 14
(e) Different signs → negative. 52 ÷ 4 = −13

Q2. Verify that a ÷ (b + c) ≠ (a ÷ b) + (a ÷ c) for a = 12, b = −4, c = 2

Show Solution
LHS: 12 ÷ ((−4) + 2) = 12 ÷ (−2) = −6
RHS: (12 ÷ (−4)) + (12 ÷ 2) = (−3) + 6 = 3
−6 ≠ 3. Division is NOT distributive over addition!

Q3. Verify that (a ÷ b) ÷ c ≠ a ÷ (b ÷ c) for a = 8, b = −4, c = 2

Show Solution
LHS: (8 ÷ (−4)) ÷ 2 = (−2) ÷ 2 = −1
RHS: 8 ÷ ((−4) ÷ 2) = 8 ÷ (−2) = −4
−1 ≠ −4. Division is NOT associative!

Q4. Fill in the blanks: (a) 72 ÷ (_) = −8   (b) (_) ÷ (−4) = 9   (c) (_) ÷ (−6) = −7   (d) (−54) ÷ (_) = −6

Show Solution
(a) 72 ÷ x = −8 → x = 72 ÷ (−8) = −9
(b) x ÷ (−4) = 9 → x = 9 × (−4) = −36
(c) x ÷ (−6) = −7 → x = (−7) × (−6) = 42
(d) (−54) ÷ x = −6 → x = (−54) ÷ (−6) = 9

Q5. An elevator descends 3 floors per minute from ground floor. (a) Where is it after 6 minutes? (b) How long to reach floor −24?

Show Solution
(a) After 6 min: 6 × (−3) = −18 (18 floors below ground, i.e., B18)
(b) (−24) ÷ (−3) = 8 minutes

Q6. The temperature at noon was 10°C. It drops 3°C every hour. (a) Temperature at midnight (12 hours later)? (b) At what time will it be −8°C?

Show Solution
(a) Drop in 12 hours = 12 × (−3) = −36. Temp = 10 + (−36) = −26°C
(b) Need to go from 10 to −8 = drop of 18°C. Time = 18 ÷ 3 = 6 hours after noon = 6:00 PM

Q7. State whether true or false with reason: (a) (−7) ÷ 0 = 0   (b) 0 ÷ (−7) = 0   (c) (−5) ÷ (−1) = 5   (d) (−8) ÷ 1 = −8

Show Solution
(a) False. Division by zero is undefined, not zero!
(b) True. 0 divided by any non-zero number = 0.
(c) True. Same signs → positive. 5 ÷ 1 = 5. So (−5) ÷ (−1) = 5 ✓
(d) True. Dividing by 1 gives the same number. (−8) ÷ 1 = −8 ✓

HOTS — Higher Order Thinking Skills Advanced

HardHOTS

1. The product of two integers is −36. If one of them is +4, find the other. Can you find another pair of integers whose product is −36?

Show Answer
(−36) ÷ 4 = −9. Other pairs: (−6, 6), (−12, 3), (−18, 2), (−1, 36), (9, −4), etc. Any pair with different signs whose product of absolute values = 36.
HardHOTS

2. Is it possible for the product of three negative integers to be positive? Why or why not?

Show Answer
No. Three negatives = odd count of negative signs = negative result. For the product to be positive, you need an even number of negative signs (0, 2, 4, ...).
HardHOTS

3. Ritu deposited Rs 5000 in a bank. She withdrew Rs 1200, then Rs 800, then deposited Rs 500, then withdrew Rs 2000. What is her final balance? Write this as a single integer expression.

Show Answer
Expression: 5000 + (−1200) + (−800) + 500 + (−2000)
= 5000 + 500 − 1200 − 800 − 2000
= 5500 − 4000 = Rs 1500
HardHOTS

4. Replace the blank with an integer: (−5) × ___ = 40. Is there more than one answer?

Show Answer
40 ÷ (−5) = −8. This is the only integer answer because division gives a unique result.

Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

  • Thinking (−) × (−) = negative
    (−3) × (−5) = −15
    (−3) × (−5) = +15
    Same signs = positive! Use the pattern table to remember.
  • Forgetting to flip when subtracting a negative
    5 − (−3) = 2
    5 − (−3) = 5 + 3 = 8
    Removing a debt = gaining!
  • Thinking −8 is greater than −3
    −8 > −3 (because 8 > 3)
    −8 < −3
    Think temperature: −8°C is colder!
  • Dividing by zero
    (−12) ÷ 0 = 0
    (−12) ÷ 0 is UNDEFINED
    You can never divide by zero.
  • Assuming subtraction is commutative
    5 − 3 = 3 − 5
    5 − 3 = 2 but 3 − 5 = −2
    Order matters in subtraction!
  • Forgetting BODMAS with integers
    3 + 2 × (−4) = 5 × (−4) = −20
    3 + 2 × (−4) = 3 + (−8) = −5
    Multiplication comes before addition!
