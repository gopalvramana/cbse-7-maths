Number Systems

Chapter 1: Integers

Summary

  • Integers include all positive numbers, zero, and negative numbers (e.g., … −3, −2, −1, 0, 1, 2, 3, …).
  • On a number line, numbers get smaller as you move left and bigger as you move right (e.g., −5 is to the left of −2, so −5 < −2).
  • Adding two positive numbers gives a positive result. Adding two negative numbers gives a negative result (e.g., (−3) + (−4) = −7).
  • Subtracting an integer is the same as adding its opposite (e.g., 5 − (−3) = 5 + 3 = 8).
  • Multiplying or dividing two numbers with the same sign gives a positive answer. Two numbers with different signs give a negative answer (e.g., (−4) × (−5) = +20, but (−4) × 5 = −20).
  • Commutative property: a + b = b + a and a × b = b × a (e.g., 3 + (−5) = (−5) + 3 = −2).
  • Associative property: (a + b) + c = a + (b + c) (e.g., (2 + 3) + 4 = 2 + (3 + 4) = 9).
  • Distributive property: a × (b + c) = a × b + a × c (e.g., 2 × (3 + 4) = 2 × 3 + 2 × 4 = 14).
  • Identity elements: 0 is the identity for addition (a + 0 = a) and 1 is the identity for multiplication (a × 1 = a) (e.g., −7 + 0 = −7 and −7 × 1 = −7).

Key Formulas

a - (-b) = a + b — Subtracting a negative is the same as adding.

(-a) × (-b) = ab — Negative times negative equals positive.

(-a) × b = -(ab) — Negative times positive equals negative.

a ÷ (-b) = -(a ÷ b) — Dividing by a negative gives a negative result.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Find (-3) × (-5) × 2

Step 1: Multiply the first two numbers: (-3) × (-5) = 15 (negative times negative = positive).
Step 2: Multiply the result by 2: 15 × 2 = 30.

Example 2: Simplify (-36) ÷ (-4)

Step 1: Both numbers are negative, so the answer will be positive.
Step 2: 36 ÷ 4 = 9.

Example 3: Evaluate (-8) + (-3) - (-5)

Step 1: Rewrite subtraction as addition of opposite: (-8) + (-3) + 5.
Step 2: Add the negatives: (-8) + (-3) = -11.
Step 3: Add the result to 5: -11 + 5 = -6.

Practice Problems

Easy

1. Find the value of (-7) + 3.

Show Answer

(-7) + 3 = -4. We move 3 steps to the right from -7 on the number line.

Easy

2. Find the value of (-4) × 5.

Show Answer

(-4) × 5 = -20. A negative times a positive gives a negative result.

Medium

3. Find the value of (-15) ÷ 3 + (-2) × 4.

Show Answer

First, (-15) ÷ 3 = -5. Then, (-2) × 4 = -8. Finally, -5 + (-8) = -13.

Medium

4. Arrange the following integers in ascending order: -3, 5, -8, 2.

Show Answer

From smallest to largest: -8, -3, 2, 5.

Hard

5. Find the value of (-2) × (-3) × (-4) × (-1).

Show Answer

(-2) × (-3) = 6. Then 6 × (-4) = -24. Then (-24) × (-1) = 24. With an even number of negative signs, the result is positive.

Hard

6. The temperature in a city was -8°C in the morning. It rose by 15°C during the day and then dropped by 6°C at night. What was the final temperature?

Show Answer

Start: -8°C. After rising 15°C: -8 + 15 = 7°C. After dropping 6°C: 7 - 6 = 1°C.

Easy

7. Find (-9) + 4.

Show Answer

(-9) + 4 = -5. We move 4 steps to the right from -9 on the number line.

Easy

8. What is (-6) × 1?

Show Answer

(-6) × 1 = -6. Multiplying any number by 1 gives the number itself (identity property).

Medium

9. Simplify: (-12) ÷ 4 + (-3) × 2.

Show Answer

First, (-12) ÷ 4 = -3. Then, (-3) × 2 = -6. Finally, -3 + (-6) = -9.

Medium

10. Find the product: (-5) × 3 × (-2).

Show Answer

(-5) × 3 = -15. Then (-15) × (-2) = 30. Two negative signs give a positive result.

Hard

11. The temperature at midnight was -5°C. By noon it rose by 12°C, then dropped 8°C by evening. What was the evening temperature?

Show Answer

Start: -5°C. After rising 12°C: -5 + 12 = 7°C. After dropping 8°C: 7 - 8 = -1°C.

Hard

12. Evaluate: [(-36) ÷ 6] × [(-8) + 3].

Show Answer

First bracket: (-36) ÷ 6 = -6. Second bracket: (-8) + 3 = -5. Now multiply: (-6) × (-5) = 30. Negative times negative equals positive.

Common Mistakes

  • Forgetting that negative × negative = positive. Many students write (-3) × (-5) = -15 instead of +15. Remember: same signs give a positive answer!
  • Confusing subtraction with adding the opposite. Always rewrite a - (-b) as a + b before solving. It makes things much easier.
  • Getting the wrong sign when dividing negatives. Check the signs first! Same signs = positive result, different signs = negative result.
