What is Congruence?

Real-world connection: Two coins of the same value are congruent — same shape and size. Two pages of the same textbook are congruent. Stamps printed from the same block are congruent. If you can place one on top of the other and they match perfectly, they’re congruent!
  • Congruent figures have exactly the same shape and same size.
  • If you cut out one figure and place it on the other, they overlap perfectly.
  • The symbol for congruence is ≅ (e.g., ▵ABC ≅ ▵DEF).
  • The order of letters matters! ▵ABC ≅ ▵DEF means: A↔D, B↔E, C↔F. So AB=DE, BC=EF, AC=DF, and ∠A=∠D, ∠B=∠E, ∠C=∠F.
Do we need to check ALL 6 parts (3 sides + 3 angles)?
No! That would be too much work. We only need to check 3 specific parts using one of the congruence criteria (SSS, SAS, ASA, or RHS). The rest follow automatically!

The 4 Congruence Criteria

1. SSS (Side-Side-Side)

If all 3 sides of one triangle are equal to all 3 sides of another, they are congruent.

Example: ▵ABC with sides 3, 4, 5 and ▵PQR with sides 3, 4, 5 → ▵ABC ≅ ▵PQR by SSS

2. SAS (Side-Angle-Side)

If 2 sides and the included angle (the angle BETWEEN those 2 sides) are equal, the triangles are congruent.

Example: AB=5, ∠B=60°, BC=7 in both triangles → Congruent by SAS
Critical: The angle MUST be between the two given sides! If the angle is not included, SAS doesn’t apply.

3. ASA (Angle-Side-Angle)

If 2 angles and the included side (the side BETWEEN those 2 angles) are equal, the triangles are congruent.

Example: ∠A=40°, AB=6cm, ∠B=80° in both triangles → Congruent by ASA

4. RHS (Right angle-Hypotenuse-Side)

If both triangles have a right angle, equal hypotenuse, and one equal side, they are congruent.

Example: Both have 90°, hypotenuse=10, one leg=6 → Congruent by RHS
Note: RHS only works for right triangles!
Why doesn’t AAA (Angle-Angle-Angle) work?
Because two triangles can have all the same angles but be different sizes! A small triangle with angles 60°, 60°, 60° and a big triangle with the same angles are similar (same shape) but NOT congruent (different size). AAA proves similarity, not congruence.

CPCT — Corresponding Parts of Congruent Triangles

Once you prove two triangles are congruent, ALL their corresponding parts are automatically equal. This is called CPCT.

Example: If ▵ABC ≅ ▵PQR, what can we conclude?

Sides: AB = PQ, BC = QR, AC = PR
Angles: ∠A = ∠P, ∠B = ∠Q, ∠C = ∠R
ALL 6 parts match! We only needed to prove 3 parts (using SSS/SAS/ASA/RHS), and the rest follow by CPCT.
Exam strategy: When a question says “Prove that AB = PQ” or “Show that ∠A = ∠D”, first prove the triangles congruent using a criterion, then state the result “by CPCT.”

NCERT Exercise Solutions — All Questions Solved

Exercise 7.1 — Identifying Congruence (10 Questions)

Q1. Complete: If ▵ABC ≅ ▵FED under the correspondence ABC ↔ FED, write all corresponding parts.

Show Solution
Step 1: Read the correspondence: A↔F, B↔E, C↔D
Step 2: Corresponding sides: AB=FE, BC=ED, AC=FD
Step 3: Corresponding angles: ∠A=∠F, ∠B=∠E, ∠C=∠D

Q2. If ▵DEF ≅ ▵BCA, write the part corresponding to: (a) ∠E (b) DF (c) ∠A (d) BC

Show Solution
Step 1: Correspondence: D↔B, E↔C, F↔A
(a) ∠E corresponds to ∠C
(b) DF corresponds to BA
(c) ∠A corresponds to ∠F
(d) BC corresponds to DE

Q3. Two triangles have sides 5, 6, 7 cm and 5, 6, 7 cm. Are they congruent? Which criterion?

Show Solution
Step 1: Compare all three pairs of sides:
Step 2: Side 1: 5 = 5 ✓ Side 2: 6 = 6 ✓ Side 3: 7 = 7 ✓
Step 3: All three sides match.
Answer: Yes, congruent by SSS (Side-Side-Side)

Q4. In ▵ABC and ▵DEF: AB=DE=5cm, ∠B=∠E=50°, BC=EF=7cm. Are they congruent? By which rule?

Show Solution
Step 1: We have two sides and an angle. Is the angle INCLUDED (between the two sides)?
Step 2: AB and BC meet at B. ∠B is at vertex B, which IS between sides AB and BC. ✓
Step 3: AB=DE, ∠B=∠E, BC=EF (Side-Angle-Side)
Answer: ▵ABC ≅ ▵DEF by SAS

Q5. ▵ABC: ∠A=40°, AB=5cm, ∠B=60°. ▵DEF: ∠D=40°, DE=5cm, ∠E=60°. Congruent?

Show Solution
Step 1: We have two angles and a side. Is the side INCLUDED (between the two angles)?
Step 2: ∠A and ∠B are at the ends of side AB. So AB is the included side. ✓
Step 3: ∠A=∠D=40°, AB=DE=5cm, ∠B=∠E=60° (Angle-Side-Angle)
Answer: ▵ABC ≅ ▵DEF by ASA

Q6. In ▵PQR and ▵STU: ∠Q=∠T=90°, PR=SU=10cm, PQ=ST=6cm. Congruent?

Show Solution
Step 1: Both have a right angle (∠Q=∠T=90°). ✓
Step 2: PR and SU are the sides opposite the right angles = hypotenuses. PR=SU=10cm. ✓
Step 3: One more side: PQ=ST=6cm. ✓
Answer: ▵PQR ≅ ▵STU by RHS (Right angle-Hypotenuse-Side)

Q7. Two triangles have angles 50°, 60°, 70° each. Are they definitely congruent?

Show Solution
Step 1: All three angles match: 50°=50°, 60°=60°, 70°=70°.
Step 2: But this is AAA (Angle-Angle-Angle).
Step 3: AAA only proves similarity, NOT congruence. The triangles could be different sizes!
Answer: NOT necessarily congruent. They are similar but may have different side lengths.

Q8. ▵ABC ≅ ▵PQR. If AB=6cm, BC=8cm, ∠B=90°, find PQ, QR, and ∠Q.

Show Solution
Step 1: Correspondence: A↔P, B↔Q, C↔R
Step 2: By CPCT: PQ = AB = 6 cm
Step 3: By CPCT: QR = BC = 8 cm
Step 4: By CPCT: ∠Q = ∠B = 90°

Q9. If ▵ABC ≅ ▵DEF, what angle corresponds to ∠C?

Show Solution
Step 1: Read the correspondence: A↔D, B↔E, C↔F
Step 2: C corresponds to F.
Answer: ∠C corresponds to ∠F

Q10. ▵XYZ ≅ ▵LMN. If XY=5cm, YZ=7cm, ∠Y=60°. Find LM, MN, ∠M.

Show Solution
Step 1: Correspondence: X↔L, Y↔M, Z↔N
Step 2: By CPCT: LM = XY = 5 cm
Step 3: By CPCT: MN = YZ = 7 cm
Step 4: By CPCT: ∠M = ∠Y = 60°
Exercise 7.2 — Proving Congruence (8 Questions)

Q1. In isosceles ▵ABC with AB=AC, line from A to midpoint M of BC. Prove ▵ABM ≅ ▵ACM.

Show Solution
Step 1: List what we know:
• AB = AC (given, isosceles)
• BM = CM (M is midpoint of BC)
• AM = AM (common side)
Step 2: We have 3 pairs of equal sides.
Step 3: ▵ABM ≅ ▵ACM by SSS
Bonus: By CPCT, ∠AMB = ∠AMC. Since they form a linear pair, each = 90°. So AM is perpendicular to BC!

Q2. In quadrilateral ABCD, AB=CD and AD=BC. Prove ▵ABD ≅ ▵CDB.

Show Solution
Step 1: In ▵ABD and ▵CDB:
Step 2: AB = CD (given) ✓
Step 3: AD = BC (given) ✓
Step 4: BD = DB (common side) ✓
Step 5: 3 sides equal → ▵ABD ≅ ▵CDB by SSS

Q3. Two right triangles have hypotenuse 15 cm each and one leg 9 cm each. Are they congruent? Find the other leg.

Show Solution
Step 1: Both have right angle (90°). ✓
Step 2: Hypotenuse: 15 = 15. ✓
Step 3: One leg: 9 = 9. ✓
Step 4: Yes, congruent by RHS.
Step 5: Find other leg using Pythagoras:
a² + 9² = 15²
a² + 81 = 225
a² = 144
a = √144 = 12 cm

Q4. AB is a line segment. P and Q are points on opposite sides such that AP=AQ and BP=BQ. Prove ▵APB ≅ ▵AQB.

Show Solution
Step 1: In ▵APB and ▵AQB:
Step 2: AP = AQ (given) ✓
Step 3: BP = BQ (given) ✓
Step 4: AB = AB (common side) ✓
Step 5: ▵APB ≅ ▵AQB by SSS

Q5. In ▵ABC and ▵PQR: ∠A=∠R, ∠B=∠P, and AB=RP. Which congruence rule? Write the correspondence.

Show Solution
Step 1: Match the equal parts: ∠A=∠R, ∠B=∠P, AB=RP
Step 2: The side AB is between angles A and B. The side RP is between angles R and P.
Step 3: Two angles and the included side match → ASA
Step 4: Correspondence: A↔R, B↔P, C↔Q
Answer: ▵ABC ≅ ▵RPQ by ASA

Q6. In right triangles ABC and DEF: ∠B=∠E=90°, AC=DF, AB=DE. Prove ▵ABC ≅ ▵DEF.

Show Solution
Step 1: ∠B = ∠E = 90° (both right angles) ✓
Step 2: AC = DF (these are the hypotenuses, opposite the right angles) ✓
Step 3: AB = DE (one pair of legs) ✓
Step 4: Right angle + hypotenuse + one side = RHS
▵ABC ≅ ▵DEF by RHS

Q7. In ▵ABC, ∠A=50°, AB=5cm. In ▵DEF, ∠D=50°, DE=5cm, ∠E=80°. Can you conclude ▵ABC ≅ ▵DEF?

Show Solution
Step 1: For ▵ABC we know: ∠A=50°, AB=5cm. That’s only 2 pieces of information.
Step 2: We don’t know ∠B or any other side of ▵ABC.
Step 3: We need at least 3 matching parts (from SSS, SAS, ASA, or RHS).
Answer: No, we cannot conclude congruence. Insufficient information about ▵ABC.

Q8. Prove that in an equilateral triangle, the median to any side also bisects the angle at the vertex.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let ▵ABC be equilateral with AB=BC=CA. Let M be midpoint of BC.
Step 2: In ▵ABM and ▵ACM:
Step 3: AB = AC (equilateral triangle) ✓
Step 4: BM = CM (M is midpoint) ✓
Step 5: AM = AM (common side) ✓
Step 6: ▵ABM ≅ ▵ACM by SSS
Step 7: By CPCT: ∠BAM = ∠CAM. This means AM bisects ∠A. Proved!

Show Answer

Step 2: In ▵ABM and ▵ACM: AB=AC (given), BM=CM (midpoint), AM=AM (common)
Step 3: ▵ABM ≅ ▵ACM by SSS
Step 4: By CPCT: ∠B = ∠C. Proved!
Hard

8. Two right triangles have hypotenuse 15 cm and one leg 9 cm each. Are they congruent? Find the other leg.

Show Answer
Step 1: Right angle ✓, Hypotenuse 15=15 ✓, Leg 9=9 ✓
Step 2: Yes, congruent by RHS
Step 3: Other leg: a² + 81 = 225 → a² = 144 → a = 12 cm
Hard

9. ABCD is a square. Prove ▵ABC ≅ ▵ADC.

Show Answer
Step 1: In a square: AB=AD (sides), BC=DC (sides), ∠B=∠D=90°
Step 2: Also AC=AC (common diagonal)
Step 3: AB=AD, BC=DC, AC=AC → ▵ABC ≅ ▵ADC by SSS
(Or: ∠B=∠D=90°, AC=AC (hyp), AB=AD → RHS also works!)
Hard

10. In ▵ABC, AB=AC. D is a point on BC such that BD=DC. Prove AD⊥BC.

Show Answer
Step 1: In ▵ABD and ▵ACD: AB=AC (given), BD=DC (given), AD=AD (common)
Step 2: ▵ABD ≅ ▵ACD by SSS
Step 3: By CPCT: ∠ADB = ∠ADC
Step 4: But ∠ADB + ∠ADC = 180° (linear pair)
Step 5: So 2∠ADB = 180° → ∠ADB = 90°
Step 6: AD ⊥ BC. Proved!

HOTS — Higher Order Thinking Advanced

HardHOTS

1. Is SSA (Side-Side-Angle) a valid congruence criterion? Why or why not?

Show Answer
No! SSA is NOT a valid criterion. Two different triangles can have the same SSA but be non-congruent. This is called the “ambiguous case.” Only SSS, SAS, ASA, and RHS are valid.
HardHOTS

2. In ▵ABC, AB=AC and ∠A=80°. In ▵DEF, DE=DF and ∠D=80°. Is ▵ABC necessarily congruent to ▵DEF?

Show Answer
Not necessarily! We know AB=AC and DE=DF (both isosceles) and ∠A=∠D=80°. But we don’t know if AB=DE. The triangles could be different sizes with the same shape. We need at least one pair of sides to be equal to prove congruence.
HardHOTS

3. ABCD is a rectangle. Prove that the two diagonals are equal (AC = BD).

Show Answer
Step 1: Consider ▵ABC and ▵DCB.
Step 2: AB = DC (opposite sides of rectangle are equal)
Step 3: ∠ABC = ∠DCB = 90° (all angles in rectangle are 90°)
Step 4: BC = CB (common side)
Step 5: ▵ABC ≅ ▵DCB by SAS
Step 6: By CPCT: AC = DB. Diagonals are equal!

Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

  • For SAS, the angle must be BETWEEN the two sides
    AB=DE, BC=EF, ∠C=∠F → SAS
    The angle must be the included angle. ∠C is NOT between AB and BC. This is SSA, which is NOT valid!
  • Writing correspondence in wrong order
    ▵ABC ≅ ▵PQR means A↔Q
    ▵ABC ≅ ▵PQR means A↔P, B↔Q, C↔R. The order of letters defines the correspondence!
  • Using AAA to prove congruence
    All 3 angles match → congruent!
    AAA only proves similarity. Two triangles can have equal angles but different sizes. You need at least one side!
  • Forgetting to state the congruence rule
    “The triangles are congruent.” (no rule stated)
    “▵ABC ≅ ▵DEF by SSS.” Always state which criterion!
  • Applying RHS to non-right triangles
    Hypotenuse + side match → RHS
    RHS requires a right angle in BOTH triangles. Without 90°, there’s no hypotenuse!
