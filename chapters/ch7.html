Congruence of Triangles

Summary

  • Two figures are congruent if they have exactly the same shape and size (e.g., two coins of the same denomination are congruent).
  • Congruent triangles match perfectly when placed on top of each other.
  • The order of letters matters! ▵ABC ≅ ▵DEF means A↔D, B↔E, C↔F (e.g., ▵ABC ≅ ▵DEF means AB = DE, BC = EF, AC = DF).
  • SSS (Side-Side-Side): All 3 sides of one triangle equal all 3 sides of the other (e.g., triangles with sides 3, 4, 5 and 3, 4, 5 are congruent).
  • SAS (Side-Angle-Side): 2 sides and the angle between them are equal (e.g., two sides 5 cm, 7 cm with a 60° angle between them).
  • ASA (Angle-Side-Angle): 2 angles and the side between them are equal.
  • RHS (Right angle-Hypotenuse-Side): Right angle, hypotenuse, and one side are equal.
  • CPCT = Corresponding Parts of Congruent Triangles (are equal). Once you prove triangles are congruent, all their matching parts are equal.

Key Formulas / Criteria

SSS: If all 3 sides match → triangles are congruent
SAS: If 2 sides + included angle match → triangles are congruent
ASA: If 2 angles + included side match → triangles are congruent
RHS: If right angle + hypotenuse + one side match → triangles are congruent

Worked Examples

Example 1: SSS Congruence

▵ABC has sides 5, 7, 9 cm. ▵PQR has sides 5, 7, 9 cm. Are they congruent?

Step 1: Compare sides: AB=PQ=5, BC=QR=7, AC=PR=9
Step 2: All three pairs of sides are equal → ▵ABC ≅ ▵PQR by SSS

Example 2: SAS Congruence

▵ABC: AB = 4 cm, ∠B = 50°, BC = 6 cm. ▵DEF: DE = 4 cm, ∠E = 50°, EF = 6 cm.

Step 1: AB = DE = 4 cm ✓
Step 2: ∠B = ∠E = 50° (this is the included angle) ✓
Step 3: BC = EF = 6 cm ✓
Answer: ▵ABC ≅ ▵DEF by SAS

Example 3: ASA Congruence

▵ABC: ∠A = 40°, AB = 5 cm, ∠B = 60°. ▵DEF: ∠D = 40°, DE = 5 cm, ∠E = 60°.

Step 1: ∠A = ∠D = 40° ✓
Step 2: AB = DE = 5 cm (included side between the two angles) ✓
Step 3: ∠B = ∠E = 60° ✓
Answer: ▵ABC ≅ ▵DEF by ASA

Practice Problems

Easy

Two triangles have sides 3, 4, 5 cm and 3, 4, 5 cm. Which congruence rule applies?

Show Answer
SSS — all three pairs of sides are equal.
Easy

If ▵ABC ≅ ▵XYZ, what is the side corresponding to BC?

Show Answer
B↔Y and C↔Z, so BC corresponds to YZ.
Medium

In ▵PQR and ▵STU: PQ = ST, ∠Q = ∠T = 90°, PR = SU. Which rule makes them congruent?

Show Answer
RHS — right angle at Q and T, hypotenuse PR = SU, side PQ = ST.
Medium

▵ABC: ∠A = 55°, AC = 7 cm, ∠C = 65°. ▵DEF: ∠D = 55°, DF = 7 cm, ∠F = 65°. Are they congruent? By which rule?

Show Answer
Yes — ASA. ∠A = ∠D, AC = DF (included side), ∠C = ∠F.
Hard

Can two triangles with all three angles equal (AAA) be called congruent? Why or why not?

Show Answer
No. AAA only proves the triangles are similar (same shape), not congruent. They could be different sizes — like a small and big version of the same triangle.
Hard

▵ABC ≅ ▵PQR. If AB = 6 cm, BC = 8 cm, ∠B = 90°, find PQ and QR.

Show Answer
By CPCT: PQ = AB = 6 cm, QR = BC = 8 cm, and ∠Q = 90°.
Easy

If ▵ABC ≅ ▵DEF, what angle corresponds to ∠C?

Show Answer
Since A↔D, B↔E, C↔F, the angle corresponding to ∠C is ∠F.
Easy

Two triangles have sides 6, 8, 10 and 6, 8, 10. Are they congruent? Which rule?

Show Answer
Yes, they are congruent by SSS — all three pairs of corresponding sides are equal.
Medium

In ▵ABC and ▵PQR, AB = PQ, ∠A = ∠P = 90°, and BC = QR. Which congruence rule applies?

Show Answer
RHS (Right angle – Hypotenuse – Side). Both triangles have a right angle (∠A = ∠P = 90°), equal hypotenuses (BC = QR), and one equal side (AB = PQ).
Medium

▵XYZ ≅ ▵LMN. If XY = 5 cm, YZ = 7 cm, and ∠Y = 60°, find LM, MN, and ∠M.

Show Answer
By CPCT (X↔L, Y↔M, Z↔N): LM = XY = 5 cm, MN = YZ = 7 cm, and ∠M = ∠Y = 60°.
Hard

In an isosceles triangle ABC with AB = AC, a line from A to the midpoint M of BC is drawn. Prove that ▵ABM ≅ ▵ACM. Which rule?

Show Answer
In ▵ABM and ▵ACM: (1) AB = AC (given), (2) BM = CM (M is the midpoint of BC), (3) AM = AM (common side). Therefore ▵ABM ≅ ▵ACM by SSS.
Hard

Two right triangles have hypotenuse 15 cm each. One leg of each is 9 cm. Are they congruent? Find the other leg.

Show Answer
Yes, they are congruent by RHS (right angle, hypotenuse = 15 cm, one side = 9 cm). The other leg: a² + 9² = 15² → a² + 81 = 225 → a² = 144 → a = 12 cm.

Common Mistakes

  • For SAS, the angle must be BETWEEN the two sides — not just any angle!
  • Writing the correspondence in the wrong order (e.g., ▵ABC ≅ ▵PQR means A↔P, not A↔Q).
  • Using AAA to prove congruence — AAA only proves similarity, not congruence.
  • Forgetting to state which congruence rule (SSS, SAS, ASA, or RHS) you are using.
