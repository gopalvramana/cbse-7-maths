Perimeter and Area

Summary

  • Perimeter = total distance around a shape (e.g., perimeter of a 5 cm × 3 cm rectangle = 2(5+3) = 16 cm). Area = space inside a shape (e.g., area of that rectangle = 5 × 3 = 15 cm²).
  • A parallelogram's area = base × height (height is perpendicular to the base) — e.g., base 8 cm, height 5 cm → area = 40 cm².
  • A triangle's area = ½ × base × height — e.g., base 10 cm, height 6 cm → area = 30 cm².
  • A circle's area = πr² (where r is the radius, π ≈ 22/7 or 3.14) — e.g., radius 7 cm → area = 22/7 × 7 × 7 = 154 cm².
  • Circumference (perimeter of a circle) = 2πr — e.g., radius 7 cm → circumference = 2 × 22/7 × 7 = 44 cm.
  • Converting units: 1 m² = 10,000 cm², 1 km² = 1,000,000 m² (e.g., a 2 m × 3 m room = 6 m² = 60,000 cm²).
  • To find the area of complex shapes, break them into simpler shapes and add up (e.g., an L-shaped room can be split into two rectangles).

Key Formulas

Rectangle: Area = length × breadth, Perimeter = 2(l + b)
Square: Area = side², Perimeter = 4 × side
Parallelogram: Area = base × height
Triangle: Area = ½ × base × height
Circle: Area = πr², Circumference = 2πr

Worked Examples

Example 1: Area of a Parallelogram

A parallelogram has base 12 cm and height 8 cm. Find its area.

Step 1: Area = base × height
Step 2: Area = 12 × 8 = 96 cm²

Example 2: Area of a Triangle

A triangle has base 10 cm and height 6 cm. Find its area.

Step 1: Area = ½ × base × height
Step 2: Area = ½ × 10 × 6 = 30 cm²

Example 3: Circumference and Area of a Circle

A circle has radius 7 cm. Find its circumference and area. (Use π = 22/7)

Step 1: Circumference = 2πr = 2 × 22/7 × 7 = 44 cm
Step 2: Area = πr² = 22/7 × 7 × 7 = 154 cm²

Practice Problems

Easy

Find the area of a rectangle with length 15 cm and breadth 8 cm.

Show Answer
Area = 15 × 8 = 120 cm²
Easy

Find the perimeter of a square with side 9 cm.

Show Answer
Perimeter = 4 × 9 = 36 cm
Medium

A parallelogram has base 15 cm and height 10 cm. A triangle has the same base and height. Find both areas.

Show Answer
Parallelogram = 15 × 10 = 150 cm². Triangle = ½ × 15 × 10 = 75 cm². (Triangle is exactly half!)
Medium

Find the circumference of a circle with diameter 14 cm. (Use π = 22/7)

Show Answer
Radius = 14/2 = 7 cm. Circumference = 2 × 22/7 × 7 = 44 cm
Hard

A circular garden has radius 21 m. Find the cost of fencing it at Rs 10 per metre. (Use π = 22/7)

Show Answer
Circumference = 2 × 22/7 × 21 = 132 m. Cost = 132 × 10 = Rs 1320
Hard

A rectangular field is 50 m long and 30 m wide. A path 2 m wide runs inside along the boundary. Find the area of the path.

Show Answer
Outer area = 50 × 30 = 1500 m². Inner dimensions = 46 × 26. Inner area = 1196 m². Path area = 1500 − 1196 = 304 m²
Easy

Find the area of a triangle with base 14 cm and height 10 cm.

Show Answer
Area = ½ × 14 × 10 = 70 cm²
Easy

Find the area of a square with side 11 cm.

Show Answer
Area = 11 × 11 = 121 cm²
Medium

A parallelogram has base 20 cm and area 300 cm². Find the height.

Show Answer
Area = base × height, so height = Area ÷ base = 300 ÷ 20 = 15 cm
Medium

Find the area of a circle with radius 14 cm. (Use π = 22/7)

Show Answer
Area = πr² = 22/7 × 14 × 14 = 22/7 × 196 = 616 cm²
Hard

A rectangular park 60 m × 40 m has two crossing paths, each 3 m wide (one lengthwise, one widthwise). Find the area of the paths.

Show Answer
Area of lengthwise path = 60 × 3 = 180 m². Area of widthwise path = 40 × 3 = 120 m². Overlapping square = 3 × 3 = 9 m². Total path area = 180 + 120 − 9 = 291 m²
Hard

The circumference of a circle is 88 cm. Find its radius and area. (Use π = 22/7)

Show Answer
Circumference = 2πr, so 88 = 2 × 22/7 × r, giving r = 88 × 7 / 44 = 14 cm. Area = πr² = 22/7 × 14 × 14 = 616 cm²

Common Mistakes

  • Using the slant side instead of the perpendicular height for parallelogram/triangle area.
  • Confusing radius and diameter — diameter = 2 × radius!
  • Forgetting to write the correct units (cm² for area, cm for perimeter).
  • Using the wrong value of π — use 22/7 when the radius is a multiple of 7, otherwise use 3.14.
