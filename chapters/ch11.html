Rectangle and Square

Real-world: Your classroom floor, a book cover, a phone screen — all rectangles! Knowing area helps you figure out how much carpet to buy, how much paint you need, or how many tiles to order.

Example: Find the area and perimeter of a rectangle 15 cm long and 8 cm wide.

Step 1: Write what we know: length (l) = 15 cm, breadth (b) = 8 cm.
Step 2: Area formula: A = l × b
A = 15 × 8 = 120 cm²
(We write cm² because area is measured in “square centimetres”)
Step 3: Perimeter formula: P = 2 × (l + b)
First add: l + b = 15 + 8 = 23
Then multiply: P = 2 × 23 = 46 cm
(Perimeter is in cm, not cm², because it’s a length, not an area)

Parallelogram

WHY is area = base × height?
Imagine cutting a right triangle from one side of the parallelogram and moving it to the other side. It becomes a rectangle! And a rectangle’s area = length × breadth = base × height.

Circle

When to use 22/7 vs 3.14:
• If radius is a multiple of 7 (7, 14, 21...), use π = 22/7 — the 7s cancel nicely!
• Otherwise, use π = 3.14.

Example: Find the area and circumference of a circle with radius 7 cm.

Step 1: r = 7 cm. Since 7 is a multiple of 7, use π = 22/7.
Step 2: Circumference = 2πr
= 2 × 22/7 × 7
= 2 × 22 × 7/7 (the 7s cancel: 7÷7 = 1)
= 2 × 22 × 1
= 44 cm
Step 3: Area = πr² = π × r × r
= 22/7 × 7 × 7
= 22 × 7 × 7/7 (one 7 cancels)
= 22 × 7
= 154 cm²

NCERT Exercise Solutions — All Questions Solved

Exercise 11.1 — Parallelogram & Triangle Area (8 Questions)

Q1. Find the area of a parallelogram with base 12 cm and height 8 cm.

Show Solution
Step 1: Write what we know: base (b) = 12 cm, height (h) = 8 cm.
Step 2: Formula: Area = base × height
Step 3: Substitute: Area = 12 × 8
Step 4: Calculate: 12 × 8 = 96 cm²

Q2. Find the area of a triangle with base 10 cm and height 6 cm.

Show Solution
Step 1: Write what we know: base (b) = 10 cm, height (h) = 6 cm.
Step 2: Formula: Area = ½ × base × height
Step 3: Substitute: Area = ½ × 10 × 6
Step 4: Calculate step by step:
First: 10 × 6 = 60
Then: ½ × 60 = 60 ÷ 2 = 30 cm²

Q3. A parallelogram has area 300 cm² and base 20 cm. Find the height.

Show Solution
Step 1: We know: Area = 300 cm², base = 20 cm. Find: height = ?
Step 2: Formula: Area = base × height. We need to rearrange to find height.
Step 3: height = Area ÷ base (divide both sides by base)
Step 4: height = 300 ÷ 20
Long division: 300 ÷ 20 = 15
(Think: 20 × 15 = 300 ✓)
Answer: height = 15 cm

Q4. Find the area of a triangle with base 14 cm and height 10 cm.

Show Solution
Step 1: base = 14 cm, height = 10 cm
Step 2: Area = ½ × base × height
Step 3: = ½ × 14 × 10
Step 4: First: 14 × 10 = 140
Then: 140 ÷ 2 = 70 cm²

Q5. A rectangular garden is 50 m long and 30 m wide. A path 2 m wide runs inside along the boundary. Find the area of the path.

Show Solution
Step 1: Outer dimensions: length = 50 m, width = 30 m.
Step 2: Outer area = 50 × 30 = 1500 m²
Step 3: The path is 2 m wide on ALL sides. So the inner rectangle is smaller by 2 m on each side:
Inner length = 50 − 2 − 2 = 50 − 4 = 46 m
Inner width = 30 − 2 − 2 = 30 − 4 = 26 m
Step 4: Inner area = 46 × 26
Calculate: 46 × 26 = 46 × 20 + 46 × 6 = 920 + 276 = 1196 m²
Step 5: Path area = Outer area − Inner area
= 1500 − 1196 = 304 m²

Q6. Find the circumference of a circle with diameter 14 cm.

Show Solution
Step 1: Diameter = 14 cm. Radius = diameter ÷ 2 = 14 ÷ 2 = 7 cm.
Step 2: Since radius = 7 (multiple of 7), use π = 22/7.
Step 3: Circumference = 2πr = 2 × 22/7 × 7
Step 4: The 7 in the numerator and 7 in the denominator cancel: 7 ÷ 7 = 1.
= 2 × 22 × 1 = 44 cm

Q7. Find the area of a circle with radius 14 cm. (Use π = 22/7)

Show Solution
Step 1: r = 14 cm, π = 22/7
Step 2: Area = πr² = π × r × r
Step 3: = 22/7 × 14 × 14
Step 4: Simplify: 14 ÷ 7 = 2. So 22/7 × 14 = 22 × 2 = 44.
Step 5: Now: 44 × 14 = 44 × 10 + 44 × 4 = 440 + 176 = 616 cm²

Q8. The circumference of a circle is 88 cm. Find its radius and area.

Show Solution
Step 1: C = 88 cm. Formula: C = 2πr. We need to find r.
Step 2: 88 = 2 × 22/7 × r
Step 3: Simplify left side: 2 × 22/7 = 44/7. So: 88 = 44/7 × r
Step 4: Solve for r: r = 88 ÷ (44/7) = 88 × 7/44
= (88 × 7) / 44
= 88/44 × 7 (divide 88 by 44 first: 88 ÷ 44 = 2)
= 2 × 7 = 14 cm
Step 5: Now find area: A = πr² = 22/7 × 14 × 14 = 616 cm² (same as Q7)

2. Perimeter of square with side 9cm.

Show Answer
Step 1: Perimeter = 4 × side = 4 × 9
Step 2: 4 × 9 = 36 cm
Medium

3. Area of parallelogram: base 15cm, height 10cm. Area of triangle with same base and height.

Show Answer
Parallelogram: A = 15 × 10 = 150 cm²
Triangle: A = ½ × 15 × 10 = 150 ÷ 2 = 75 cm²
Notice: Triangle is exactly half the parallelogram!
Medium

4. Circumference of circle with diameter 14cm.

Show Answer
Step 1: d = 14, so r = 14 ÷ 2 = 7 cm
Step 2: C = 2πr = 2 × 22/7 × 7
Step 3: 7 ÷ 7 = 1. So: 2 × 22 = 44 cm
Hard

5. Circular garden radius 21m. Cost of fencing at Rs 10/m.

Show Answer
Step 1: Fencing = circumference = 2πr
Step 2: = 2 × 22/7 × 21
Step 3: 21 ÷ 7 = 3. So: 2 × 22 × 3 = 132 m
Step 4: Cost = 132 × 10 = Rs 1320
Hard

6. Rectangular field 50m × 30m. Two crossing paths 3m wide. Find area of paths.

Show Answer
Step 1: Lengthwise path: 50 × 3 = 150 m²
Step 2: Widthwise path: 30 × 3 = 90 m²
Step 3: But they overlap at center! Overlap = 3 × 3 = 9 m²
Step 4: Total = 150 + 90 − 9 = 231 m²

HOTS Advanced

HardHOTS

1. A wire 88 cm long is bent into a circle. Find the radius and area of the circle.

Show Answer
Step 1: The wire becomes the circumference. C = 88 cm.
Step 2: C = 2πr → 88 = 2 × 22/7 × r
Step 3: r = 88 × 7 / 44 = 2 × 7 = 14 cm
Step 4: Area = πr² = 22/7 × 14 × 14 = 616 cm²
HardHOTS

2. If the radius of a circle is doubled, what happens to the area?

Show Answer
Step 1: Original area = πr²
Step 2: New radius = 2r. New area = π(2r)² = π × 4r² = 4πr²
Step 3: New area = 4 × original area.
Answer: Area becomes 4 times! (Doubling radius quadruples area.)
HardHOTS

3. A rectangular field 80m × 60m. A horse is tied to a corner with a 14m rope. Find the grazing area.

Show Answer
Step 1: The horse can graze in a quarter-circle (90° corner).
Step 2: Quarter-circle area = ¼ × πr²
Step 3: = ¼ × 22/7 × 14 × 14
Step 4: = ¼ × 22 × 2 × 14 = ¼ × 616 = 154 m²

Common Mistakes

  • Using slant side instead of height for parallelogram
    Area = base × slant side
    Area = base × perpendicular height (the dashed line, not the slant!)
  • Confusing radius and diameter
    Diameter = 14, using r = 14 in formula
    Diameter = 14, so r = 14 ÷ 2 = 7. Always check which one is given!
  • Wrong units for area vs perimeter
    Area = 120 cm, Perimeter = 46 cm²
    Area = 120 cm² (squared!), Perimeter = 46 cm (not squared)
  • Forgetting to subtract overlap in crossing paths
    Two paths: just add both areas
    Paths overlap at the intersection. Subtract the overlapping square once!
