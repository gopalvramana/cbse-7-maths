What are Rational Numbers?

Real-world: You eat 3/4 of a pizza. The temperature is −2.5°C (= −5/2). You owe Rs 1.50 (= −3/2). All of these are rational numbers!
  • A rational number is any number that can be written as p/q where q ≠ 0.
  • Every integer is rational: 5 = 5/1, −3 = −3/1, 0 = 0/1.
  • Every fraction is rational: 3/4, −2/5, 7/3.
  • Positive rationals: p and q have same sign (e.g., 3/4 or −2/−5 = 2/5).
  • Negative rationals: p and q have different signs (e.g., −3/4 or 5/−6).
  • Standard form: denominator is positive, no common factors (e.g., −4/6 → −2/3).
Why can’t q = 0?
Division by zero is undefined. There’s no number that when multiplied by 0 gives you something other than 0. So 5/0 doesn’t exist — it’s NOT a rational number!

Rational Numbers on the Number Line

Just like integers, rational numbers can be placed on a number line. But now we can also place fractions between the integers!

Finding rationals between two numbers: To find rationals between 1/4 and 1/2, convert to equivalent fractions with bigger denominator: 1/4 = 4/16, 1/2 = 8/16. Numbers between: 5/16, 6/16, 7/16. You can always find infinitely many rationals between any two rationals!

Operations on Rational Numbers

Addition & Subtraction

Same denominator: a/b + c/b = (a+c)/b
Different denominators: Find LCM, convert, then add/subtract.

Example: Add −1/3 + 3/4

Step 1: LCM of 3 and 4 = 12
Step 2: Convert: −1/3 = −4/12 and 3/4 = 9/12
Step 3: Add: −4/12 + 9/12 = (−4 + 9)/12 = 5/12

Multiplication & Division

Multiply: a/b × c/d = ac/bd (tops × tops, bottoms × bottoms)
Divide: a/b ÷ c/d = a/b × d/c (flip second, then multiply)

Exercise 9.1 — Rational Numbers Basics (10 Questions)

Q1. List five rational numbers between −1 and 0.

Show Solution
Step 1: −1 = −6/6 and 0 = 0/6. Use denominator 6.
Step 2: Numbers between: −5/6, −4/6, −3/6, −2/6, −1/6
Step 3: Simplify: −5/6, −2/3, −1/2, −1/3, −1/6

Q2. Write 7 as a rational number with denominator (a) 1 (b) −1 (c) 5

Show Solution
(a) 7 = 7/1
(b) 7 = −7/(−1) = −7/−1 (but standard form: 7/1)
(c) 7 = (7×5)/(1×5) = 35/5

Q3. Is 0 a rational number? Can it be written as p/0?

Show Solution
Step 1: 0 = 0/1 (p=0, q=1, q≠0). Yes, 0 is rational.
Step 2: 0/0 is undefined (q cannot be 0). No, cannot write as p/0.

Q4. Write −6/8 in standard form.

Show Solution
Step 1: Check: denominator is positive (8 > 0). ✓
Step 2: Find HCF of 6 and 8 = 2
Step 3: Divide both by 2: −6/8 = −3/4

Q5. Find equivalent of 2/3 with denominator 15.

Show Solution
Step 1: Need denominator 15. Current = 3. Multiply by 5.
Step 2: (2×5)/(3×5) = 10/15

Q6. Arrange in ascending order: −2/3, 1/2, −1/6, 3/4

Show Solution
Step 1: Find LCM of denominators 3, 2, 6, 4 = 12
Step 2: Convert all to /12:
−2/3 = −8/12
1/2 = 6/12
−1/6 = −2/12
3/4 = 9/12
Step 3: Arrange numerators: −8 < −2 < 6 < 9
Answer: −2/3, −1/6, 1/2, 3/4

Q7. Add: −3/7 + 5/14

Show Solution
Step 1: LCM of 7 and 14 = 14
Step 2: Convert: −3/7 = −6/14
Step 3: Add: −6/14 + 5/14 = (−6+5)/14 = −1/14

Q8. Subtract: 3/5 − 1/3

Show Solution
Step 1: LCM of 5 and 3 = 15
Step 2: Convert: 3/5 = 9/15 and 1/3 = 5/15
Step 3: Subtract: 9/15 − 5/15 = 4/15

Q9. Find: −2/3 × 9/4

Show Solution
Step 1: Multiply tops: −2 × 9 = −18
Step 2: Multiply bottoms: 3 × 4 = 12
Step 3: = −18/12
Step 4: Simplify (both ÷ 6): −3/2

Q10. Divide: 5/6 ÷ (−2/3)

Show Solution
Step 1: Flip the second fraction: reciprocal of −2/3 = −3/2
Step 2: Multiply: 5/6 × (−3/2)
Step 3: = (5 × −3)/(6 × 2) = −15/12
Step 4: Simplify (both ÷ 3): −5/4
Exercise 9.2 — Properties & Word Problems (8 Questions)

Q1. Verify: −1/3 + 2/5 = 2/5 + (−1/3). Which property?

Show Solution
LHS: −1/3 + 2/5. LCM=15. = −5/15 + 6/15 = 1/15
RHS: 2/5 + (−1/3). LCM=15. = 6/15 + (−5/15) = 1/15
LHS = RHS = 1/15 ✓ This is the commutative property of addition.

Q2. Find the additive inverse of: (a) 3/5 (b) −7/9 (c) 0

Show Solution
Rule: Additive inverse of a/b = −a/b (the number that adds to 0).
(a) Additive inverse of 3/5 = −3/5 (3/5 + (−3/5) = 0 ✓)
(b) Additive inverse of −7/9 = 7/9
(c) Additive inverse of 0 = 0 (0 + 0 = 0)

Q3. Find the reciprocal of: (a) 3/7 (b) −5 (c) 1

Show Solution
Rule: Reciprocal of a/b = b/a. A number × its reciprocal = 1.
(a) Reciprocal of 3/7 = 7/3. Check: 3/7 × 7/3 = 1 ✓
(b) −5 = −5/1. Reciprocal = −1/5
(c) Reciprocal of 1 = 1 (1 × 1 = 1)
Note: 0 has NO reciprocal (can’t divide by 0).

Q4. Simplify: (2/3 × −9/4) + (1/2)

Show Solution
Step 1: Do multiplication first: 2/3 × −9/4
Step 2: = (2 × −9)/(3 × 4) = −18/12
Step 3: Simplify: −18/12 = −3/2 (divide both by 6)
Step 4: Now add: −3/2 + 1/2
Step 5: Same denominator: (−3 + 1)/2 = −2/2 = −1

Q5. Find 3 rational numbers between 1/4 and 1/2.

Show Solution
Step 1: Convert to equivalent fractions with larger denominator.
Step 2: 1/4 = 4/16 and 1/2 = 8/16
Step 3: Numbers between 4/16 and 8/16: 5/16, 6/16 (= 3/8), 7/16
Tip: If you need more, use /32: 1/4=8/32, 1/2=16/32. Now 9 numbers between!

Q6. Is −5/0 a rational number?

Show Solution
Step 1: A rational number is p/q where q ≠ 0.
Step 2: Here q = 0. Division by zero is undefined.
Answer: No, −5/0 is NOT a rational number.

Q7. Verify distributive property: 1/2 × (3/4 + 1/3) = 1/2 × 3/4 + 1/2 × 1/3

Show Solution
LHS: 3/4 + 1/3 = 9/12 + 4/12 = 13/12. Then 1/2 × 13/12 = 13/24
RHS: 1/2 × 3/4 = 3/8. And 1/2 × 1/3 = 1/6. Now 3/8 + 1/6 = 9/24 + 4/24 = 13/24
LHS = RHS = 13/24 ✓ Distributive property verified!

Q8. A tank is 2/5 full. After adding 14 litres, it becomes 4/5 full. What is the tank’s capacity?

Show Solution
Step 1: Water added fills 4/5 − 2/5 = 2/5 of the tank
Step 2: So 2/5 of capacity = 14 litres
Step 3: Capacity = 14 ÷ 2/5 = 14 × 5/2 = 70/2 = 35 litres
Check: 2/5 of 35 = 14. After adding 14: 14+14 = 28 = 4/5 of 35. ✓

HOTS Advanced

HardHOTS

1. Is there a rational number between any two rational numbers? How many?

Show Answer
Yes, infinitely many! Between any two rationals a/b and c/d, you can always find more by converting to equivalent fractions with larger denominators. This is called the density property of rationals.
HardHOTS

2. Find a rational number exactly halfway between 1/3 and 1/2.

Show Answer
Step 1: Halfway = average = (1/3 + 1/2) ÷ 2
Step 2: 1/3 + 1/2 = 2/6 + 3/6 = 5/6
Step 3: 5/6 ÷ 2 = 5/12 = 5/12
HardHOTS

3. The product of two rational numbers is −8/9. If one is 4/3, find the other.

Show Answer
Step 1: (4/3) × x = −8/9
Step 2: x = −8/9 ÷ 4/3 = −8/9 × 3/4
Step 3: = −24/36 = −2/3
Check: 4/3 × (−2/3) = −8/9 ✓

Common Mistakes

  • Forgetting q cannot be zero
    5/0 is a rational number
    5/0 is UNDEFINED. q must never be zero!
  • Not finding common denominator before adding
    1/3 + 1/4 = 2/7
    LCM = 12. 4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12
  • Negative denominator in standard form
    Standard form of 3/−5 is 3/−5
    Move negative to top: −3/5. Denominator must be positive!
  • Not simplifying the final answer
    −18/12 (left as is)
    Divide by HCF 6: −3/2. Always simplify!
