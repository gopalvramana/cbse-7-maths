Ratios

Real-world: A recipe says mix flour and sugar in ratio 3:1. That means for every 3 cups flour, use 1 cup sugar. If you use 6 cups flour, you need 2 cups sugar. The ratio stays the same!
  • A ratio compares two quantities of the same kind (e.g., 3 boys and 5 girls = ratio 3:5).
  • Equivalent ratios are like equivalent fractions: 2:3 = 4:6 = 6:9 (multiply/divide both parts by same number).
  • To simplify a ratio, divide both parts by their HCF (e.g., 12:8 = 3:2, dividing both by 4).

Example: Express 40 cm to 2 m as a ratio.

Step 1: Convert to same units. 2 m = 200 cm.
Step 2: Ratio = 40:200
Step 3: Simplify (divide both by 40): 1:5

Percentage

Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100 — “Per cent” means “per hundred.”
WHY use percentages?
Ravi scored 18/20 in Maths and 36/50 in Science. Which is better? Hard to compare directly!
Maths: (18/20) × 100 = 90%. Science: (36/50) × 100 = 72%.
Now it’s clear — Maths is better. Percentages make comparison easy!

Example: Express 3/5 as a percentage.

Step 1: Percentage = fraction × 100
Step 2: (3/5) × 100 = 300/5 = 60%

Example: Find 25% of 600.

Step 1: 25% = 25/100 = 1/4
Step 2: 1/4 × 600 = 600/4 = 150

Profit and Loss

  • Cost Price (CP) = what you paid to buy something.
  • Selling Price (SP) = what you sold it for.
  • If SP > CP → Profit = SP − CP
  • If CP > SP → Loss = CP − SP
Critical rule: Profit% and Loss% are ALWAYS calculated on Cost Price (CP), never on SP. This is the #1 mistake students make!

Simple Interest

Simple Interest (SI) = (P × R × T) / 100
P = Principal (amount borrowed/deposited), R = Rate (% per year), T = Time (in years)
Amount = Principal + SI
Real-world: You deposit Rs 10,000 in a bank at 8% per year for 3 years.
SI = (10000 × 8 × 3)/100 = Rs 2400. You get back: 10000 + 2400 = Rs 12,400.

Example: Find SI on Rs 5000 at 6% for 2 years. Also find the amount.

Step 1: Write down: P = 5000, R = 6, T = 2
Step 2: SI = (P × R × T) / 100 = (5000 × 6 × 2) / 100
Step 3: = 60000 / 100 = Rs 600
Step 4: Amount = P + SI = 5000 + 600 = Rs 5600

NCERT Exercise Solutions — All Questions Solved

Exercise 8.1 — Ratios & Percentages (8 Questions)

Q1. Find the ratio of: (a) Rs 5 to 50 paise (b) 3 km to 300 m (c) 15 kg to 210 g

Show Solution
(a) Rs 5 to 50 paise
Step 1: We can only compare quantities in the same unit. Rs 5 is in rupees, 50 paise is in paise. Let’s convert rupees to paise.
Step 2: We know that Rs 1 = 100 paise. So Rs 5 = 5 × 100 = 500 paise.
Step 3: Now both are in paise. Ratio = 500 : 50
Step 4: To simplify, we divide both numbers by their HCF. What’s the biggest number that divides both 500 and 50? It’s 50.
Step 5: 500 ÷ 50 = 10 and 50 ÷ 50 = 1.
Answer: 10 : 1
(b) 3 km to 300 m
Step 1: Convert to same unit. 1 km = 1000 m. So 3 km = 3 × 1000 = 3000 m.
Step 2: Ratio = 3000 : 300
Step 3: Find HCF. Both are divisible by 300.
Step 4: 3000 ÷ 300 = 10 and 300 ÷ 300 = 1.
Answer: 10 : 1
(c) 15 kg to 210 g
Step 1: Convert to same unit. 1 kg = 1000 g. So 15 kg = 15 × 1000 = 15000 g.
Step 2: Ratio = 15000 : 210
Step 3: Both are divisible by 30 (HCF). Let’s divide step by step:
15000 ÷ 30 = 500 and 210 ÷ 30 = 7.
Step 4: Can we simplify 500 : 7 further? 500 and 7 share no common factor (7 is prime and doesn’t divide 500).
Answer: 500 : 7

Q2. Convert to percentage: (a) 3/4 (b) 2/5 (c) 7/8 (d) 12/25

Show Solution
Rule: To convert a fraction to percentage, multiply the fraction by 100.
(a) 3/4 as a percentage:
Multiply: 3/4 × 100
= (3 × 100) / 4 (multiply the numerator by 100)
= 300 / 4 (now divide 300 by 4)
= 75%
(b) 2/5 as a percentage:
Multiply: 2/5 × 100
= (2 × 100) / 5
= 200 / 5 (divide 200 by 5)
= 40%
(c) 7/8 as a percentage:
Multiply: 7/8 × 100
= (7 × 100) / 8
= 700 / 8 (divide 700 by 8)
= 87.5%
(d) 12/25 as a percentage:
Multiply: 12/25 × 100
= (12 × 100) / 25
= 1200 / 25 (divide 1200 by 25)
= 48%

Q3. Convert to fraction in simplest form: (a) 25% (b) 12.5% (c) 150%

Show Solution
Rule: “Percent” means “per hundred.” So to convert percentage to fraction, put it over 100 and simplify.
(a) 25% as a fraction:
Write as fraction: 25/100
Simplify: find HCF of 25 and 100. Both divisible by 25.
25 ÷ 25 = 1, and 100 ÷ 25 = 4.
Answer: 1/4
(b) 12.5% as a fraction:
Write as fraction: 12.5/100
The decimal is tricky! Multiply top and bottom by 10 to remove it:
(12.5 × 10) / (100 × 10) = 125/1000
Now simplify: HCF of 125 and 1000 = 125.
125 ÷ 125 = 1, and 1000 ÷ 125 = 8.
Answer: 1/8
(c) 150% as a fraction:
Write as fraction: 150/100
Simplify: HCF of 150 and 100 = 50.
150 ÷ 50 = 3, and 100 ÷ 50 = 2.
Answer: 3/2 = 1½
Note: More than 100% means the value is more than 1 whole!

Q4. Ritu scored 320 out of 400. Express as percentage. If pass mark is 40%, did she pass?

Show Solution
Step 1: Percentage = (320/400) × 100
Step 2: = 32000/400 = 80%
Step 3: Pass mark = 40%. Ritu scored 80%.
Step 4: 80% > 40%. Yes, she passed!

Q5. A town has population 50,000. If 52% are men and 12% are children below 10. Find the number of women and children.

Show Solution
Step 1: Men = 52% of 50000 = (52/100) × 50000 = 26,000
Step 2: Children below 10 = 12% of 50000 = (12/100) × 50000 = 6,000
Step 3: Women = Total − Men − Children = 50000 − 26000 − 6000 = 18,000

Q6. Find: (a) 15% of 250 (b) 1% of 1 hour in minutes (c) 20% of Rs 500

Show Solution
(a) 15% of 250:
15% means 15 out of 100. So: (15/100) × 250
Multiply numerator: 15 × 250 = 3750
Divide by 100: 3750 ÷ 100 = 37.5
(b) 1% of 1 hour in minutes:
First convert: 1 hour = 60 minutes.
1% of 60 = (1/100) × 60 = 60 ÷ 100 = 0.6 minutes
Convert to seconds: 0.6 minutes × 60 seconds = 36 seconds
(c) 20% of 500 = (20/100) × 500 = Rs 100

Q7. A shirt originally costs Rs 800. It is on sale at 15% discount. Find the selling price.

Show Solution
Step 1: Discount = 15% of Rs 800
Step 2: = (15/100) × 800 = 12000/100 = Rs 120
Step 3: Selling Price = Original − Discount = 800 − 120 = Rs 680

Q8. Due to price increase of 20%, 1 kg of apples now costs Rs 60. What was the old price?

Show Solution
Step 1: Let old price = x. New price = x + 20% of x = 1.2x
Step 2: 1.2x = 60
Step 3: x = 60 ÷ 1.2 = 60 × 10/12 = Rs 50
Check: 50 + 20% of 50 = 50 + 10 = 60 ✓
Exercise 8.2 — Profit, Loss & Simple Interest (10 Questions)

Q1. A shopkeeper buys a toy for Rs 250 and sells it for Rs 300. Find profit and profit%.

Show Solution
Step 1: CP = Rs 250, SP = Rs 300
Step 2: SP > CP, so it’s a profit. Profit = SP − CP = 300 − 250 = Rs 50
Step 3: Profit% = (Profit/CP) × 100 = (50/250) × 100
Step 4: = 5000/250 = 20%

Q2. A cycle is bought for Rs 1500 and sold for Rs 1350. Find loss and loss%.

Show Solution
Step 1: CP = Rs 1500, SP = Rs 1350
Step 2: CP > SP, so it’s a loss. Loss = CP − SP = 1500 − 1350 = Rs 150
Step 3: Loss% = (Loss/CP) × 100 = (150/1500) × 100
Step 4: = 15000/1500 = 10%

Q3. CP of 50 oranges = Rs 10 each. SP = Rs 13 each. Find profit%.

Show Solution
Step 1: Total CP = 50 × 10 = Rs 500
Step 2: Total SP = 50 × 13 = Rs 650
Step 3: Profit = 650 − 500 = Rs 150
Step 4: Profit% = (150/500) × 100 = 30%

Q4. A shirt was bought for Rs 400. Sold at 10% loss. Find SP.

Show Solution
Step 1: CP = Rs 400, Loss% = 10%
Step 2: Loss = 10% of 400 = (10/100) × 400 = Rs 40
Step 3: SP = CP − Loss = 400 − 40 = Rs 360

Q5. An article was sold for Rs 540 at 8% profit. Find CP.

Show Solution
Step 1: SP = CP + 8% of CP = 1.08 × CP
Step 2: 1.08 × CP = 540
Step 3: CP = 540 / 1.08 = 540 × 100/108 = Rs 500
Check: 500 + 8% of 500 = 500 + 40 = 540 ✓

Q6. Find SI on Rs 2000 at 5% per year for 3 years.

Show Solution
Step 1: P = 2000, R = 5, T = 3
Step 2: SI = (P × R × T) / 100 = (2000 × 5 × 3) / 100
Step 3: = 30000 / 100 = Rs 300
Step 4: Amount = 2000 + 300 = Rs 2300

Q7. Rs 8000 at 6% for 2 years. Find SI and amount.

Show Solution
Step 1: P = 8000, R = 6, T = 2
Step 2: SI = (8000 × 6 × 2) / 100 = 96000 / 100 = Rs 960
Step 3: Amount = 8000 + 960 = Rs 8960

Q8. Rs 6000 at 10% per year. How many years for SI = Rs 1800?

Show Solution
Step 1: SI = PRT/100. We know SI=1800, P=6000, R=10. Find T.
Step 2: 1800 = (6000 × 10 × T) / 100
Step 3: 1800 = 600T
Step 4: T = 1800 / 600 = 3 years

Q9. At what rate will Rs 4000 earn Rs 600 SI in 3 years?

Show Solution
Step 1: SI=600, P=4000, T=3. Find R.
Step 2: 600 = (4000 × R × 3) / 100
Step 3: 600 = 120R
Step 4: R = 600/120 = 5% per year

Q10. What principal earns Rs 450 SI at 9% in 2 years?

Show Solution
Step 1: SI=450, R=9, T=2. Find P.
Step 2: 450 = (P × 9 × 2) / 100
Step 3: 450 = 18P/100 → 450 × 100 = 18P
Step 4: P = 45000/18 = Rs 2500
Check: (2500 × 9 × 2)/100 = 45000/100 = 450 ✓

HOTS Advanced

HardHOTS

1. A shopkeeper marks up an item by 25% on CP, then gives 10% discount. Does he still make profit? How much?

Show Answer
Step 1: Let’s take CP = Rs 100 (easy to calculate percentages on 100).

Step 2: Find Marked Price (25% markup).
25% of 100 = (25/100) × 100 = 25.
Marked Price = CP + markup = 100 + 25 = Rs 125.

Step 3: Apply 10% discount on Marked Price.
10% of 125 = (10/100) × 125 = 1250/100 = 12.50.
Discount = Rs 12.50.

Step 4: Find Selling Price.
SP = Marked Price − Discount = 125 − 12.50 = Rs 112.50.

Step 5: Find Profit.
Profit = SP − CP = 112.50 − 100 = Rs 12.50.
Profit% = (12.50/100) × 100 = 12.5%.

Answer: Yes, the shopkeeper still makes 12.5% profit even after giving 10% discount!
HardHOTS

2. Two banks offer: Bank A: 8% for 3 years. Bank B: 10% for 2 years. Where should you deposit Rs 10,000 to earn more interest?

Show Answer
Step 1: Bank A interest.
P = Rs 10,000, R = 8%, T = 3 years
SI = (10000 × 8 × 3) / 100 = 240000 / 100 = Rs 2400

Step 2: Bank B interest.
P = Rs 10,000, R = 10%, T = 2 years
SI = (10000 × 10 × 2) / 100 = 200000 / 100 = Rs 2000

Step 3: Compare.
Bank A: Rs 2400 vs Bank B: Rs 2000.
Bank A earns Rs 400 more. Even though Bank B has a higher rate (10% vs 8%), Bank A wins because the time period is longer (3 years vs 2 years).
HardHOTS

3. If the price of an item increases by 20% and then decreases by 20%, is the final price the same as original?

Show Answer
Step 1: Let’s take the original price as Rs 100.
(We choose 100 because percentages are easy to calculate on 100.)

Step 2: First, the price INCREASES by 20%.
What is 20% of 100?
20% of 100 = (20/100) × 100 = 20.
So the price goes UP by Rs 20.
New price = 100 + 20 = Rs 120

Step 3: Now, the price DECREASES by 20%.
But careful! The 20% decrease is on the NEW price (Rs 120), not the original (Rs 100)!
What is 20% of 120?
20% of 120 = (20/100) × 120 = (20 × 120) / 100 = 2400 / 100 = 24.
So the price goes DOWN by Rs 24.
Final price = 120 − 24 = Rs 96

Step 4: Compare with original.
Original = Rs 100. Final = Rs 96.
No! The final price is NOT the same as the original. It is Rs 4 LESS.

Step 5: Why does this happen?
The 20% increase was calculated on Rs 100 (= Rs 20 increase).
But the 20% decrease was calculated on Rs 120 (= Rs 24 decrease).
The decrease amount (24) is MORE than the increase amount (20), because 20% of a bigger number is a bigger amount!
Net change = −4 out of 100 = 4% net decrease.

Common Mistakes

  • Using SP instead of CP for Profit%
    Profit% = (Profit/SP) × 100
    Profit% = (Profit/CP) × 100. Always use Cost Price!
  • Forgetting to multiply by 100 for percentage
    3/5 as percentage = 3/5 = 0.6
    3/5 × 100 = 60%
  • Confusing profit and loss
    SP < CP but wrote “profit”
    SP > CP = Profit. SP < CP = Loss. Check which is bigger first!
  • Forgetting to divide by 100 in SI formula
    SI = P × R × T = 5000 × 8 × 3 = 120000
    SI = PRT ÷ 100 = 120000/100 = Rs 1200
  • Not converting units before finding ratio
    Ratio of 5 km to 300 m = 5:300
    Convert first: 5 km = 5000 m. Ratio = 5000:300 = 50:3
