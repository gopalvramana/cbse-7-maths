Chapter 8 · Measurement & Comparison

Comparing Quantities

Summary

  • A ratio compares two quantities of the same kind (e.g., 3 : 5).
  • Equivalent ratios are like equivalent fractions — multiply or divide both parts by the same number.
  • Percentage means "per hundred" — it's a fraction with denominator 100.
  • To convert a fraction to percentage: multiply by 100.
  • Profit = Selling Price (SP) − Cost Price (CP). Loss = CP − SP.
  • Simple Interest (SI) is the extra money you pay (or earn) on a borrowed (or saved) amount.
  • Amount = Principal + Simple Interest.

Key Formulas

Percentage = (Value / Total) × 100
Profit % = (Profit / CP) × 100
Loss % = (Loss / CP) × 100
Simple Interest (SI) = (P × R × T) / 100
P = Principal, R = Rate (%), T = Time (years)
Amount = Principal + SI

Worked Examples

Example 1: Fraction to Percentage

Express 3/5 as a percentage.

Step 1: Multiply by 100: (3/5) × 100
Step 2: = 300/5 = 60%

Example 2: Profit and Profit %

A shopkeeper buys a toy for Rs 500 and sells it for Rs 575. Find the profit %.

Step 1: Profit = SP − CP = 575 − 500 = Rs 75
Step 2: Profit % = (75/500) × 100 = 15%

Example 3: Simple Interest

Find the simple interest on Rs 2000 at 5% per year for 3 years.

Step 1: SI = (P × R × T) / 100 = (2000 × 5 × 3) / 100
Step 2: SI = 30000 / 100 = Rs 300
Step 3: Amount = 2000 + 300 = Rs 2300

Practice Problems

Easy

Express 1/4 as a percentage.

Show Answer
(1/4) × 100 = 25%
Easy

A pen costs Rs 10 and is sold for Rs 12. Find the profit.

Show Answer
Profit = 12 − 10 = Rs 2
Medium

A shirt was bought for Rs 400 and sold at a loss of 10%. Find the selling price.

Show Answer
Loss = 10% of 400 = Rs 40. SP = 400 − 40 = Rs 360
Medium

Find the simple interest on Rs 5000 at 8% for 2 years.

Show Answer
SI = (5000 × 8 × 2) / 100 = 80000/100 = Rs 800
Hard

Anita scored 320 out of 400 in an exam. What is her percentage? If the pass mark is 75%, did she pass?

Show Answer
Percentage = (320/400) × 100 = 80%. Pass mark = 75%. Since 80% > 75%, yes, she passed.
Hard

Rs 6000 is deposited at 10% per year. After how many years will the SI be Rs 1800?

Show Answer
SI = PRT/100 → 1800 = (6000 × 10 × T)/100 → 1800 = 600T → T = 3 years

Common Mistakes

  • Using SP instead of CP when calculating profit% or loss% — always use CP as the base!
  • Forgetting to multiply by 100 when converting to percentage.
  • Confusing profit (SP > CP) with loss (CP > SP).
  • In the SI formula, forgetting to divide by 100.
