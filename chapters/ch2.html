Fractions — Quick Recap

Real-world connection: You eat 3 slices out of 8 in a pizza — that’s 3/8. Your phone is 75% charged — that’s 3/4. You walk half a kilometre — that’s 1/2 km. Fractions are everywhere!
  • Fraction = part of a whole. Written as numerator/denominator (e.g., 3/4 means 3 parts out of 4).
  • Proper fraction: numerator < denominator (e.g., 2/5, 3/8). Value is less than 1.
  • Improper fraction: numerator ≥ denominator (e.g., 7/3, 5/5). Value is 1 or more.
  • Mixed number: whole number + fraction (e.g., 2⅓ = 7/3). To convert: multiply whole by denominator, add numerator.
  • Equivalent fractions: same value, different form (e.g., 1/2 = 2/4 = 3/6 = 50/100).
  • Decimal is another way to write a fraction with denominator 10, 100, 1000, etc. (e.g., 0.5 = 1/2, 0.75 = 3/4).

Multiplication of Fractions

Rule: Multiply tops, multiply bottoms

a/b × c/d = (a × c) / (b × d)
  • Fraction × Fraction: 2/3 × 4/5 = (2×4)/(3×5) = 8/15
  • Fraction × Whole number: 3/7 × 5 = (3×5)/7 = 15/7 = 2&frac17; (treat 5 as 5/1)
  • Mixed number: Convert to improper first! 1½ × 2⅓ = 3/2 × 7/3 = 21/6 = 3½
WHY does “of” mean multiply?

“1/2 of 6 chocolates” means take half of 6 = 3. Mathematically: 1/2 × 6 = 3.
“2/3 of 15 students” means take 2/3 of 15 = 10. So: 2/3 × 15 = 10.

“Of” in maths always means multiplication!
Real-world example: A recipe needs 3/4 cup of sugar. You want to make half the recipe. How much sugar?
1/2 × 3/4 = 3/8 cup. Multiplying by a fraction less than 1 makes things smaller!
Exam tip: Always simplify before multiplying when possible. For 4/9 × 3/8: cancel 4 and 8 (both ÷ 4), cancel 3 and 9 (both ÷ 3). Get 1/3 × 1/2 = 1/6. Much easier!

Division of Fractions

The Golden Rule: a/b ÷ c/d = a/b × d/c — Flip the second fraction (take reciprocal), then multiply.
WHY do we flip and multiply?

“How many 1/4s fit in 1/2?” Think of it: 1/2 = 2/4, so two 1/4s fit. That’s 1/2 ÷ 1/4 = 2.
Check: 1/2 × 4/1 = 4/2 = 2. ✓ Flipping works!

Another way: Dividing by a fraction = multiplying by how many of those fractions fit in 1 whole. The reciprocal tells you exactly that.
  • Fraction ÷ Fraction: 3/4 ÷ 2/5 = 3/4 × 5/2 = 15/8 = 1⅞
  • Fraction ÷ Whole number: 2/3 ÷ 4 = 2/3 × 1/4 = 2/12 = 1/6
  • Whole number ÷ Fraction: 5 ÷ 1/3 = 5 × 3/1 = 15 (how many 1/3s in 5? Fifteen!)
Real-world example: You have 3/4 kg of rice. Each meal needs 1/8 kg. How many meals?
3/4 ÷ 1/8 = 3/4 × 8/1 = 24/4 = 6 meals.

Multiplication of Decimals

Rule: Multiply as whole numbers, then count decimal places

  • Step 1: Ignore the decimal points and multiply as whole numbers.
  • Step 2: Count the total decimal places in both numbers.
  • Step 3: Put the decimal point in the answer with that many places from the right.

Example: 0.3 × 0.02

Step 1: Multiply as whole numbers: 3 × 2 = 6
Step 2: Count decimal places: 0.3 has 1, 0.02 has 2. Total = 3 places.
Step 3: Put decimal 3 places from right in “6”: 0.006. Answer: 0.006

Example: 2.5 × 1.4

Step 1: 25 × 14 = 350
Step 2: Total decimal places = 1 + 1 = 2
Step 3: Place decimal 2 places from right: 3.50 = 3.5
WHY does counting decimal places work?
0.3 = 3/10 and 0.02 = 2/100. So 0.3 × 0.02 = (3/10) × (2/100) = 6/1000 = 0.006.
The denominators multiply (10 × 100 = 1000), which gives us 3 decimal places!
Real-world: Petrol costs Rs 96.50 per litre. You buy 2.5 litres. Cost = 96.50 × 2.5 = 9650 × 25 = 241250 → 3 decimal places → Rs 241.250 ≈ Rs 241.25

Division of Decimals

Rule: Make the divisor a whole number first

  • Step 1: If the divisor (number you divide by) has a decimal, multiply both numbers by 10, 100, etc. to make it whole.
  • Step 2: Now divide normally.

Example: 1.44 ÷ 0.12

Step 1: Multiply both by 100: 144 ÷ 12
Step 2: 144 ÷ 12 = 12

Example: 4.5 ÷ 1.5

Step 1: Multiply both by 10: 45 ÷ 15
Step 2: 45 ÷ 15 = 3

Example: 7 ÷ 0.25

Step 1: Multiply both by 100: 700 ÷ 25
Step 2: 700 ÷ 25 = 28
Real-world: If one chocolate costs Rs 0.25, how many can you buy with Rs 7? Answer: 28!
Exam tip: Dividing by 0.5 = multiplying by 2. Dividing by 0.1 = multiplying by 10. Dividing by 0.25 = multiplying by 4. These shortcuts save time!

Exercise 2.1 — Multiplication of Fractions (8 Questions)

Q1. Find: (a) 2/5 × 1/3   (b) 4/7 × 3/5   (c) 2/9 × 3/4   (d) 5/11 × 2/3

Show Solution
(a) 2/5 × 1/3
Multiply numerators: 2 × 1 = 2
Multiply denominators: 5 × 3 = 15
Answer: 2/15
(b) 4/7 × 3/5
Multiply numerators: 4 × 3 = 12
Multiply denominators: 7 × 5 = 35
Answer: 12/35
(c) 2/9 × 3/4
Multiply: (2 × 3)/(9 × 4) = 6/36
Simplify: both 6 and 36 are divisible by 6
6 ÷ 6 = 1, 36 ÷ 6 = 6
Answer: 1/6
(d) 5/11 × 2/3
Multiply numerators: 5 × 2 = 10
Multiply denominators: 11 × 3 = 33
No common factors. Answer: 10/33

Q2. Multiply and express as a mixed number: (a) 3 × 5/6   (b) 5 × 2/3   (c) 7 × 2¼

Show Solution
(a) 3 × 5/6
Write 3 as 3/1. Multiply: (3 × 5)/(1 × 6) = 15/6
Simplify: 15 ÷ 3 = 5, 6 ÷ 3 = 2. So 15/6 = 5/2
Convert to mixed: 5 ÷ 2 = 2 remainder 1
Answer: 2½
(b) 5 × 2/3
Write 5 as 5/1. Multiply: (5 × 2)/(1 × 3) = 10/3
Convert to mixed: 10 ÷ 3 = 3 remainder 1
Answer: 3⅓
(c) 7 × 2¼
First convert 2¼ to improper: (2 × 4 + 1)/4 = 9/4
Now multiply: 7/1 × 9/4 = (7 × 9)/(1 × 4) = 63/4
Convert to mixed: 63 ÷ 4 = 15 remainder 3
Answer: 15¾

Q3. Find: (a) 1/2 of 46   (b) 2/3 of 18   (c) 3/4 of 32   (d) 4/5 of 35

Show Solution
Remember: “of” means multiply!
(a) 1/2 of 46 = 1/2 × 46 = 46/2 = 23
(b) 2/3 of 18 = 2/3 × 18 = (2 × 18)/3 = 36/3 = 12
(Shortcut: first divide 18 by 3 = 6, then multiply by 2 = 12)
(c) 3/4 of 32 = 3/4 × 32 = (3 × 32)/4 = 96/4 = 24
(Shortcut: 32 ÷ 4 = 8, then 8 × 3 = 24)
(d) 4/5 of 35 = 4/5 × 35 = (4 × 35)/5 = 140/5 = 28
(Shortcut: 35 ÷ 5 = 7, then 7 × 4 = 28)

Q4. Multiply: (a) 2⅓ × 3   (b) 4½ × 2/3   (c) 3¼ × 2/5

Show Solution
(a) 2⅓ × 3
Convert: 2⅓ = (2×3+1)/3 = 7/3
Multiply: 7/3 × 3/1 = (7×3)/(3×1) = 21/3 = 7
(b) 4½ × 2/3
Convert: 4½ = (4×2+1)/2 = 9/2
Multiply: 9/2 × 2/3 = (9×2)/(2×3) = 18/6
Simplify: 18 ÷ 6 = 3
(c) 3¼ × 2/5
Convert: 3¼ = (3×4+1)/4 = 13/4
Multiply: 13/4 × 2/5 = (13×2)/(4×5) = 26/20
Simplify (both ÷ 2): 13/10
Convert to mixed: 1 3/10

Q5. A car runs 16 km using 1 litre of petrol. How far will it run using 2¾ litres?

Show Solution
Step 1: The car goes 16 km per litre. We have 2¾ litres. So: Distance = 16 × 2¾
Step 2: Convert the mixed number: 2¾ = (2 × 4 + 3)/4 = 11/4
Step 3: Multiply: 16 × 11/4. Think of 16 as 16/1.
Step 4: (16 × 11) / (1 × 4) = 176/4
Step 5: Simplify: 176 ÷ 4 = 44 km

Q6. Find: (a) 1/2 × 3/4 × 4/5   (b) 2/3 × 9/16 × 4/27

Show Solution
(a) (1×3×4)/(2×4×5) = 12/40 = 3/10
(b) (2×9×4)/(3×16×27) = 72/1296. Simplify: cancel 2&16, 9&27, 4&3 → 1/18

Q7. Sham paints 2/5 of a wall in one hour. How much will he paint in 3½ hours?

Show Solution
Step 1: In 1 hour he paints 2/5 of the wall. In 3½ hours, he paints: 2/5 × 3½
Step 2: Convert 3½ to improper fraction: (3 × 2 + 1)/2 = 7/2
Step 3: Multiply: 2/5 × 7/2 = (2 × 7)/(5 × 2) = 14/10
Step 4: Simplify: 14/10. Both divisible by 2: 14 ÷ 2 = 7, 10 ÷ 2 = 5. So = 7/5
Step 5: Convert to mixed number: 7 ÷ 5 = 1 remainder 2, so 7/5 = 1⅖ walls
Meaning: He finishes the entire wall and paints 2/5 of another wall!

Q8. Which is greater: (a) 2/7 of 3/4 or 3/5 of 5/8?   (b) 1/2 of 6/7 or 2/3 of 3/7?

Show Solution
(a) 2/7 × 3/4 = 6/28 = 3/14. And 3/5 × 5/8 = 15/40 = 3/8.
Compare 3/14 vs 3/8: same numerator, smaller denominator = bigger. So 3/8 > 3/14. Second is greater.
(b) 1/2 × 6/7 = 6/14 = 3/7. And 2/3 × 3/7 = 6/21 = 2/7.
Compare 3/7 vs 2/7: same denominator, bigger numerator wins. 3/7 > 2/7. First is greater.
Exercise 2.2 — Division of Fractions (8 Questions)

Q1. Find the reciprocal of: (a) 3/7   (b) 5   (c) 1/8   (d) 6/11

Show Solution
(a) Reciprocal of 3/7 = 7/3
(b) 5 = 5/1, so reciprocal = 1/5
(c) Reciprocal of 1/8 = 8/1 = 8
(d) Reciprocal of 6/11 = 11/6

Q2. Divide: (a) 3/4 ÷ 2/5   (b) 7/8 ÷ 1/4   (c) 5/6 ÷ 5/3   (d) 2/9 ÷ 4

Show Solution
(a) 3/4 ÷ 2/5
Flip the second fraction: reciprocal of 2/5 = 5/2
Now multiply: 3/4 × 5/2 = (3×5)/(4×2) = 15/8
Convert to mixed: 15 ÷ 8 = 1 remainder 7
Answer: 1⅞
(b) 7/8 ÷ 1/4
Flip: reciprocal of 1/4 = 4/1 = 4
Multiply: 7/8 × 4/1 = (7×4)/(8×1) = 28/8
Simplify (both ÷ 4): 7/2 = 3½
(c) 5/6 ÷ 5/3
Flip: reciprocal of 5/3 = 3/5
Multiply: 5/6 × 3/5 = (5×3)/(6×5) = 15/30
Simplify (both ÷ 15): 1/2
(d) 2/9 ÷ 4
Write 4 as 4/1. Flip: reciprocal = 1/4
Multiply: 2/9 × 1/4 = (2×1)/(9×4) = 2/36
Simplify (both ÷ 2): 1/18

Q3. Divide: (a) 5 ÷ 1/3   (b) 7 ÷ 3/5   (c) 4⅓ ÷ 2

Show Solution
(a) 5 ÷ 1/3
How many 1/3s fit in 5? Flip: reciprocal of 1/3 = 3/1 = 3
Multiply: 5 × 3 = 15
(Think: each whole has 3 thirds, so 5 wholes have 15 thirds!)
(b) 7 ÷ 3/5
Flip: reciprocal of 3/5 = 5/3
Multiply: 7/1 × 5/3 = (7×5)/(1×3) = 35/3
Convert to mixed: 35 ÷ 3 = 11 remainder 2
Answer: 11⅔
(c) 4⅓ ÷ 2
Convert: 4⅓ = (4×3+1)/3 = 13/3
Write 2 as 2/1. Flip: reciprocal = 1/2
Multiply: 13/3 × 1/2 = (13×1)/(3×2) = 13/6
Convert to mixed: 13 ÷ 6 = 2 remainder 1
Answer: 2⅙

Q4. A rope of length 7½ m is cut into pieces of 5/4 m each. How many pieces?

Show Solution
Step 1: We need to find: how many pieces of 5/4 m fit in 7½ m? This is a division problem.
Step 2: Convert the mixed number: 7½ = (7 × 2 + 1)/2 = 15/2
Step 3: Set up the division: 15/2 ÷ 5/4
Step 4: Flip the second fraction (take reciprocal): 15/2 × 4/5
Step 5: Multiply: (15 × 4) / (2 × 5) = 60/10
Step 6: Simplify: 60 ÷ 10 = 6 pieces
Verify: 6 pieces × 5/4 m each = 30/4 = 7.5 = 7½ m ✓

Q5. 3/5 of a number is 24. Find the number.

Show Solution
Step 1: “3/5 of a number is 24” means: 3/5 × x = 24
Step 2: To find x, we need to “undo” the 3/5. How? Divide both sides by 3/5.
Step 3: x = 24 ÷ 3/5
Step 4: Flip and multiply: x = 24 × 5/3
Step 5: 24 × 5 = 120. Then 120 ÷ 3 = 40
Check: 3/5 × 40 = (3 × 40)/5 = 120/5 = 24 ✓ It works!

Q6. Which is greater: 5/6 ÷ 1/3 or 5/6 × 1/3?

Show Solution
5/6 ÷ 1/3 = 5/6 × 3/1 = 15/6 = 5/2 = 2.5
5/6 × 1/3 = 5/18 ≈ 0.28
Dividing by a fraction < 1 makes the number bigger. Multiplying makes it smaller!

Q7. Cost of 3½ metres of cloth is Rs 210. Find the cost per metre.

Show Solution
Step 1: Cost per metre = Total cost ÷ Total metres = 210 ÷ 3½
Step 2: Convert 3½ to improper: (3×2+1)/2 = 7/2
Step 3: Divide: 210 ÷ 7/2. Flip and multiply: 210/1 × 2/7
Step 4: Simplify before multiplying: 210 ÷ 7 = 30. So: 30 × 2 = 60
Answer: Rs 60 per metre
Verify: 60 × 3½ = 60 × 7/2 = 420/2 = 210 ✓

Q8. A tank holds 50 litres. It loses 2½ litres per hour through a leak. How long until empty?

Show Solution
Step 1: We need: Total litres ÷ Litres lost per hour = Time
Step 2: Time = 50 ÷ 2½
Step 3: Convert 2½ to improper: (2×2+1)/2 = 5/2
Step 4: Divide: 50 ÷ 5/2. Flip and multiply: 50/1 × 2/5
Step 5: Simplify first: 50 ÷ 5 = 10. Then: 10 × 2 = 20
Answer: 20 hours
Exercise 2.3 — Multiplication of Decimals (7 Questions)

Q1. Find: (a) 0.2 × 6   (b) 3.5 × 4   (c) 0.07 × 8   (d) 2.03 × 5

Show Solution
(a) 2 × 6 = 12 → 1 decimal place → 1.2
(b) 35 × 4 = 140 → 1 decimal place → 14.0 = 14
(c) 7 × 8 = 56 → 2 decimal places → 0.56
(d) 203 × 5 = 1015 → 2 decimal places → 10.15

Q2. Find: (a) 1.2 × 0.5   (b) 0.3 × 0.3   (c) 2.5 × 0.4   (d) 0.1 × 0.01

Show Solution
(a) 12 × 5 = 60 → 2 decimal places → 0.60 = 0.6
(b) 3 × 3 = 9 → 2 decimal places → 0.09
(c) 25 × 4 = 100 → 2 decimal places → 1.00 = 1
(d) 1 × 1 = 1 → 3 decimal places → 0.001

Q3. A car covers 15.5 km per litre. How far with 3.5 litres?

Show Solution
Distance = 15.5 × 3.5
155 × 35 = 5425 → 2 decimal places → 54.25 km

Q4. A square has side 2.5 cm. Find its area.

Show Solution
Area = side × side = 2.5 × 2.5
25 × 25 = 625 → 2 decimal places → 6.25 cm²

Q5. Find: (a) 0.5 × 0.5 × 0.5   (b) 1.1 × 1.1

Show Solution
(a) 5 × 5 × 5 = 125. Total decimal places = 3. Answer: 0.125
(b) 11 × 11 = 121. Total decimal places = 2. Answer: 1.21

Q6. Cloth costs Rs 45.50 per metre. Find cost of 3.2 metres.

Show Solution
Cost = 45.50 × 3.2
4550 × 32 = 145600 → 3 decimal places → Rs 145.600 = Rs 145.60

Q7. Express as a fraction and verify: 0.4 × 0.5

Show Solution
As decimals: 0.4 × 0.5 = 0.20 = 0.2
As fractions: 4/10 × 5/10 = 20/100 = 2/10 = 1/5 = 0.2 ✓

Q2. Divide: (a) 4.8 ÷ 0.2   (b) 5.25 ÷ 0.5   (c) 3.6 ÷ 0.04   (d) 0.144 ÷ 0.12

9. A car travels 15.75 km on 1 litre of petrol. Petrol costs Rs 96.40 per litre. What is the fuel cost for a 63 km trip?

Show Answer
Step 1: Find how many litres are needed for 63 km.
Litres = Total distance ÷ km per litre = 63 ÷ 15.75

Step 2: Make the divisor a whole number. Multiply both by 100:
6300 ÷ 1575

Step 3: Simplify. Both are divisible by 225: 6300 ÷ 1575 = 4 litres
(Quick check: 15.75 × 4 = 63 ✓)

Step 4: Find the cost. Cost = litres × price per litre
= 4 × Rs 96.40

Step 5: Calculate 4 × 96.40:
4 × 96 = 384. And 4 × 0.40 = 1.60.
Total = 384 + 1.60 = Rs 385.60
Hard

10. Simplify: 2⅓ × 1½ ÷ 3½

Show Answer
Step 1: Convert ALL mixed numbers to improper fractions first:
• 2⅓ = (2 × 3 + 1)/3 = 7/3
• 1½ = (1 × 2 + 1)/2 = 3/2
• 3½ = (3 × 2 + 1)/2 = 7/2

Step 2: Now the problem becomes: 7/3 × 3/2 ÷ 7/2

Step 3: Do the multiplication first (left to right):
7/3 × 3/2 = (7 × 3) / (3 × 2) = 21/6

Step 4: Simplify 21/6. Both divisible by 3: 21 ÷ 3 = 7, 6 ÷ 3 = 2. So 21/6 = 7/2

Step 5: Now divide: 7/2 ÷ 7/2
Flip the second fraction: 7/2 × 2/7 = (7 × 2) / (2 × 7) = 14/14 = 1

HOTS — Higher Order Thinking Advanced

HardHOTS

1. The product of two fractions is 3/5. If one of them is 9/7, find the other.

Show Answer
x × 9/7 = 3/5. So x = 3/5 ÷ 9/7 = 3/5 × 7/9 = 21/45 = 7/15
HardHOTS

2. Is 0.999... (repeating) equal to 1? Show why.

Show Answer
Let x = 0.999...
10x = 9.999...
10x − x = 9.999... − 0.999... = 9
9x = 9, so x = 1. Yes, 0.999... = 1 exactly!
HardHOTS

3. A recipe serves 4 people and needs 2/3 cup of flour. How much flour for 6 people?

Show Answer
For 1 person: 2/3 ÷ 4 = 2/12 = 1/6 cup.
For 6 people: 1/6 × 6 = 1 cup
HardHOTS

4. Without calculating, tell which is bigger: 7/13 × 15/8 OR 7/13 ÷ 15/8? Explain why.

Show Answer
7/13 ÷ 15/8 is bigger when the divisor > 1? No! 15/8 > 1, so dividing by it makes the number smaller. Actually: 7/13 × 15/8 = 105/104 > 1. And 7/13 ÷ 15/8 = 7/13 × 8/15 = 56/195 < 1. So multiplying gives the bigger result when the fraction > 1.

Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

  • Forgetting to take the reciprocal when dividing fractions
    3/4 ÷ 2/5 = 3/4 × 2/5 = 6/20
    3/4 ÷ 2/5 = 3/4 × 5/2 = 15/8
    You must FLIP the second fraction!
  • Not simplifying the final answer
    2/4 × 3/6 = 6/24 (left unsimplified)
    6/24 = 1/4. Always simplify! Better: cancel before multiplying.
  • Misplacing the decimal point
    0.3 × 0.2 = 0.6
    0.3 × 0.2 = 0.06 (2 decimal places, not 1!)
  • Forgetting to convert mixed numbers before multiplying
    2½ × 3 = 2 × 3 + 1/2 = 6½
    2½ = 5/2. Then 5/2 × 3 = 15/2 = 7½
  • Thinking “multiplying always makes bigger”
    1/2 × 6 should be bigger than 6?
    1/2 × 6 = 3. Multiplying by a fraction < 1 makes the number smaller!
