Number Systems

Chapter 2: Fractions and Decimals

Summary

  • A fraction represents a part of a whole. It is written as numerator/denominator (e.g., 3/4 means 3 out of 4 equal parts).
  • In a proper fraction, the numerator is smaller than the denominator (like 2/5). In an improper fraction, the numerator is bigger (like 7/3).
  • To multiply fractions, multiply the numerators together and multiply the denominators together.
  • To divide fractions, flip the second fraction (take its reciprocal) and then multiply.
  • To convert a fraction to a decimal, divide the numerator by the denominator. For example, 3/4 = 0.75.
  • When multiplying decimals, multiply them as whole numbers first, then count the total decimal places in both numbers and put the decimal point in the answer.
  • When dividing decimals, first make the divisor a whole number by moving the decimal point, then divide normally.

Key Formulas

a/b × c/d = ac/bd — Multiply the tops, multiply the bottoms.

a/b ÷ c/d = a/b × d/c — Flip the second fraction and multiply.

Reciprocal of a/b = b/a — Just swap the numerator and denominator.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Find 2/3 × 4/5

Step 1: Multiply the numerators: 2 × 4 = 8.
Step 2: Multiply the denominators: 3 × 5 = 15.
Step 3: The answer is 8/15. It is already in simplest form.

Example 2: Find 3/4 ÷ 2/5

Step 1: Take the reciprocal of 2/5, which is 5/2.
Step 2: Multiply: 3/4 × 5/2.
Step 3: Numerators: 3 × 5 = 15. Denominators: 4 × 2 = 8.
Step 4: The answer is 15/8 (or 1 7/8 as a mixed number).

Example 3: Find 0.3 × 0.02

Step 1: Ignore the decimals and multiply: 3 × 2 = 6.
Step 2: Count total decimal places: 0.3 has 1 place, 0.02 has 2 places. Total = 3 places.
Step 3: Place the decimal: 6 becomes 0.006.

Practice Problems

Easy

1. Find 1/2 × 3/7.

Show Answer

1 × 3 = 3 and 2 × 7 = 14. Answer: 3/14.

Easy

2. Convert 5/8 to a decimal.

Show Answer

5 ÷ 8 = 0.625.

Medium

3. Find 5/6 ÷ 10/3.

Show Answer

Flip 10/3 to get 3/10. Multiply: 5/6 × 3/10 = 15/60 = 1/4.

Medium

4. Calculate 2.5 × 0.4.

Show Answer

25 × 4 = 100. Total decimal places = 2. Answer: 1.00 = 1.

Hard

5. A recipe needs 3/4 kg of sugar. If you want to make 2/3 of the recipe, how much sugar do you need?

Show Answer

3/4 × 2/3 = 6/12 = 1/2 kg of sugar.

Hard

6. A ribbon is 4.5 metres long. How many pieces of 0.75 metres can be cut from it?

Show Answer

4.5 ÷ 0.75 = 45 ÷ 7.5 = 6 pieces.

Common Mistakes

  • Forgetting to take the reciprocal when dividing. When you see ÷ with fractions, you must flip the second fraction first, then multiply. Don't just multiply straight away!
  • Not simplifying the final answer. Always check if your answer can be reduced. For example, 6/12 should be written as 1/2.
  • Misplacing the decimal point. When multiplying decimals, carefully count the total number of decimal places in both numbers. That's how many decimal places your answer needs.
