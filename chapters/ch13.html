What are Exponents?

Real-world: A bacteria doubles every hour. After 10 hours: 210 = 1024 bacteria! Exponents are nature’s way of showing rapid growth. Also used for: computer memory (210 = 1 KB), distances in space (108 km to Sun), and tiny atoms (10−10 m).
  • Instead of writing 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2, we write 25. Much shorter!
  • In an: ‘a’ is the base (the number being multiplied) and ‘n’ is the exponent (how many times).
  • We read 25 as “2 raised to the power 5” or “2 to the 5th.”
Powers of 2 (memorize these!):
21 = 2   |   22 = 4   |   23 = 8   |   24 = 16   |   25 = 32   |   26 = 64   |   27 = 128   |   210 = 1024

Laws of Exponents

Law 1: Multiplying same base — ADD exponents

am × an = am+n
Why? 23 × 24 = (2×2×2) × (2×2×2×2) = 2 multiplied 3+4 = 7 times = 27
Example: 53 × 52 = 53+2 = 55 = 3125

Law 2: Dividing same base — SUBTRACT exponents

am ÷ an = am−n
Why? 25 ÷ 23 = (2×2×2×2×2) / (2×2×2). Three 2’s cancel out, leaving 2×2 = 22 = 25−3
Example: 76 ÷ 74 = 76−4 = 72 = 49

Law 3: Power of a power — MULTIPLY exponents

(am)n = am×n
Why? (23)2 = 23 × 23 = 23+3 = 26 = 23×2
Example: (32)4 = 32×4 = 38 = 6561

Law 4: Power zero — always equals 1

a0 = 1 (where a ≠ 0)
Why? Using the division law: an ÷ an = an−n = a0. But anything divided by itself = 1. So a0 = 1!
Examples: 50 = 1,   1000 = 1,   (−7)0 = 1
CRITICAL: Laws only work for the SAME base!
23 × 24 = 27 ✓ (same base 2)
23 × 34 = ??? (different bases — CANNOT use the law!)

Standard Form (Scientific Notation)

Standard form = a number between 1 and 10, multiplied by a power of 10.
N = a × 10n where 1 ≤ a < 10.
Why use standard form? Writing 3,84,00,000 is hard to read. Writing 3.84 × 107 is much clearer! Scientists use this for very large numbers (distance to stars) and very small numbers (size of atoms).

Example: Express 47,00,000 in standard form.

Step 1: Move the decimal point left until you get a number between 1 and 10.
47,00,000 → 4.700000. We moved the decimal 6 places to the left.
Step 2: Count how many places you moved = 6. This becomes the power of 10.
Step 3: Write it: 4.7 × 106
Check: 4.7 × 1000000 = 47,00,000 ✓

Example: Express 0.00052 in standard form.

Step 1: Move decimal point RIGHT until you get a number between 1 and 10.
0.00052 → 5.2. We moved 4 places to the right.
Step 2: Since we moved RIGHT, the power is negative: −4.
Step 3: 5.2 × 10−4

NCERT Exercise Solutions — All Questions Solved

Exercise 13.1 — Exponents Basics (6 Questions)

Q1. Find the value of: (a) 34   (b) (−2)5   (c) (−1)100

Show Solution
(a) 34
Step 1: 34 means multiply 3 by itself 4 times.
Step 2: 3 × 3 = 9
Step 3: 9 × 3 = 27
Step 4: 27 × 3 = 81
(b) (−2)5
Step 1: Multiply (−2) by itself 5 times.
Step 2: (−2) × (−2) = +4 (negative × negative = positive)
Step 3: 4 × (−2) = −8
Step 4: (−8) × (−2) = +16
Step 5: 16 × (−2) = −32
Note: 5 is odd, so the answer is negative. (Even power → positive, odd power → negative)
(c) (−1)100
Step 1: 100 is even.
Step 2: Even power of −1 = +1. (Pairs of negatives cancel!)
Answer: 1

Q2. Express in exponent form: (a) 729 (b) 1024 (c) 3125

Show Solution
(a) 729:
Try dividing by small primes.
729 ÷ 3 = 243.   243 ÷ 3 = 81.   81 ÷ 3 = 27.   27 ÷ 3 = 9.   9 ÷ 3 = 3.   3 ÷ 3 = 1.
We divided by 3 six times. So 729 = 36
(b) 1024:
1024 ÷ 2 = 512.   512 ÷ 2 = 256.   256 ÷ 2 = 128.   128 ÷ 2 = 64.
64 ÷ 2 = 32.   32 ÷ 2 = 16.   16 ÷ 2 = 8.   8 ÷ 2 = 4.   4 ÷ 2 = 2.   2 ÷ 2 = 1.
Divided by 2 ten times. So 1024 = 210
(c) 3125:
3125 ÷ 5 = 625.   625 ÷ 5 = 125.   125 ÷ 5 = 25.   25 ÷ 5 = 5.   5 ÷ 5 = 1.
Divided by 5 five times. So 3125 = 55

Q3. Simplify: (a) 23 × 24   (b) 56 ÷ 52   (c) (32)3

Show Solution
(a) 23 × 24
Step 1: Same base (both are 2). Use Law 1: add exponents.
Step 2: 23+4 = 27
Step 3: Calculate: 27 = 2×2×2×2×2×2×2 = 128
(b) 56 ÷ 52
Step 1: Same base (5). Use Law 2: subtract exponents.
Step 2: 56−2 = 54
Step 3: Calculate: 54 = 5×5×5×5 = 25×25 = 625
(c) (32)3
Step 1: Power of a power. Use Law 3: multiply exponents.
Step 2: 32×3 = 36
Step 3: Calculate: 36 = 3×3×3×3×3×3 = 9×9×9 = 81×9 = 729

Q4. Simplify and evaluate: (23 × 24) ÷ 25

Show Solution
Step 1: First do the multiplication in the bracket.
23 × 24 = 23+4 = 27 (Law 1: add exponents)
Step 2: Now divide: 27 ÷ 25
= 27−5 = 22 (Law 2: subtract exponents)
Step 3: Calculate: 22 = 2 × 2 = 4

Q5. Express in standard form: (a) 3,84,00,000   (b) 0.00052

Show Solution
(a) 3,84,00,000
Step 1: Place the decimal after the first non-zero digit: 3.8400000
Step 2: Count how many places we moved the decimal from the end: 3,84,00,000. has 7 digits after the 3.
We moved 7 places to the left.
Step 3: Moving left = positive power.
Answer: 3.84 × 107
(b) 0.00052
Step 1: Move decimal right until we get a number between 1 and 10: 5.2
Step 2: Count places moved right: 0.00052 → we jumped 4 places right.
Step 3: Moving right = negative power.
Answer: 5.2 × 10−4

Q6. Find x: (a) 2x = 64   (b) 3x = 243   (c) 5x = 625

Show Solution
(a) 2x = 64
Step 1: Express 64 as a power of 2.
64 = 2 × 32 = 2 × 2 × 16 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 8 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 4 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 26
Step 2: So 2x = 26. Since bases are equal, exponents must be equal.
Answer: x = 6
(b) 3x = 243
243 = 3 × 81 = 3 × 3 × 27 = 3 × 3 × 3 × 9 = 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 = 35
Answer: x = 5
(c) 5x = 625
625 = 5 × 125 = 5 × 5 × 25 = 5 × 5 × 5 × 5 = 54
Answer: x = 4
Exercise 13.2 — Laws & Standard Form (6 Questions)

Q1. Simplify: (−3)2 × (−3)3

Show Solution
Step 1: Same base (−3). Law 1: add exponents.
Step 2: (−3)2+3 = (−3)5
Step 3: Calculate: 5 is odd, so result is negative. 35 = 243.
Answer: −243

Q2. Evaluate: (−2)3 × (−2)4 × (−2)0

Show Solution
Step 1: (−2)0 = 1 (anything to power 0 = 1).
Step 2: So: (−2)3 × (−2)4 × 1 = (−2)3+4 = (−2)7
Step 3: 7 is odd → negative. 27 = 128.
Answer: −128

Q3. Using suitable properties, find: (−5)4 × (−5)2 ÷ (−5)3

Show Solution
Step 1: All same base (−5). First multiply: (−5)4+2 = (−5)6
Step 2: Now divide: (−5)6 ÷ (−5)3 = (−5)6−3 = (−5)3
Step 3: 3 is odd → negative. 53 = 125.
Answer: −125

Q4. Verify: (23)2 = 26

Show Solution
LHS: (23)2. Using Law 3: 23×2 = 26 = 64.
RHS: 26 = 64.
LHS = RHS = 64 ✓ Verified!

Q5. Express as a single power: (35 × 32) ÷ 34

Show Solution
Step 1: Numerator: 35 × 32 = 35+2 = 37 (Law 1)
Step 2: Divide: 37 ÷ 34 = 37−4 = 33 (Law 2)
Step 3: Calculate: 33 = 3 × 3 × 3 = 27

Q6. Express 0.00000347 in standard form.

Show Solution
Step 1: We need a number between 1 and 10: 3.47
Step 2: Count decimal places moved RIGHT: 0.00000347
Move 1: 0.0000347
Move 2: 0.000347
Move 3: 0.00347
Move 4: 0.0347
Move 5: 0.347
Move 6: 3.47 ✓ (now it’s between 1 and 10)
Step 3: Moved 6 places RIGHT = negative power: 10−6
Answer: 3.47 × 10−6

HardHOTS

1. Which is bigger: 210 or 102?

Show Answer
Step 1: 210 = 1024
Step 2: 102 = 100
Step 3: 1024 > 100. So 210 is bigger!
Exponents grow much faster than you think!
HardHOTS

Show Answer
Step 1: Group same bases together.
Powers of 2: 23 × 22 = 23+2 = 25
Powers of 3: 32 × 33 = 32+3 = 35
Answer: 25 × 35 = 32 × 243 = 7776
HardHOTS

3. If 8x = 212, find x.

Show Answer
Step 1: Express 8 as a power of 2: 8 = 23.
Step 2: So 8x = (23)x = 23x (Law 3: multiply exponents).
Step 3: 23x = 212. Bases equal, so exponents equal: 3x = 12.
Step 4: x = 12 ÷ 3 = 4.

Common Mistakes

  • Multiplying the bases instead of adding exponents
    23 × 24 = 47
    23 × 24 = 27 = 128. The base STAYS 2. Only the exponents add!
  • Thinking a0 = 0
    50 = 0
    50 = 1. Any non-zero number to power 0 is always 1!
  • Adding exponents when bases are different
    23 × 34 = 67
    Different bases (2 and 3) — cannot use the law! Must calculate separately: 8 × 81 = 648.
  • Confusing (am)n with am × an
    (23)2 = 25
    (23)2 = 23×2 = 26 = 64. Power of power = MULTIPLY exponents, not add!
  • Wrong direction for standard form
    0.005 = 5 × 103
    Moved decimal RIGHT (small number) = negative power.
    0.005 = 5 × 10−3
