Types of Triangles

Real-world: Traffic signs are equilateral triangles. The roof of a house is usually an isosceles triangle. A slice of pizza is roughly a scalene triangle!

By Sides

By Angles

  • Acute: All angles < 90° (e.g., 60°, 70°, 50°)
  • Right: One angle = 90° (e.g., 90°, 45°, 45°)
  • Obtuse: One angle > 90° (e.g., 120°, 30°, 30°)

Angle Sum Property

The three angles of ANY triangle always add up to 180°.
WHY 180°? Try this: draw a triangle, tear off all 3 corners, and arrange them together. They form a straight line (180°)! This works for every triangle, no matter the shape.

Example: Two angles are 60° and 70°. Find the third.

Step 1: Sum = 180°
Step 2: Third = 180 − 60 − 70 = 50°

Exterior Angle Property

Exterior angle = Sum of the two remote (non-adjacent) interior angles

Triangle Inequality

Sum of any two sides must be GREATER than the third side
Check all 3 pairs: a+b > c, b+c > a, a+c > b
Real-world test: Take 3 sticks of length 3 cm, 4 cm, and 8 cm. Can you make a triangle? Try it! The two short sticks (3+4=7) can’t reach across the long one (8). 7 < 8, so no triangle!

Pythagoras Theorem

In a right triangle: a² + b² = c² where c is the hypotenuse (longest side, opposite the right angle).
Ladder problem: A 10 m ladder leans against a wall. Its foot is 6 m from the wall. How high does it reach?
h² + 6² = 10² → h² = 64 → h = 8 m
Common Pythagorean triples to memorize: (3,4,5), (5,12,13), (8,15,17), (7,24,25). Any multiple also works: (6,8,10), (9,12,15), etc.

Exercise 6.1 — Angle Sum & Exterior Angle (8 Questions)

Q1. Two angles are 50° and 60°. Find the third.

Show Solution
Step 1: Angle sum = 180°
Step 2: Third = 180 − 50 − 60 = 70°

Q2. Angles in ratio 2:3:4. Find all three.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let angles = 2x, 3x, 4x
Step 2: 2x + 3x + 4x = 180 → 9x = 180 → x = 20
Step 3: Angles: 40°, 60°, 80°
Check: 40+60+80 = 180 ✓

Q3. Angles are (x+10)°, (2x)°, (x+30)°. Find all.

Show Solution
Step 1: (x+10) + 2x + (x+30) = 180
Step 2: 4x + 40 = 180 → 4x = 140 → x = 35
Step 3: Angles: 45°, 70°, 65°
Check: 45+70+65 = 180 ✓

Q4. Exterior angle = 110°. One remote interior = 45°. Find the other.

Show Solution
Step 1: Exterior = sum of remote interior angles
Step 2: 110 = 45 + other → other = 110 − 45 = 65°

Q5. Exterior angle = 120°. Both remote interior are equal. Find all 3 angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: 120 = x + x = 2x → x = 60°
Step 2: Third angle = 180 − 120 = 60°
Answer: All three = 60° (equilateral!)

Q6. Can a triangle have (a) two right angles? (b) two obtuse? (c) all < 60°?

Show Solution
(a) 90+90 = 180. Third = 0°. No!
(b) Min: 91+91 = 182 > 180. No!
(c) Max: 59+59+59 = 177 < 180. No!

Q7. Right triangle, one angle 35°. Find the unknown.

Show Solution
Step 1: One angle = 90° (right triangle)
Step 2: Third = 180 − 90 − 35 = 55°

Q8. Isosceles triangle, vertex angle 80°. Find base angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Two base angles are equal. Let each = x.
Step 2: 80 + x + x = 180 → 2x = 100 → x = 50°
Exercise 6.2 — Triangle Inequality (5 Questions)

Q1. Can sides 3, 4, 8 form a triangle?

Show Solution
Step 1: Check: 3 + 4 = 7. Is 7 > 8? No!
Cannot form a triangle.

Q2. Can sides 5, 7, 11 form a triangle?

Show Solution
5+7=12 > 11 ✓   5+11=16 > 7 ✓   7+11=18 > 5 ✓
Yes! All three checks pass.

Q3. Two sides are 5 cm and 9 cm. What are possible third side lengths?

Show Solution
Step 1: Third < 5 + 9 = 14
Step 2: Third > 9 − 5 = 4
Answer: Between 4 cm and 14 cm (exclusive)

Q4. Can sides 2,3,4 form a triangle? What about 1,1,3?

Show Solution
2,3,4: 2+3=5>4 ✓, 2+4=6>3 ✓, 3+4=7>2 ✓. Yes!
1,1,3: 1+1=2. Is 2>3? No! Cannot form a triangle.

Q5. Sides in ratio 3:4:5, perimeter 48 cm. Find sides. Is it right triangle?

Show Solution
Step 1: 3x+4x+5x = 48 → 12x = 48 → x = 4
Step 2: Sides: 12, 16, 20 cm
Step 3: Check: 12²+16² = 144+256 = 400 = 20² ✓
Yes, it’s a right triangle!
Exercise 6.3 — Pythagoras Theorem (5 Questions)

Q1. Legs 3 cm, 4 cm. Find hypotenuse.

Show Solution
Step 1: c² = 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25
Step 2: c = √25 = 5 cm

Q2. Hypotenuse 13, one leg 5. Find the other.

Show Solution
Step 1: a² + 25 = 169 → a² = 144
Step 2: a = √144 = 12 cm

Q3. Ladder 10 m, foot 6 m from wall. Height reached?

Show Solution
Step 1: Ladder = hypotenuse. h² + 36 = 100
Step 2: h² = 64 → h = 8 m

Q4. Is 5, 12, 13 a Pythagorean triple?

Show Solution
25 + 144 = 169 = 13² ✓ Yes!

Q5. Field 40 m × 30 m. Diagonal length?

Show Solution
Step 1: d² = 40² + 30² = 1600 + 900 = 2500
Step 2: d = √2500 = 50 m
Exercise 6.4 — Mixed (6 Questions)

Q1. Classify: (a) 6,6,6 (b) 5,5,3 (c) 3,4,6

Show Solution
(a) All equal → Equilateral
(b) Two equal → Isosceles
(c) All different → Scalene

Q2. Hypotenuse 15, one leg 9. Find other leg.

Show Solution
a² + 81 = 225 → a² = 144 → a = 12 cm

Q3. Isosceles: equal sides 8 cm, base 6 cm. Find altitude to base.

Show Solution
Step 1: Altitude bisects base: half = 3 cm
Step 2: h² + 9 = 64 → h² = 55 → h = √55 ≈ 7.42 cm

Q4. Right triangle legs 6, 8. Find perimeter.

Show Solution
Step 1: Hypotenuse = √(36+64) = √100 = 10
Step 2: Perimeter = 6+8+10 = 24 cm

Q5. Angles (3x−5)°, (x+15)°, (2x−10)°. Find all. Acute/right/obtuse?

Show Solution
Step 1: 6x + 0 = 180 → x = 30
Step 2: Angles: 85°, 45°, 50°
Step 3: All < 90° → Acute triangle

Q6. Exterior 130°. Remote interior in ratio 2:3. Find all 3 interior angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: 130 = 2x + 3x = 5x → x = 26
Step 2: Remote interior: 52° and 78°
Step 3: Third = 180 − 130 = 50°
Check: 52+78+50 = 180 ✓ and 52+78 = 130 ✓

Common Mistakes

  • Angle sum = 360°?
    Triangle angles add to 360°
    Triangle = 180°. Quadrilateral = 360°.
  • Not checking all 3 pairs for inequality
    3+4=7>5, so 3,4,8 works
    3+4=7 vs 8. 7 is NOT > 8. Fails!
  • Pythagoras on non-right triangles
    Any triangle: a²+b²=c²
    ONLY for right triangles!
  • Confusing median and altitude
    They’re the same
    Median = to midpoint. Altitude = perpendicular. Same only in equilateral!
