The Triangle and Its Properties

Summary

  • A triangle has 3 sides, 3 angles, and 3 vertices.
  • Angle sum property: the three angles of any triangle always add up to 180°.
  • By sides: Equilateral (all sides equal), Isosceles (two sides equal), Scalene (no sides equal).
  • By angles: Acute (all angles < 90°), Right (one angle = 90°), Obtuse (one angle > 90°).
  • Exterior angle of a triangle = sum of the two interior opposite angles.
  • Triangle inequality: the sum of any two sides must be greater than the third side.
  • In a right triangle, the longest side (opposite the right angle) is the hypotenuse.
  • Pythagoras theorem: hypotenuse² = base² + height².
  • A median connects a vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side.
  • An altitude is the perpendicular distance from a vertex to the opposite side.

Key Formulas

Angle sum: ∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°
Exterior angle = Sum of two remote interior angles
Pythagoras theorem: a² + b² = c² (where c is the hypotenuse)
Triangle inequality: a + b > c, b + c > a, a + c > b

Worked Examples

Example 1: Finding a Missing Angle

Two angles of a triangle are 60° and 70°. Find the third angle.

Step 1: Angle sum = 180°
Step 2: Third angle = 180° − 60° − 70° = 50°

Example 2: Pythagoras Theorem

A right triangle has legs 3 cm and 4 cm. Find the hypotenuse.

Step 1: c² = a² + b² = 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25
Step 2: c = √25 = 5 cm

Example 3: Triangle Inequality

Can sides of length 3, 4, and 8 form a triangle?

Step 1: Check: 3 + 4 = 7. Is 7 > 8? No!
Step 2: Since the sum of two sides is NOT greater than the third, these cannot form a triangle.

Practice Problems

Easy

The angles of a triangle are 50°, 60°, and x. Find x.

Show Answer
x = 180° − 50° − 60° = 70°
Easy

Classify a triangle with sides 5 cm, 5 cm, and 5 cm.

Show Answer
All sides are equal, so it is an equilateral triangle.
Medium

An exterior angle of a triangle is 110°. One of the interior opposite angles is 45°. Find the other interior opposite angle.

Show Answer
Exterior angle = sum of remote interior angles. Other angle = 110° − 45° = 65°
Medium

Can sides 5, 7, and 11 form a triangle?

Show Answer
5 + 7 = 12 > 11 ✓, 5 + 11 = 16 > 7 ✓, 7 + 11 = 18 > 5 ✓. Yes, they can form a triangle.
Hard

A right triangle has hypotenuse 13 cm and one leg 5 cm. Find the other leg.

Show Answer
a² + 5² = 13² → a² + 25 = 169 → a² = 144 → a = 12 cm
Hard

A triangle has angles in the ratio 2 : 3 : 4. Find all three angles.

Show Answer
2x + 3x + 4x = 180° → 9x = 180° → x = 20°. Angles: 40°, 60°, 80°

Common Mistakes

  • Thinking the angle sum of a triangle is 360° — it's 180° (360° is for quadrilaterals).
  • Not checking ALL three pairs when testing the triangle inequality.
  • Confusing median (to midpoint) with altitude (perpendicular to side).
  • Applying Pythagoras theorem to non-right triangles — it only works for right triangles!
