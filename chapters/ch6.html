The Triangle and Its Properties

Summary

  • A triangle has 3 sides, 3 angles, and 3 vertices.
  • Angle sum property: the three angles of any triangle always add up to 180° (e.g., if two angles are 50° and 60°, the third is 180° − 50° − 60° = 70°).
  • By sides: Equilateral (all sides equal), Isosceles (two sides equal), Scalene (no sides equal) — e.g., 5, 5, 5 is equilateral; 5, 5, 3 is isosceles; 3, 4, 6 is scalene.
  • By angles: Acute (all angles < 90°), Right (one angle = 90°), Obtuse (one angle > 90°) — e.g., all angles 60° = acute; one angle 90° = right; one angle 110° = obtuse.
  • Exterior angle of a triangle = sum of the two interior opposite angles (e.g., if the two opposite interior angles are 40° and 60°, the exterior angle = 100°).
  • Triangle inequality: the sum of any two sides must be greater than the third side (e.g., sides 3, 4, 8 won't work because 3 + 4 = 7 < 8).
  • In a right triangle, the longest side (opposite the right angle) is the hypotenuse (e.g., in a right triangle with legs 3 and 4, hypotenuse = 5).
  • Pythagoras theorem: hypotenuse² = base² + height² (e.g., 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25 = 5²).
  • A median connects a vertex to the midpoint of the opposite side (e.g., from vertex A to the midpoint of side BC).
  • An altitude is the perpendicular distance from a vertex to the opposite side (e.g., the height of a triangle drawn from a vertex perpendicular to the base).

Key Formulas

Angle sum: ∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°
Exterior angle = Sum of two remote interior angles
Pythagoras theorem: a² + b² = c² (where c is the hypotenuse)
Triangle inequality: a + b > c, b + c > a, a + c > b

Worked Examples

Example 1: Finding a Missing Angle

Two angles of a triangle are 60° and 70°. Find the third angle.

Step 1: Angle sum = 180°
Step 2: Third angle = 180° − 60° − 70° = 50°

Example 2: Pythagoras Theorem

A right triangle has legs 3 cm and 4 cm. Find the hypotenuse.

Step 1: c² = a² + b² = 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25
Step 2: c = √25 = 5 cm

Example 3: Triangle Inequality

Can sides of length 3, 4, and 8 form a triangle?

Step 1: Check: 3 + 4 = 7. Is 7 > 8? No!
Step 2: Since the sum of two sides is NOT greater than the third, these cannot form a triangle.

Practice Problems

Easy

The angles of a triangle are 50°, 60°, and x. Find x.

Show Answer
x = 180° − 50° − 60° = 70°
Easy

Classify a triangle with sides 5 cm, 5 cm, and 5 cm.

Show Answer
All sides are equal, so it is an equilateral triangle.
Medium

An exterior angle of a triangle is 110°. One of the interior opposite angles is 45°. Find the other interior opposite angle.

Show Answer
Exterior angle = sum of remote interior angles. Other angle = 110° − 45° = 65°
Medium

Can sides 5, 7, and 11 form a triangle?

Show Answer
5 + 7 = 12 > 11 ✓, 5 + 11 = 16 > 7 ✓, 7 + 11 = 18 > 5 ✓. Yes, they can form a triangle.
Hard

A right triangle has hypotenuse 13 cm and one leg 5 cm. Find the other leg.

Show Answer
a² + 5² = 13² → a² + 25 = 169 → a² = 144 → a = 12 cm
Hard

A triangle has angles in the ratio 2 : 3 : 4. Find all three angles.

Show Answer
2x + 3x + 4x = 180° → 9x = 180° → x = 20°. Angles: 40°, 60°, 80°
Easy

Is a triangle with sides 6, 6, 6 equilateral, isosceles, or scalene?

Show Answer
All three sides are equal, so it is an equilateral triangle.
Easy

Can a triangle have two right angles? Why or why not?

Show Answer
No. Two right angles would add up to 90° + 90° = 180°, leaving 0° for the third angle. A triangle’s angles must all be greater than 0°, so a triangle cannot have two right angles.
Medium

The angles of a triangle are (x + 10)°, (2x)°, and (x + 30)°. Find all three angles.

Show Answer
(x + 10) + 2x + (x + 30) = 180 → 4x + 40 = 180 → 4x = 140 → x = 35. The angles are 35 + 10 = 45°, 2 × 35 = 70°, and 35 + 30 = 65°.
Medium

A right triangle has legs 5 cm and 12 cm. Find the hypotenuse.

Show Answer
c² = 5² + 12² = 25 + 144 = 169. c = √169 = 13 cm.
Hard

An exterior angle of a triangle is 120°. The two interior opposite angles are equal. Find all angles of the triangle.

Show Answer
Exterior angle = sum of the two interior opposite angles. So 120° = x + x = 2x, giving x = 60°. The two interior opposite angles are each 60°. The third angle (adjacent to the exterior angle) = 180° − 120° = 60°. All three angles are 60° (equilateral triangle).
Hard

The sides of a triangle are in the ratio 3:4:5 and its perimeter is 48 cm. Find the sides and check if it is a right triangle.

Show Answer
Let the sides be 3x, 4x, and 5x. Perimeter = 3x + 4x + 5x = 12x = 48, so x = 4. The sides are 12 cm, 16 cm, and 20 cm. Check: 12² + 16² = 144 + 256 = 400 = 20². Since the Pythagoras theorem holds, it is a right triangle.

Common Mistakes

  • Thinking the angle sum of a triangle is 360° — it's 180° (360° is for quadrilaterals).
  • Not checking ALL three pairs when testing the triangle inequality.
  • Confusing median (to midpoint) with altitude (perpendicular to side).
  • Applying Pythagoras theorem to non-right triangles — it only works for right triangles!

Exercise 6.1 — Angle Sum Property (8 Questions)

Q1. Two angles of a triangle are 50° and 60°. Find the third angle.

Show Solution
Step 1: Angle sum property: all angles add to 180°.
Step 2: Third angle = 180° − 50° − 60°
Step 3: = 180° − 110° = 70°

Q2. The angles of a triangle are in the ratio 2:3:4. Find all three angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let the angles be 2x, 3x, and 4x.
Step 2: Sum = 180°: 2x + 3x + 4x = 180
Step 3: 9x = 180 → x = 20
Step 4: Angles: 2(20) = 40°, 3(20) = 60°, 4(20) = 80°
Check: 40 + 60 + 80 = 180 ✓

Q3. The angles of a triangle are (x+10)°, (2x)°, and (x+30)°. Find all angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Sum = 180°: (x+10) + 2x + (x+30) = 180
Step 2: Simplify: 4x + 40 = 180
Step 3: 4x = 140 → x = 35
Step 4: Angles: 35+10 = 45°, 2(35) = 70°, 35+30 = 65°
Check: 45 + 70 + 65 = 180 ✓

Q4. An exterior angle of a triangle is 110°. One interior opposite angle is 45°. Find the other.

Show Solution
Step 1: Exterior angle = sum of two remote interior angles.
Step 2: 110° = 45° + other angle
Step 3: Other angle = 110° − 45° = 65°

Q5. An exterior angle of a triangle is 120°. The two interior opposite angles are equal. Find all angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let each interior opposite angle = x
Step 2: Exterior angle = sum: 120 = x + x = 2x
Step 3: x = 60°
Step 4: Third angle (adjacent to exterior) = 180 − 120 = 60°
Answer: All three angles are 60° (equilateral triangle!)

Q6. Can a triangle have: (a) two right angles? (b) two obtuse angles? (c) all angles less than 60°?

Show Solution
(a) Two right angles? 90 + 90 = 180. Third = 0°. No! (angle can’t be 0)
(b) Two obtuse? Min: 91 + 91 = 182 > 180. No!
(c) All < 60°? Max: 59 + 59 + 59 = 177 < 180. No! Sum must = 180.

Q7. Find the unknown angle in a right triangle where one angle is 35°.

Show Solution
Step 1: Right triangle has one 90° angle.
Step 2: Third angle = 180 − 90 − 35 = 55°

Q8. In an isosceles triangle, the vertex angle is 80°. Find the base angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Isosceles = two equal base angles. Let each = x.
Step 2: 80 + x + x = 180 → 2x = 100 → x = 50°
Base angles: 50° each
Exercise 6.2 — Triangle Inequality (5 Questions)

Q1. Can sides 3, 4, 8 form a triangle?

Show Solution
Step 1: Check all three pairs (sum of any two > third):
Step 2: 3 + 4 = 7. Is 7 > 8? No!
Answer: Cannot form a triangle. (Failed at first check itself.)

Q2. Can sides 5, 7, 11 form a triangle?

Show Solution
Check 1: 5 + 7 = 12 > 11 ✓
Check 2: 5 + 11 = 16 > 7 ✓
Check 3: 7 + 11 = 18 > 5 ✓
All three pass. Yes, it can form a triangle!

Q3. Two sides of a triangle are 5 cm and 9 cm. What are possible lengths for the third side?

Show Solution
Step 1: Third side must be less than 5 + 9 = 14
Step 2: Third side must be more than 9 − 5 = 4
Answer: Third side must be between 4 cm and 14 cm (not including 4 and 14).

Q4. Can sides 2, 3, 4 form a triangle? What about 1, 1, 3?

Show Solution
2, 3, 4: 2+3=5>4 ✓, 2+4=6>3 ✓, 3+4=7>2 ✓. Yes!
1, 1, 3: 1+1=2. Is 2 > 3? No! Cannot form a triangle.

Q5. The sides of a triangle are in ratio 3:4:5 and perimeter is 48 cm. Find the sides and check if it’s a right triangle.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let sides = 3x, 4x, 5x
Step 2: Perimeter: 3x + 4x + 5x = 48 → 12x = 48 → x = 4
Step 3: Sides: 12 cm, 16 cm, 20 cm
Step 4: Check Pythagoras: 12² + 16² = 144 + 256 = 400 = 20² ✓
Yes, it is a right triangle!
Exercise 6.3 — Pythagoras Theorem (5 Questions)

Q1. A right triangle has legs 3 cm and 4 cm. Find the hypotenuse.

Show Solution
Step 1: Pythagoras: c² = a² + b²
Step 2: c² = 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25
Step 3: c = √25 = 5 cm

Q2. Hypotenuse = 13 cm, one leg = 5 cm. Find the other leg.

Show Solution
Step 1: a² + b² = c² → a² + 5² = 13²
Step 2: a² + 25 = 169
Step 3: a² = 169 − 25 = 144
Step 4: a = √144 = 12 cm

Q3. A ladder 10 m long leans against a wall. Its foot is 6 m from the wall. How high does it reach?

Show Solution
Step 1: The ladder, wall, and ground form a right triangle.
Step 2: Ladder = hypotenuse = 10 m. Ground = 6 m. Wall height = ?
Step 3: h² + 6² = 10² → h² + 36 = 100
Step 4: h² = 64 → h = √64 = 8 m

Q4. Check if 5, 12, 13 is a Pythagorean triple.

Show Solution
Step 1: Check: does 5² + 12² = 13²?
Step 2: 25 + 144 = 169. And 13² = 169.
Step 3: 169 = 169 ✓ Yes, it’s a Pythagorean triple!

Q5. A field is 40 m long and 30 m wide. What is the length of the diagonal?

Show Solution
Step 1: The diagonal divides the rectangle into two right triangles.
Step 2: d² = 40² + 30² = 1600 + 900 = 2500
Step 3: d = √2500 = 50 m
Exercise 6.4 — Mixed Problems (6 Questions)

Q1. Classify: (a) sides 6,6,6 (b) sides 5,5,3 (c) sides 3,4,6

Show Solution
(a) All sides equal → Equilateral
(b) Two sides equal (5,5) → Isosceles
(c) All sides different → Scalene

Q2. A right triangle has hypotenuse 15 cm and one leg 9 cm. Find the other leg.

Show Solution
Step 1: a² + 9² = 15²
Step 2: a² + 81 = 225
Step 3: a² = 144
Step 4: a = 12 cm

Q3. The equal sides of an isosceles triangle are 8 cm each and the base is 6 cm. Find the altitude from the vertex to the base.

Show Solution
Step 1: The altitude bisects the base. So each half = 6/2 = 3 cm.
Step 2: Now we have a right triangle: hypotenuse = 8, base = 3.
Step 3: h² + 3² = 8² → h² + 9 = 64 → h² = 55
Step 4: h = √55 ≈ 7.42 cm

Q4. Find the perimeter of a right triangle with legs 6 cm and 8 cm.

Show Solution
Step 1: Find hypotenuse: c² = 6² + 8² = 36 + 64 = 100 → c = 10 cm
Step 2: Perimeter = 6 + 8 + 10 = 24 cm

Q5. The three angles of a triangle are (3x−5)°, (x+15)°, and (2x−10)°. Find x and all angles. Is it acute, right, or obtuse?

Show Solution
Step 1: Sum = 180: (3x−5) + (x+15) + (2x−10) = 180
Step 2: 6x + 0 = 180 → x = 30
Step 3: Angles: 3(30)−5 = 85°, 30+15 = 45°, 2(30)−10 = 50°
Step 4: All angles < 90° → Acute triangle
Check: 85 + 45 + 50 = 180 ✓

Q6. The exterior angle of a triangle is 130°. The interior opposite angles are in ratio 2:3. Find all three interior angles.

Show Solution
Step 1: Exterior angle = sum of remote interior: 130 = 2x + 3x = 5x
Step 2: x = 26
Step 3: Two interior angles: 2(26) = 52°, 3(26) = 78°
Step 4: Third = 180 − 130 = 50°
Check: 52 + 78 + 50 = 180 ✓. And 52 + 78 = 130 (exterior) ✓

Common Mistakes

  • Thinking angle sum is 360°
    Angles of triangle add to 360°
    Triangle = 180°. Quadrilateral = 360°.
  • Not checking ALL three pairs for triangle inequality
    3+4=7 > 5 ✓, so 3,4,8 works
    Must check: 3+4=7 vs 8. 7 is NOT > 8. Fails!
  • Applying Pythagoras to non-right triangles
    Any triangle: a² + b² = c²
    Pythagoras ONLY works for right triangles!
  • Confusing median and altitude
    They’re the same thing
    Median = to midpoint. Altitude = perpendicular to base. Same only in equilateral!
