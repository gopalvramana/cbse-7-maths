What are Algebraic Expressions?

Real-world connection: “The cost of x notebooks at Rs 30 each” = 30x. “5 more than twice a number” = 2n + 5. Algebraic expressions let us write maths in a short, powerful way!
  • An algebraic expression is made up of variables (letters like x, y), constants (numbers like 5, −3), and operations (+, −, ×).
  • Examples: 3x + 5,   2a² − 4b + 7,   −y
  • Monomial = 1 term (e.g., 5x, −3y², 7)
  • Binomial = 2 terms (e.g., x + 5, 3a − 2b)
  • Trinomial = 3 terms (e.g., x² + 2x − 3)

Terms and Coefficients

Breaking down an expression: In the expression 4x² + 3y − 7:

• It has 3 terms: 4x²,   3y,   −7
• The terms are separated by + and − signs
• 4x²: coefficient = 4, variable = x²
• 3y: coefficient = 3, variable = y
• −7: this is a constant term (no variable, just a number)
Exam tip: The sign (+/−) before a term belongs TO that term. In 5x − 3y, the terms are +5x and −3y. The coefficient of y is −3 (not 3!).

Like Terms and Unlike Terms

Like terms = same variable(s) raised to the same power. Only like terms can be combined!
Why can’t we add unlike terms?
Think of it like fruits: 3 apples + 2 apples = 5 apples. But 3 apples + 2 oranges = still 3 apples and 2 oranges (can’t combine!). Same with algebra: 3x + 2x = 5x, but 3x + 2y stays as 3x + 2y.

Adding and Subtracting Expressions

Rule: Combine like terms only!
Adding like terms: ax + bx = (a + b)x.   Subtracting: ax − bx = (a − b)x.

Example: Add (3x + 5y − 2) + (2x − 3y + 7)

Step 1: Group like terms together.
x terms: 3x + 2x.   y terms: 5y + (−3y).   Constants: −2 + 7.
Step 2: Add each group.
3x + 2x = (3+2)x = 5x.
5y + (−3y) = (5−3)y = 2y.
−2 + 7 = 5.
Answer: 5x + 2y + 5
Subtracting expressions — the SIGN TRAP!
When subtracting, change the sign of EVERY term in the expression being subtracted:
(7a − 2b) − (3a + 4b) = 7a − 2b − 3a − 4b (both signs changed!)
= (7−3)a + (−2−4)b = 4a − 6b

Finding the Value of an Expression

Substitute = replace each variable with its given number value, then calculate.

Example: Find the value of 3a² − 2a + 5 when a = 2.

Step 1: Write the expression: 3a² − 2a + 5
Step 2: Replace every ‘a’ with 2:
= 3(2)² − 2(2) + 5
Step 3: Calculate powers first (BODMAS):
(2)² = 2 × 2 = 4. So: = 3 × 4 − 2 × 2 + 5
Step 4: Do multiplications:
3 × 4 = 12.   2 × 2 = 4. So: = 12 − 4 + 5
Step 5: Add/subtract left to right:
12 − 4 = 8. Then 8 + 5 = 13

NCERT Exercise Solutions — All Questions Solved

Exercise 12.1 — Terms, Coefficients, Types (8 Questions)

Q1. Identify the terms in: (a) 5x + 3y − 8   (b) 2a²b − 3ab + 4

Show Solution
(a) 5x + 3y − 8
Step 1: Terms are separated by + and − signs.
Step 2: Term 1 = 5x (positive, before the +)
Term 2 = 3y (positive, after the +)
Term 3 = −8 (negative sign belongs to this term)
Answer: 3 terms — 5x, 3y, −8
(b) 2a²b − 3ab + 4
Step 1: Term 1 = 2a²b
Term 2 = −3ab (the minus sign is part of this term!)
Term 3 = 4 (constant)
Answer: 3 terms — 2a²b, −3ab, 4

Q2. Write the coefficient of x in: (a) 7x (b) −3xy (c) x (d) −x/2

Show Solution
The coefficient is the number multiplied with the variable.
(a) 7x: The number multiplying x is 7.
(b) −3xy: The coefficient of x is −3y (everything multiplied with x). But if they ask just “coefficient of x” ignoring other variables, it’s −3.
(c) x: x means 1 × x. So coefficient = 1.
(d) −x/2: This means (−1/2) × x. So coefficient = −1/2.

Q3. Classify as monomial, binomial, or trinomial: (a) 5x (b) x + y (c) x² + 2x − 3 (d) 4ab (e) a + b − c

Show Solution
Count the terms in each expression:
(a) 5x — 1 term → Monomial
(b) x + y — 2 terms → Binomial
(c) x² + 2x − 3 — 3 terms → Trinomial
(d) 4ab — 1 term (4 × a × b is one product) → Monomial
(e) a + b − c — 3 terms → Trinomial

Q4. Identify like terms: 5x, 3y, −2x, 7, 4y, −x

Show Solution
Step 1: Group by variable and power:
• x terms: 5x, −2x, −x (all have variable x to power 1)
• y terms: 3y, 4y (both have variable y to power 1)
• Constants: 7 (no variable)
Answer:
Like terms: {5x, −2x, −x} and {3y, 4y}
7 has no like term (it’s the only constant).

Q5. Add: (4a + 3b) + (2a − 5b)

Show Solution
Step 1: Group like terms.
a terms: 4a + 2a. b terms: 3b + (−5b).
Step 2: Add a terms: 4 + 2 = 6, so 6a.
Add b terms: 3 + (−5) = 3 − 5 = −2, so −2b.
Answer: 6a − 2b

Q6. Subtract (3a + 4b − 2) from (7a − 2b + 5).

Show Solution
Step 1: Write it out. “Subtract X from Y” means Y − X.
= (7a − 2b + 5) − (3a + 4b − 2)
Step 2: Open the bracket. When we subtract, EVERY sign inside the bracket flips:
+3a becomes −3a
+4b becomes −4b
−2 becomes +2
So: = 7a − 2b + 5 − 3a − 4b + 2
Step 3: Group like terms.
a terms: 7a − 3a = (7−3)a = 4a
b terms: −2b − 4b = (−2−4)b = −6b
Constants: 5 + 2 = 7
Answer: 4a − 6b + 7

Q7. Find the value of 5x − 3y when x = 2 and y = −1.

Show Solution
Step 1: Write the expression: 5x − 3y
Step 2: Replace x with 2 and y with −1:
= 5(2) − 3(−1)
Step 3: Calculate each multiplication:
5 × 2 = 10
3 × (−1) = −3 (positive × negative = negative)
Step 4: Now: 10 − (−3). Subtracting a negative = adding!
= 10 + 3 = 13

Q8. Simplify: (5x² + 3x − 7) + (2x² − 4x + 3) − (x² + 2x − 1)

Show Solution
Step 1: Open the last bracket (subtraction flips all signs):
= 5x² + 3x − 7 + 2x² − 4x + 3 − x² − 2x + 1
Step 2: Group like terms.
x² terms: 5x² + 2x² − x². Add coefficients: 5 + 2 − 1 = 6. So: 6x².
x terms: 3x − 4x − 2x. Add coefficients: 3 − 4 − 2 = −3. So: −3x.
Constants: −7 + 3 + 1. Calculate: −7 + 3 = −4. Then −4 + 1 = −3.
Answer: 6x² − 3x − 3

Step 2: b terms: 3b + (−5b) = (3−5)b = −2b.
Answer: 6a − 2b

4. Find value of 5x − 3y when x=2, y=−1.

Show Answer
Step 1: Substitute: 5(2) − 3(−1)
Step 2: = 10 − (−3) = 10 + 3 = 13
Hard

5. Simplify: (2x²+3xy−y²) + (x²−5xy+3y²) + (−x²+xy−y²)

Show Answer
Step 1: Group x² terms: 2+1−1 = 2 → 2x²
Step 2: Group xy terms: 3−5+1 = −1 → −xy
Step 3: Group y² terms: −1+3−1 = 1 → y²
Answer: 2x² − xy + y²
Hard

6. If P = 3a²−5a+7 and Q = a²+2a−3, find 2P−Q.

Show Answer
Step 1: Find 2P: multiply each term of P by 2.
2 × 3a² = 6a², 2 × (−5a) = −10a, 2 × 7 = 14.
So 2P = 6a² − 10a + 14.

Step 2: Subtract Q (flip all signs of Q):
2P − Q = (6a²−10a+14) − (a²+2a−3)
= 6a²−10a+14 − a² − 2a + 3

Step 3: Group like terms:
a²: 6−1 = 5 → 5a²
a: −10−2 = −12 → −12a
Constants: 14+3 = 17

Answer: 5a² − 12a + 17

HOTS Advanced

HardHOTS

1. If the perimeter of a triangle is (7x+3y+5) and two sides are (2x+y+1) and (3x+y+2), find the third side.

Show Answer
Step 1: Third side = Perimeter − Side1 − Side2
= (7x+3y+5) − (2x+y+1) − (3x+y+2)

Step 2: Open brackets (flip signs when subtracting):
= 7x+3y+5 −2x−y−1 −3x−y−2

Step 3: Group like terms:
x: 7−2−3 = 2 → 2x
y: 3−1−1 = 1 → y
Constants: 5−1−2 = 2

Answer: Third side = 2x + y + 2
HardHOTS

2. Find the value of 2x²+3xy−y² when x=1 and y=−2.

Show Answer
Step 1: Substitute x=1, y=−2:
= 2(1)² + 3(1)(−2) − (−2)²

Step 2: Calculate powers:
(1)² = 1. (−2)² = (−2)×(−2) = 4 (negative × negative = positive!)

Step 3: Multiply:
2 × 1 = 2.   3 × 1 × (−2) = −6.

Step 4: = 2 + (−6) − 4 = 2 − 6 − 4 = −8
HardHOTS

3. What should be added to 3x² + 2x − 5 to get 5x² − x + 3?

Show Answer
Step 1: Let the expression to add = E.
(3x²+2x−5) + E = (5x²−x+3)

Step 2: E = (5x²−x+3) − (3x²+2x−5)
= 5x²−x+3 −3x²−2x+5

Step 3: Group like terms:
x²: 5−3 = 2 → 2x²
x: −1−2 = −3 → −3x
Constants: 3+5 = 8

Answer: 2x² − 3x + 8

Common Mistakes

  • Adding unlike terms
    3x + 2y = 5xy
    3x + 2y = 3x + 2y (stays as is! Different variables can’t be combined)
  • Forgetting to flip ALL signs when subtracting
    (5a+3b) − (2a+b) = 5a+3b − 2a+b = 3a+4b
    (5a+3b) − (2a+b) = 5a+3b −2a−b = 3a+2b
    The +b inside bracket becomes −b!
  • Thinking x and x² are like terms
    3x + 2x² = 5x²
    3x and 2x² are unlike terms (different powers!). They stay as 2x² + 3x.
  • Forgetting that x means 1x
    Coefficient of x in the term “x” is 0
    x = 1 × x. Coefficient = 1, not 0!
  • Not following BODMAS when substituting
    3a² when a=2: 3×2² = 6² = 36
    Power first: 2² = 4. THEN multiply: 3×4 = 12.
    (The ² applies to a only, not to 3a!)
