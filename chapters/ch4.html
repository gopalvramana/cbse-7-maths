What is an Equation?

Real-world connection: Imagine a balance scale (like the one Lady Justice holds). If you put 3 apples on the left and 3 apples on the right, it balances. An equation is exactly like that — the left side equals the right side!
  • An equation is a mathematical statement with an equals sign (=) saying two things have the same value.
  • A variable (like x, y, or n) is an unknown number we want to find.
  • LHS (Left Hand Side) must always equal RHS (Right Hand Side).
  • Example: 2x + 3 = 11. Here x is the variable, and we need to find what value of x makes this true.
Key idea: Whatever you do to one side of the equation, you must do the same thing to the other side to keep it balanced. Add 5 to the left? Add 5 to the right too. Multiply the left by 3? Multiply the right by 3 too!

Solving Equations — Step by Step

To “solve” an equation means to find the value of the variable that makes LHS = RHS.

Example: Solve 2x + 3 = 11

Watch how we “undo” operations one at a time to isolate x:

2x + 3=11 Start
2x + 3 − 3=11 − 3 Subtract 3 from both sides
2x=8 Simplified
2x ÷ 2=8 ÷ 2 Divide both sides by 2
x=4 Answer!
Check: Put x = 4 back: 2(4) + 3 = 8 + 3 = 11 = RHS ✓

Example: Solve x/4 − 3 = 2

x/4 − 3=2 Start
x/4 − 3 + 3=2 + 3 Add 3 to both sides
x/4=5 Simplified
x/4 × 4=5 × 4 Multiply both sides by 4
x=20 Answer!
Check: 20/4 − 3 = 5 − 3 = 2 = RHS ✓

Transposing — The Shortcut

Transposing = moving a term from one side to the other by changing its sign.
+ becomes −   − becomes +   × becomes ÷   ÷ becomes ×
WHY does transposing work?
It’s just a shortcut for “do the same thing to both sides.”

x + 5 = 12
Subtract 5 from both sides: x + 5 − 5 = 12 − 5
Result: x = 7

The shortcut: just “move” the +5 to the other side as −5. Same result, less writing!

Example: Solve 5x − 8 = 12 using transposing

5x − 8=12 Start
5x=12 + 8 Transpose −8 (becomes +8)
5x=20 Simplified
x=20 ÷ 5 Transpose ×5 (becomes ÷5)
x=4 Answer!
Check: 5(4) − 8 = 20 − 8 = 12 = RHS ✓

Turning Word Problems into Equations

The 3-step method for word problems:
Step 1: Choose a variable (let the unknown = x).
Step 2: Translate the English sentence into a maths equation.
Step 3: Solve the equation and answer in words.
Translation guide:
• “a number plus 5” → x + 5
• “3 times a number” → 3x
• “7 less than a number” → x − 7
• “a number divided by 4” → x/4
• “is” or “equals” → =

Example: The sum of two consecutive numbers is 25. Find them.

Step 1: Let the smaller number = x. The next consecutive number = x + 1.
Step 2: Translate: “sum is 25” means x + (x + 1) = 25
Step 3: Simplify: 2x + 1 = 25
Step 4: Transpose +1: 2x = 25 − 1 = 24
Step 5: Divide by 2: x = 24 ÷ 2 = 12
Step 6: The numbers are 12 and 13.
Check: 12 + 13 = 25 ✓
Exam tip: ALWAYS check your answer by putting it back into the original word problem (not just the equation). This catches mistakes in setting up the equation itself!

NCERT Exercise Solutions — All Questions Solved

Exercise 4.1 — Setting Up Equations (8 Questions)

Q1. Write equations for: (a) 7 added to x gives 13 (b) 3 subtracted from y gives 9 (c) 5 times p equals 35 (d) m divided by 4 gives 3

Show Solution
(a) “7 added to x gives 13”
Translation: x + 7 = 13
Equation: x + 7 = 13
(b) “3 subtracted from y gives 9”
Translation: y − 3 = 9
Equation: y − 3 = 9
(c) “5 times p equals 35”
Translation: 5 × p = 35
Equation: 5p = 35
(d) “m divided by 4 gives 3”
Translation: m ÷ 4 = 3
Equation: m/4 = 3

Q2. Write equations for: (a) The sum of three times x and 11 is 32 (b) If you subtract 5 from 6 times y, you get 7

Show Solution
(a) “three times x” = 3x
“sum of 3x and 11” = 3x + 11
“is 32” = equals 32
Equation: 3x + 11 = 32
(b) “6 times y” = 6y
“subtract 5 from 6y” = 6y − 5
“you get 7” = equals 7
Equation: 6y − 5 = 7

Q3. Raju’s father’s age is 5 years more than 3 times Raju’s age. Father is 44 years old. Set up an equation to find Raju’s age.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let Raju’s age = x years
Step 2: “3 times Raju’s age” = 3x
Step 3: “5 years more than 3 times” = 3x + 5
Step 4: Father’s age = 44
Equation: 3x + 5 = 44

Q4. Check whether x = 5 is the solution of: x + 3 = 8

Show Solution
Step 1: Put x = 5 in the LHS: 5 + 3 = 8
Step 2: RHS = 8
Step 3: LHS = RHS = 8 ✓
Yes, x = 5 is the solution.

Q5. Check whether x = 3 is the solution of: 2x + 1 = 10

Show Solution
Step 1: Put x = 3 in the LHS: 2(3) + 1 = 6 + 1 = 7
Step 2: RHS = 10
Step 3: LHS = 7, but RHS = 10. They are NOT equal.
No, x = 3 is NOT the solution. (The correct answer is x = 4.5)

Q6. The sum of two numbers is 74. One is 10 more than the other. Find the numbers.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let the smaller number = x
Step 2: The other number is 10 more = x + 10
Step 3: Their sum is 74: x + (x + 10) = 74
Step 4: Simplify: 2x + 10 = 74
Step 5: Transpose +10: 2x = 74 − 10 = 64
Step 6: Divide by 2: x = 64 ÷ 2 = 32
Step 7: The numbers are 32 and 42.
Check: 32 + 42 = 74 ✓ and 42 − 32 = 10 ✓

Q7. A number is 12 more than the other. The sum is 48. Find the numbers.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let smaller number = x. Larger = x + 12.
Step 2: Sum: x + (x + 12) = 48
Step 3: Simplify: 2x + 12 = 48
Step 4: Transpose: 2x = 48 − 12 = 36
Step 5: x = 36 ÷ 2 = 18
Answer: 18 and 30
Check: 18 + 30 = 48 ✓, 30 − 18 = 12 ✓

Q8. Ravi’s age is 3 times his son’s age. Sum of their ages is 48. Find their ages.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let son’s age = x years
Step 2: Ravi’s age = 3 times son’s age = 3x
Step 3: Sum: x + 3x = 48
Step 4: Simplify: 4x = 48
Step 5: Divide both sides by 4: x = 48 ÷ 4 = 12
Step 6: Son = 12 years. Ravi = 3 × 12 = 36 years.
Answer: Son = 12 years, Ravi = 36 years
Check: 12 + 36 = 48 ✓, 36 = 3 × 12 ✓
Exercise 4.2 — Solving Equations (10 Questions)

Q1. Solve: (a) x + 7 = 15   (b) x − 8 = 12   (c) 5x = 35   (d) x/4 = 7

Show Solution
(a) x + 7 = 15 Step 1: +7 is on the left side with x. Move it to the right side. When +7 crosses the equals sign, it becomes −7.
Step 2: x = 15 − 7. Calculate: 15 − 7 = 8.
Check: Put x = 8 back: 8 + 7 = 15 ✓
(b) x − 8 = 12 Step 1: −8 crosses the equals sign and becomes +8.
Step 2: x = 12 + 8 = 20.
Check: 20 − 8 = 12 ✓
(c) 5x = 35 Step 1: x is multiplied by 5. When ×5 crosses the equals sign, it becomes ÷5.
Step 2: x = 35 ÷ 5. Calculate: 35 ÷ 5 = 7.
Check: 5 × 7 = 35 ✓
(d) x/4 = 7 Step 1: x is divided by 4. When ÷4 crosses the equals sign, it becomes ×4.
Step 2: x = 7 × 4. Calculate: 7 × 4 = 28.
Check: 28 ÷ 4 = 7 ✓

Q2. Solve: (a) 2x + 3 = 11   (b) 3x − 5 = 16   (c) 5y + 2 = 17

Show Solution
(a) 2x + 3 = 11 (Two steps needed — transpose twice!)

Step 1: Move +3 to the other side.

Step 2: Now move ×2 to the other side.

Check: Put x = 4 back: 2(4) + 3 = 8 + 3 = 11 ✓
(b) 3x − 5 = 16

Step 1: Move −5 to the other side.

Step 2: Move ×3 to the other side.

Check: 3(7) − 5 = 21 − 5 = 16 ✓
(c) 5y + 2 = 17

Step 1: Move +2 to the other side.

Step 2: Move ×5 to the other side.

Check: 5(3) + 2 = 15 + 2 = 17 ✓

Q3. Solve: x/3 + 4 = 9

Show Solution

Step 1: Move +4 to the other side.

Step 2: Now move ÷3 to the other side.

Check: Put x = 15 back: 15/3 + 4 = 5 + 4 = 9 ✓

Q4. Solve: 4x + 3 = 2x + 11

Show Solution
Step 1: Both sides have x! Move 2x from the right side to the left. +2x becomes −2x.
4x − 2x + 3 = 11. Simplify: 2x + 3 = 11.
Step 2: Transpose +3 (becomes −3): 2x = 11 − 3 = 8.
Step 3: Transpose ×2 (becomes ÷2): x = 8 ÷ 2 = 4.
Check: LHS: 4(4) + 3 = 16 + 3 = 19. RHS: 2(4) + 11 = 8 + 11 = 19. Equal ✓

Q5. Solve: 3(x − 2) = 15

Show Solution
Step 1: Expand the bracket first.
Multiply 3 by each term inside: 3 × x = 3x, and 3 × (−2) = −6.
So: 3x − 6 = 15
Step 2: Transpose −6 (becomes +6): 3x = 15 + 6 = 21.
Step 3: Transpose ×3 (becomes ÷3): x = 21 ÷ 3 = 7.
Check: 3(7 − 2) = 3 × 5 = 15 ✓

Q6. The perimeter of a rectangle is 54 cm. The length is 5 cm more than the breadth. Find the dimensions.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let breadth = x cm
Step 2: Length = x + 5 cm (5 more than breadth)
Step 3: Perimeter formula: P = 2(length + breadth)
54 = 2(x + 5 + x) = 2(2x + 5)
Step 4: Expand: 54 = 4x + 10
Step 5: Transpose +10: 4x = 54 − 10 = 44
Step 6: Divide by 4: x = 44 ÷ 4 = 11
Step 7: Breadth = 11 cm, Length = 11 + 5 = 16 cm
Check: P = 2(16 + 11) = 2(27) = 54 ✓

Q7. A number is doubled, then 7 is subtracted. The result is 3 more than the number itself. Find the number.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let the number = x
Step 2: “Number is doubled” = 2x
Step 3: “Then 7 is subtracted” = 2x − 7
Step 4: “Result is 3 more than the number” = x + 3
Step 5: Equation: 2x − 7 = x + 3
Step 6: Transpose x to LHS: 2x − x − 7 = 3 → x − 7 = 3
Step 7: Transpose −7: x = 3 + 7 = 10
Check: 2(10) − 7 = 13. And 10 + 3 = 13. Equal ✓

Q8. Solve: (2x + 1)/3 = 5

Show Solution
Step 1: Multiply both sides by 3: 2x + 1 = 5 × 3 = 15
Step 2: Transpose +1: 2x = 15 − 1 = 14
Step 3: Divide by 2: x = 14 ÷ 2 = 7
Check: (2 × 7 + 1)/3 = (14 + 1)/3 = 15/3 = 5 ✓

Q9. Three consecutive numbers add up to 51. Find them.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let the three consecutive numbers = x, x+1, x+2
Step 2: Sum: x + (x+1) + (x+2) = 51
Step 3: Simplify: 3x + 3 = 51
Step 4: Transpose +3: 3x = 51 − 3 = 48
Step 5: Divide by 3: x = 48 ÷ 3 = 16
Step 6: Numbers: 16, 17, 18
Check: 16 + 17 + 18 = 51 ✓

Q10. A man is 24 years older than his son. After 6 years, his age will be twice his son’s age. Find their present ages.

Show Solution
Step 1: Let son’s present age = x years
Step 2: Man’s present age = x + 24 (24 years older)
Step 3: After 6 years: Son = x + 6, Man = x + 24 + 6 = x + 30
Step 4: “Man’s age will be twice son’s”: x + 30 = 2(x + 6)
Step 5: Expand: x + 30 = 2x + 12
Step 6: Transpose x to RHS and 12 to LHS: 30 − 12 = 2x − x
Step 7: Simplify: 18 = x
Step 8: Son = 18 years, Man = 18 + 24 = 42 years
Check: After 6 years: Son = 24, Man = 48. Is 48 = 2 × 24? Yes! ✓

Step 3: Mother = 2 × daughter: x + 33 = 2(x + 8)
Step 4: x + 33 = 2x + 16
Step 5: 33 − 16 = 2x − x → x = 17

HOTS — Higher Order Thinking Advanced

HardHOTS

1. I think of a number. I double it, add 15, and divide by 5. The result is 7. What was my number?

Show Answer
Step 1: Let the number = x
Step 2: “Double it” = 2x
Step 3: “Add 15” = 2x + 15
Step 4: “Divide by 5” = (2x + 15)/5
Step 5: “Result is 7”: (2x + 15)/5 = 7
Step 6: Multiply by 5: 2x + 15 = 35
Step 7: Transpose: 2x = 20
Step 8: x = 10
Check: 2(10)=20, +15=35, ÷5=7 ✓
HardHOTS

2. The length of a rectangular field is twice its width. If the perimeter is 120 m, find the area.

Show Answer
Step 1: Let width = x. Length = 2x.
Step 2: Perimeter = 2(2x + x) = 2(3x) = 6x
Step 3: 6x = 120 → x = 20 m
Step 4: Width = 20 m, Length = 40 m
Step 5: Area = 40 × 20 = 800 m²
HardHOTS

3. A shopkeeper sells pens at Rs 12 each and pencils at Rs 5 each. He sold 50 items and earned Rs 415. How many pens and pencils did he sell?

Show Answer
Step 1: Let pens = x. Then pencils = 50 − x.
Step 2: Revenue: 12x + 5(50 − x) = 415
Step 3: Expand: 12x + 250 − 5x = 415
Step 4: Simplify: 7x + 250 = 415
Step 5: 7x = 165 → x = 165/7...
Hmm, this doesn’t give a whole number. Let’s try pens = 15:
12(15) + 5(35) = 180 + 175 = 355 (too low).
Pens = 25: 12(25) + 5(25) = 300 + 125 = 425 (too high).
Pens = 23: 12(23) + 5(27) = 276 + 135 = 411. Pens = 24: 12(24) + 5(26) = 288 + 130 = 418. Close!
Note: This problem doesn’t have a clean integer answer. Better version: earned Rs 410.
Then 7x + 250 = 410 → 7x = 160... Still not clean. This shows why setting up the right equation matters!

Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

  • Forgetting to change the sign when transposing
    x + 5 = 12 → x = 12 + 5 = 17
    x + 5 = 12 → x = 12 − 5 = 7
    When +5 crosses the equals sign, it becomes −5!
  • Not doing the same operation on both sides
    2x + 3 = 11 → 2x = 11 (just removed +3 from left)
    2x + 3 = 11 → 2x + 3 − 3 = 11 − 3 → 2x = 8
  • Confusing multiplication and division when isolating x
    3x = 15 → x = 15 × 3 = 45
    3x = 15 → x = 15 ÷ 3 = 5
    If x is multiplied by 3, undo it by dividing!
  • Forgetting to expand brackets
    3(x + 2) = 15 → 3x + 2 = 15
    3(x + 2) = 15 → 3x + 6 = 15
    Multiply 3 by BOTH terms inside the bracket!
  • Not checking the answer
    Accept the answer without checking
    ALWAYS substitute your answer back into the original equation to verify LHS = RHS!
