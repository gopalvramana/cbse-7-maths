Chapter 4 · Algebra

Simple Equations

Summary

  • An equation is a statement with an equal sign (=) that says two things are the same.
  • A variable (like x) is an unknown number we want to find.
  • LHS (Left Hand Side) must always equal RHS (Right Hand Side).
  • To solve: get the variable alone on one side by doing the same operation to both sides.
  • Transposing means moving a number to the other side — but you flip its sign (+ becomes −, × becomes ÷).
  • You can check your answer by putting it back into the original equation.
  • Word problems can be turned into equations by using a variable for the unknown quantity.

Key Formulas

If x + a = b, then x = b − a
If x − a = b, then x = b + a
If ax = b, then x = b ÷ a
If x/a = b, then x = a × b

Worked Examples

Example 1: Solve 3x + 5 = 20

Step 1: Transpose +5 to RHS: 3x = 20 − 5 = 15
Step 2: Divide both sides by 3: x = 15 ÷ 3 = 5
Check: 3(5) + 5 = 15 + 5 = 20 ✓

Example 2: Solve x/4 − 3 = 2

Step 1: Transpose −3 to RHS: x/4 = 2 + 3 = 5
Step 2: Multiply both sides by 4: x = 5 × 4 = 20
Check: 20/4 − 3 = 5 − 3 = 2 ✓

Example 3: Word Problem

The sum of two consecutive numbers is 25. Find them.

Step 1: Let the smaller number = x. Then the next one = x + 1
Step 2: Equation: x + (x + 1) = 25 → 2x + 1 = 25
Step 3: 2x = 24, so x = 12
Answer: The numbers are 12 and 13

Practice Problems

Easy

Solve: x + 7 = 15

Show Answer
x = 15 − 7 = 8
Easy

Solve: 5x = 35

Show Answer
x = 35 ÷ 5 = 7
Medium

Solve: 2x − 3 = 11

Show Answer
2x = 11 + 3 = 14. x = 14 ÷ 2 = 7
Medium

Solve: x/3 + 4 = 9

Show Answer
x/3 = 9 − 4 = 5. x = 5 × 3 = 15
Hard

A number is multiplied by 3 and then 7 is added. The result is 28. Find the number.

Show Answer
3x + 7 = 28 → 3x = 21 → x = 7
Hard

Ravi's age is 3 times his son's age. If the sum of their ages is 48, find their ages.

Show Answer
Let son's age = x. Ravi's age = 3x. x + 3x = 48 → 4x = 48 → x = 12. Son = 12 years, Ravi = 36 years

Common Mistakes

  • Forgetting to change the sign when transposing (e.g., +5 must become −5 on the other side).
  • Not doing the same operation on both sides of the equation.
  • Confusing multiplication and division when isolating the variable.
  • Forgetting to check the answer by substituting back into the original equation.
