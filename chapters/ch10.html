Chapter 10 · Geometry

Practical Geometry

Summary

  • We can construct a unique triangle when given enough information about its sides and angles.
  • SSS construction: Given all 3 side lengths — draw one side, then use a compass to draw arcs from each end (e.g., given sides 4 cm, 5 cm, 6 cm).
  • SAS construction: Given 2 sides and the angle between them — draw one side, make the angle with a protractor, mark the other side (e.g., given sides 5 cm, 7 cm with a 60° angle between them).
  • ASA construction: Given 2 angles and the side between them — draw the side, make angles at each end, lines meet at the third vertex (e.g., given angles 50°, 70° with a 6 cm side between them).
  • RHS construction: Given right angle, hypotenuse, and one side — draw the side, make 90°, draw arc of hypotenuse length (e.g., given right angle, hypotenuse 5 cm, one leg 3 cm).
  • Tools needed: ruler, compass, protractor.
  • Always check the triangle inequality (sum of any two sides > third side) before starting a construction (e.g., sides 2, 3, 7 won't work because 2 + 3 = 5 < 7).
  • If you know two angles of a triangle, you can find the third (since they add up to 180°) — e.g., if two angles are 50° and 60°, the third = 180° − 50° − 60° = 70°.

Key Formulas

Triangle inequality: a + b > c (check for all three pairs before constructing)
If two angles are known: third angle = 180° − (angle 1 + angle 2)

Worked Examples

Example 1: SSS Construction (sides 4, 5, 6 cm)

Step 1: Draw a line segment BC = 6 cm (the longest side as base).
Step 2: With B as centre and radius 4 cm, draw an arc above BC.
Step 3: With C as centre and radius 5 cm, draw another arc cutting the first arc. Call the intersection A.
Step 4: Join A to B and A to C. ▵ABC is ready!

Example 2: SAS Construction (sides 5 cm, 4 cm, included angle 60°)

Step 1: Draw AB = 5 cm.
Step 2: At A, use a protractor to draw a ray making 60° with AB.
Step 3: On this ray, mark point C such that AC = 4 cm.
Step 4: Join B to C. ▵ABC is complete!

Example 3: RHS Construction (hypotenuse 5 cm, one side 3 cm)

Step 1: Draw BC = 3 cm.
Step 2: At B, construct a 90° angle using a protractor.
Step 3: With C as centre and radius 5 cm (hypotenuse), draw an arc cutting the perpendicular. Call this point A.
Step 4: Join A to C. ▵ABC is a right triangle!

Practice Problems

Easy

Construct a triangle with sides 3 cm, 4 cm, and 5 cm. Which construction type is this?

Show Answer
SSS construction. Draw 5 cm base, arc of 3 cm from one end, arc of 4 cm from the other. The intersection is the third vertex.
Easy

Can you construct a triangle with sides 2 cm, 3 cm, and 6 cm? Why or why not?

Show Answer
No. 2 + 3 = 5, which is NOT greater than 6. Triangle inequality fails.
Medium

Construct ▵PQR with PQ = 6 cm, ∠P = 50°, and PR = 5 cm. Which construction type?

Show Answer
SAS construction. Two sides (PQ=6, PR=5) and the included angle (∠P=50°) are given.
Medium

Construct ▵ABC with ∠A = 45°, AB = 7 cm, and ∠B = 75°. What is ∠C?

Show Answer
ASA construction. ∠C = 180° − 45° − 75° = 60°
Hard

Construct a right triangle with hypotenuse 10 cm and one leg 6 cm. Find the other leg before constructing.

Show Answer
By Pythagoras: other leg = √(10² − 6²) = √(100 − 36) = √64 = 8 cm. Then use RHS construction.
Easy

What information is needed for an ASA construction?

Show Answer
For an ASA construction, you need two angles and the side between them (the included side).
Easy

Can you construct a triangle with sides 1 cm, 2 cm, and 5 cm? Why or why not?

Show Answer
No. Triangle inequality fails: 1 + 2 = 3, which is NOT greater than 5. The sum of any two sides must be greater than the third side.
Medium

Construct ▵ABC with AB = 5.5 cm, ∠A = 75°, ∠B = 55°. What is ∠C? Describe the steps.

Show Answer
∠C = 180° − 75° − 55° = 50°. This is an ASA construction. Steps: (1) Draw AB = 5.5 cm. (2) At A, use a protractor to draw a ray making 75° with AB. (3) At B, draw a ray making 55° with AB. (4) The two rays meet at point C. Join to complete ▵ABC.
Medium

What construction type would you use if given: hypotenuse = 6 cm, one leg = 4 cm, angle = 90°?

Show Answer
RHS construction. You are given a right angle (90°), the hypotenuse (6 cm), and one side (4 cm). Draw the leg of 4 cm, construct a 90° angle at one end, then draw an arc of radius 6 cm (hypotenuse) from the other end to locate the third vertex.
Hard

A triangle has angles 40°, 60°, and 80° with the side between the 40° and 60° angles being 6 cm. Which construction method should you use? Describe the steps.

Show Answer
ASA construction (two angles and the included side are known). Steps: (1) Draw the base = 6 cm. (2) At the left end, use a protractor to make an angle of 40°. (3) At the right end, make an angle of 60°. (4) Extend both rays until they meet at the third vertex. The angle at the top will automatically be 80° (since 180° − 40° − 60° = 80°).

Common Mistakes

  • Not checking the triangle inequality before starting the construction — the arcs won't meet if it fails!
  • For SAS, using the wrong angle (not the one between the two given sides).
  • Inaccurate compass or protractor measurements — even 1° or 1 mm off can change the result.
  • Forgetting to label all vertices and sides clearly.
