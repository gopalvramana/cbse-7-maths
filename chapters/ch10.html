Tools You Need

Real-world connection: Architects, engineers, and designers construct precise shapes every day. A bridge engineer needs exact triangle measurements for structural strength. The tools you learn here are the same principles used in real construction!
  • Ruler: For drawing straight lines and measuring lengths (in cm).
  • Compass: For drawing arcs and circles. Set the compass to the required length, then draw.
  • Protractor: For measuring and drawing angles (in degrees).
Before you start any construction:
1. Check the triangle inequality (sum of any two sides > third side). If it fails, the triangle is impossible — arcs won’t meet!
2. If two angles are given, check their sum is < 180°. The third angle = 180° − (sum of two).

SSS Construction (Given 3 Sides)

How to construct a triangle when all 3 sides are given

Example: Construct ▵ABC with AB = 6 cm, BC = 5 cm, AC = 4 cm.

Step 1: Draw the longest side as the base. Draw a line segment AB = 6 cm using a ruler.
Step 2: Set your compass to 5 cm (length of BC). Place the compass point on B and draw an arc above the line.
Step 3: Now set the compass to 4 cm (length of AC). Place the compass point on A and draw another arc that intersects the first arc.
Step 4: The point where the two arcs meet is vertex C. Mark it.
Step 5: Join A to C and B to C using straight lines. ▵ABC is complete!

SAS Construction (Given 2 Sides + Included Angle)

How to construct when 2 sides and the angle BETWEEN them are given

Example: Construct ▵PQR with PQ = 5 cm, ∠P = 60°, PR = 4 cm.

Step 1: Draw the first side PQ = 5 cm as the base.
Step 2: At point P, use a protractor to draw a ray making 60° with PQ.
Step 3: On this ray, mark point R such that PR = 4 cm (measure with ruler or compass).
Step 4: Join Q to R. ▵PQR is complete!
Critical: The angle MUST be the one between the two given sides. If the angle is not included, SAS doesn’t apply!

ASA Construction (Given 2 Angles + Included Side)

How to construct when 2 angles and the side BETWEEN them are given

Example: Construct ▵XYZ with ∠X = 50°, XY = 6 cm, ∠Y = 70°.

Step 1: Draw the included side XY = 6 cm.
Step 2: At X, use a protractor to draw a ray making 50° with XY.
Step 3: At Y, draw a ray making 70° with XY (on the same side as the first ray).
Step 4: The two rays will meet at a point. That point is Z.
Step 5: ▵XYZ is complete! (Note: ∠Z = 180° − 50° − 70° = 60°. It forms automatically!)

RHS Construction (Right Angle + Hypotenuse + Side)

How to construct a right triangle when hypotenuse and one leg are given

Example: Construct right ▵ABC with ∠B = 90°, hypotenuse AC = 5 cm, BC = 3 cm.

Step 1: Draw BC = 3 cm (the given leg, not the hypotenuse).
Step 2: At B, construct a 90° angle using a protractor. Draw a ray perpendicular to BC.
Step 3: Set compass to 5 cm (hypotenuse length). Place compass on C and draw an arc that cuts the perpendicular ray.
Step 4: The intersection point is A. Join A to C.
Step 5: ▵ABC is complete! (By Pythagoras, AB = √(25−9) = 4 cm)

NCERT Exercise Solutions — All Questions Solved

Exercise 10.1 — SSS Construction (4 Questions)

Q1. Construct ▵ABC with AB = 4 cm, BC = 5 cm, AC = 6 cm.

Show Solution
Step 1: Check triangle inequality first.
4+5=9 > 6 ✓, 4+6=10 > 5 ✓, 5+6=11 > 4 ✓. All pass — construction is possible!
Step 2: Draw the longest side AC = 6 cm as the base using a ruler.
Step 3: Set compass to 4 cm. Place compass point on A. Draw an arc above the base line.
Step 4: Set compass to 5 cm. Place compass point on C. Draw another arc that intersects the first arc.
Step 5: Mark the intersection point as B.
Step 6: Join A to B and C to B using a ruler. ▵ABC is complete!
Verify: Measure all sides with a ruler: AB should be 4 cm, BC should be 5 cm, AC is 6 cm.

Q2. Construct ▵PQR with PQ = 3 cm, QR = 4 cm, PR = 5 cm.

Show Solution
Step 1: Check inequality. 3+4=7 > 5 ✓, 3+5=8 > 4 ✓, 4+5=9 > 3 ✓. Possible!
Step 2: Draw PR = 5 cm as base (longest side).
Step 3: Compass = 3 cm on P. Draw arc above base.
Step 4: Compass = 4 cm on R. Draw arc cutting the first arc. Intersection = Q.
Step 5: Join PQ and RQ. ▵PQR is complete!
Interesting: Since 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25 = 5², this is a right triangle! ∠Q = 90°. Check with protractor.

Q3. Can you construct a triangle with sides 2 cm, 3 cm, and 6 cm? Why or why not?

Show Solution
Step 1: Check triangle inequality: 2 + 3 = 5. Is 5 > 6?
Step 2: No! 5 is NOT greater than 6.
Step 3: This means if you try to draw arcs of radius 2 and 3 from the ends of a 6 cm line, the arcs will not meet. The two shorter sides together aren’t long enough to “reach across” the longest side.
Answer: Construction is impossible.

Q4. Construct an equilateral triangle with side 5 cm.

Show Solution
Step 1: This is SSS with all three sides = 5 cm.
Step 2: Draw AB = 5 cm.
Step 3: Compass = 5 cm on A. Draw arc above base.
Step 4: Compass = 5 cm on B. Draw arc cutting the first. Mark intersection as C.
Step 5: Join AC and BC. ▵ABC is equilateral!
Verify: All sides = 5 cm. All angles should be 60° (check with protractor).
Exercise 10.2 — SAS Construction (4 Questions)

Q1. Construct ▵DEF with DE = 5 cm, ∠D = 60°, DF = 4 cm.

Show Solution
Step 1: Identify. Two sides (DE=5, DF=4) and the included angle (∠D=60°, which is at vertex D, between sides DE and DF). This is SAS.
Step 2: Draw DE = 5 cm using ruler.
Step 3: Place protractor at D. Mark 60° from the line DE. Draw a ray from D at 60°.
Step 4: On this ray, mark point F at 4 cm from D (use ruler or compass).
Step 5: Join E to F. ▵DEF is complete!
Verify: Measure ∠D with protractor (should be 60°), DE with ruler (5 cm), DF (4 cm).

Q2. Construct ▵ABC with AB = 7 cm, ∠A = 75°, AC = 5 cm.

Show Solution
Step 1: SAS: two sides AB=7, AC=5, included angle ∠A=75°.
Step 2: Draw AB = 7 cm.
Step 3: At A, make 75° angle using protractor. Draw ray.
Step 4: On the ray, mark C at 5 cm from A.
Step 5: Join B to C. Done!

Q3. Construct a right triangle with legs 3 cm and 4 cm.

Show Solution
Step 1: This is SAS with the included angle = 90° (right angle).
Step 2: Draw one leg AB = 3 cm.
Step 3: At A (or B), construct 90° using protractor.
Step 4: On the perpendicular ray, mark the other leg = 4 cm.
Step 5: Join the endpoints. The hypotenuse will automatically be 5 cm (3-4-5 triple!).

Q4. Construct an isosceles triangle with equal sides 5 cm and included angle 40°.

Show Solution
Step 1: SAS: two sides = 5 cm each, included angle = 40°.
Step 2: Draw one side = 5 cm.
Step 3: At one end, make 40° angle.
Step 4: Mark 5 cm on the ray.
Step 5: Join endpoints. Since both sides = 5 cm, it’s isosceles.
Note: Base angles = (180° − 40°)/2 = 70° each.
Exercise 10.3 — ASA Construction (4 Questions)

Q1. Construct ▵XYZ with ∠X = 50°, XY = 6 cm, ∠Y = 70°.

Show Solution
Step 1: ASA: two angles (50°, 70°) and the included side (XY = 6 cm between them).
Step 2: First, find the third angle: ∠Z = 180° − 50° − 70° = 60°.
Step 3: Draw XY = 6 cm.
Step 4: At X, use protractor to make 50° angle. Draw a ray from X.
Step 5: At Y, make 70° angle (on the same side). Draw a ray from Y.
Step 6: The two rays meet at Z. ▵XYZ is complete!
Verify: Measure ∠Z — it should be 60°.

Q2. Construct ▵ABC with ∠A = 75°, AB = 5.5 cm, ∠B = 55°.

Show Solution
Step 1: Find ∠C = 180° − 75° − 55° = 50°.
Step 2: Draw AB = 5.5 cm.
Step 3: At A, make 75°. Draw ray.
Step 4: At B, make 55° (same side). Draw ray.
Step 5: Rays meet at C. Join to complete ▵ABC.

Q3. Construct ▵PQR with ∠P = 45°, PQ = 5 cm, ∠Q = 90°.

Show Solution
Step 1: ∠R = 180° − 45° − 90° = 45°. This will be a right isosceles triangle!
Step 2: Draw PQ = 5 cm.
Step 3: At P, make 45° angle. Draw ray.
Step 4: At Q, make 90° angle (same side). Draw perpendicular ray.
Step 5: Rays meet at R. ▵PQR is a right isosceles triangle.

Q4. Can you construct a triangle with angles 80°, 60°, and 50°? Why or why not?

Show Solution
Step 1: Check if angles add to 180°: 80 + 60 + 50 = 190°.
Step 2: 190° ≠ 180°. The angles don’t satisfy the angle sum property.
Answer: No, this triangle is impossible. The angles of any triangle must add to exactly 180°.
Exercise 10.4 — RHS Construction (3 Questions)

Q1. Construct right ▵ABC with ∠B = 90°, hypotenuse AC = 5 cm, BC = 3 cm.

Show Solution
Step 1: Draw BC = 3 cm (the given leg, NOT the hypotenuse).
Step 2: At B, construct a 90° angle. Draw a perpendicular ray upward from B.
Step 3: Set compass to 5 cm (hypotenuse = AC). Place compass on C.
Step 4: Draw an arc from C that cuts the perpendicular ray from B. This intersection is A.
Step 5: Join A to C. ▵ABC is complete!
Verify: By Pythagoras, AB = √(5² − 3²) = √(25−9) = √16 = 4 cm. Measure AB to check.

Q2. Construct right ▵PQR with ∠Q = 90°, hypotenuse PR = 10 cm, PQ = 6 cm.

Show Solution
Step 1: Draw PQ = 6 cm.
Step 2: At Q, make 90° angle (perpendicular ray).
Step 3: Compass = 10 cm on P. Draw arc cutting the perpendicular. That’s R.
Step 4: Join PR. Done!
Verify: QR = √(10² − 6²) = √(100−36) = √64 = 8 cm.

Q3. Construct right ▵LMN with ∠M = 90°, hypotenuse LN = 13 cm, MN = 5 cm.

Show Solution
Step 1: Draw MN = 5 cm.
Step 2: At M, construct 90°.
Step 3: Compass = 13 cm on N. Arc cuts perpendicular at L.
Step 4: Join LN.
Verify: LM = √(13² − 5²) = √(169−25) = √144 = 12 cm. This is a 5-12-13 Pythagorean triple!

Extra Practice

Easy

1. What construction type is needed if given: 3 sides = 4cm, 5cm, 7cm?

Show Answer
Step 1: Three sides given = SSS construction.
Step 2: Check inequality: 4+5=9>7 ✓, 4+7=11>5 ✓, 5+7=12>4 ✓. Possible!
Easy

2. Can you construct a triangle with sides 1, 2, 5 cm?

Show Answer
Step 1: Check: 1+2=3. Is 3 > 5?
Step 2: No! 3 < 5.
Answer: Impossible. Triangle inequality fails.
Medium

3. Construct ▵ABC with AB=5.5cm, ∠A=75°, ∠B=55°. What is ∠C?

Show Answer
Step 1: This is ASA (2 angles + included side).
Step 2: Find ∠C = 180° − 75° − 55° = 50°.
Step 3: Draw AB=5.5cm, make 75° at A, 55° at B. Rays meet at C.
Medium

4. What type if given: hypotenuse=6cm, one leg=4cm, angle=90°?

Show Answer
Step 1: Right angle + hypotenuse + one side = RHS construction.
Step 2: Draw the leg (4cm), make 90°, arc from other end (radius=hypotenuse=6cm) to find third vertex.
Step 3: Other leg = √(36−16) = √20 ≈ 4.47 cm.
Hard

5. Construct a right triangle with hypotenuse 10cm and one leg 6cm. Find the other leg first.

Show Answer
Step 1: Find other leg using Pythagoras.
Step 2: a² + 6² = 10²
Step 3: a² + 36 = 100
Step 4: a² = 64
Step 5: a = √64 = 8 cm
Step 6: Now use RHS: draw 6cm leg, 90° at corner, arc of 10cm for hypotenuse.
Hard

6. Angles are 40°, 60°, 80° with side between 40° and 60° = 6cm. Which method? Describe steps.

Show Answer
Step 1: Two angles and included side = ASA construction.
Step 2: Draw base = 6 cm.
Step 3: At left end, make 40° with protractor. Draw ray.
Step 4: At right end, make 60°. Draw ray.
Step 5: Rays meet at third vertex.
Step 6: The angle at top = 80° automatically (40+60+80=180).

HOTS Advanced

HardHOTS

1. You need to construct a triangle but only have a ruler (no compass, no protractor). Can you construct an equilateral triangle? How?

Show Answer
Not exactly. Without a compass, you can’t draw precise arcs. However, you can draw a straight line, measure equal lengths, and estimate 60° angles. For a perfect construction, you need at least a ruler and compass. With a compass, you can construct 60° without a protractor (using equal arcs)!
HardHOTS

2. If someone gives you 3 angles (say 60°, 70°, 50°) but no side length, can you construct a unique triangle?

Show Answer
No! Without at least one side measurement, you can draw infinitely many triangles with those angles — all the same shape but different sizes. These are called similar triangles. You need at least one side to fix the size and get a unique triangle.
HardHOTS

3. How many different triangles can you construct with sides 3, 4, 5? What about with angles 60°, 60°, 60°?

Show Answer
Sides 3, 4, 5: Exactly ONE unique triangle (up to position/orientation). SSS always gives a unique triangle.

Angles 60°, 60°, 60°: Infinitely many! Any equilateral triangle (side 1cm, 5cm, 100cm...) has all angles 60°. AAA doesn’t give a unique triangle — you need at least one side.

Common Mistakes

  • Not checking triangle inequality before starting
    Start drawing sides 2, 3, 6... arcs don’t meet!
    Always check first: 2+3=5 < 6. Impossible! Save time by checking before drawing.
  • Using wrong angle for SAS (not the included one)
    Given AB=5, BC=7, ∠C=60°. Use SAS with ∠C.
    ∠C is NOT between AB and BC. The included angle is ∠B. This is NOT a valid SAS setup!
  • Drawing arcs too small or on wrong side
    Arcs drawn below the base line
    Draw arcs on the same side (usually above). Make arcs large enough that they clearly intersect.
  • Inaccurate measurements
    Measuring “approximately 5 cm”
    Be precise! Even 1mm or 1° off changes the triangle. Use sharp pencil, align ruler carefully.
  • Forgetting to label vertices and sides
    Triangle drawn but no labels
    Always label vertices (A, B, C) and write side lengths. Makes checking easier and gets full marks in exams!
