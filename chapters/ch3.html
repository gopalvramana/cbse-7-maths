Data Handling

Summary

  • Data is information collected for a purpose — like marks, heights, or favourite colours.
  • Mean (average) = add up all values and divide by how many there are.
  • Median = the middle value when you line up all values from smallest to largest.
  • Mode = the value that appears the most number of times.
  • Range = largest value minus smallest value.
  • Bar graphs use rectangular bars to show data — taller bar means bigger value.
  • Probability tells you how likely something is to happen, always between 0 and 1.
  • If an event is impossible, probability = 0. If it's certain, probability = 1.

Key Formulas

Mean = Sum of all observations ÷ Number of observations
Range = Maximum value − Minimum value
Probability = Number of favourable outcomes ÷ Total number of outcomes

Worked Examples

Example 1: Finding the Mean

Find the mean of: 4, 8, 6, 10, 12

Step 1: Add all values: 4 + 8 + 6 + 10 + 12 = 40
Step 2: Count the values: there are 5 numbers
Step 3: Mean = 40 ÷ 5 = 8

Example 2: Finding the Median

Find the median of: 3, 7, 2, 9, 5

Step 1: Arrange in order: 2, 3, 5, 7, 9
Step 2: There are 5 values (odd), so the middle one is the 3rd value
Step 3: Median = 5

Example 3: Finding Probability

A bag has 3 red balls, 2 blue balls, and 5 green balls. What is the probability of picking a blue ball?

Step 1: Favourable outcomes (blue balls) = 2
Step 2: Total outcomes = 3 + 2 + 5 = 10
Step 3: Probability = 2/10 = 1/5

Practice Problems

Easy

Find the mean of: 10, 20, 30, 40, 50

Show Answer
Sum = 10+20+30+40+50 = 150. Mean = 150 ÷ 5 = 30
Easy

Find the mode of: 5, 3, 7, 3, 8, 3, 9

Show Answer
3 appears 3 times (most frequent). Mode = 3
Medium

The marks of 6 students are: 45, 60, 55, 70, 50, 80. Find the mean and range.

Show Answer
Sum = 45+60+55+70+50+80 = 360. Mean = 360 ÷ 6 = 60. Range = 80 − 45 = 35
Medium

Find the median of: 12, 7, 3, 15, 9, 21

Show Answer
Arrange: 3, 7, 9, 12, 15, 21. Even count (6), so median = average of 3rd and 4th values = (9+12)/2 = 10.5
Hard

A die is rolled once. What is the probability of getting: (a) an even number, (b) a number greater than 4?

Show Answer
(a) Even numbers: 2, 4, 6 → 3 out of 6 = 1/2
(b) Numbers > 4: 5, 6 → 2 out of 6 = 1/3
Hard

The mean of 5 numbers is 12. Four of the numbers are 10, 14, 8, and 15. Find the fifth number.

Show Answer
Sum of 5 numbers = 12 × 5 = 60. Sum of 4 known = 10+14+8+15 = 47. Fifth number = 60 − 47 = 13
Easy

Find the mode of: 2, 5, 2, 8, 2, 9.

Show Answer
2 appears 3 times (most frequent). Mode = 2
Easy

What is the range of: 15, 22, 8, 31, 19?

Show Answer
Range = Maximum − Minimum = 31 − 8 = 23
Medium

The mean of 4 numbers is 20. Three of them are 15, 25, and 18. Find the fourth number.

Show Answer
Sum of 4 numbers = 20 × 4 = 80. Sum of 3 known = 15+25+18 = 58. Fourth number = 80 − 58 = 22
Medium

A spinner has 4 equal sections: red, blue, green, yellow. What is the probability of landing on red or blue?

Show Answer
Favourable outcomes = 2 (red or blue). Total outcomes = 4. Probability = 2/4 = 1/2
Hard

The marks of 7 students are: 45, 67, 52, 78, 52, 89, 52. Find the mean, median, and mode.

Show Answer
Sum = 45+67+52+78+52+89+52 = 435. Mean = 435 ÷ 7 = 62.14 (approx).
Arrange in order: 45, 52, 52, 52, 67, 78, 89. Median (4th value) = 52.
Mode (most frequent) = 52 (appears 3 times).
Hard

Two coins are tossed together. What is the probability of getting at least one head?

Show Answer
Total outcomes = {HH, HT, TH, TT} = 4. Outcomes with at least one head = {HH, HT, TH} = 3. Probability = 3/4

Common Mistakes

  • Forgetting to arrange data in order before finding the median.
  • Confusing mean (average) with median (middle value) — they're different!
  • Writing a probability greater than 1 — probability is always between 0 and 1.
  • Forgetting to count ALL outcomes when calculating probability.
